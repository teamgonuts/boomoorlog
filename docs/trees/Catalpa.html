Catalpa — Catalpa (Catalpa)

The flamboyant showboat of the boulevard — enormous heart-shaped leaves, orchid-like flowers, absurdly long seed pods dangling like ammunition belts, and poisonous roots that dare anything to dig too close.

Combat flavor

Light wood delivers rapid strikes, but don't underestimate it — its toxic roots could trigger a "ground poison" area-denial ability, and the sheer leaf canopy size (natural umbrella) makes it a natural protector/buffer Support unit for adjacent allies.

Real-world facts

Typical / max height12–18 m typical; up to 27 m (Northern Catalpa, C. speciosa)
Lifespan50–70 years; relatively short-lived, trunks prone to internal rot on older specimens
WoodAir-dry density ~460 kg/m³; Janka hardness ~2,450 N (550 lbf) — light, soft, and low-density; very rot-resistant despite softness (used historically for fence posts and railroad ties)
Growth speedFast (rapid early growth, slows with age)
Ecology / notable traitsNative to eastern North America and East Asia; the catalpaworm (Ceratomia catalpae) larvae feed exclusively on its leaves — the tree is sometimes deliberately grown as fish-bait "worm farms"; roots are highly poisonous; aerial parts (leaves, flowers, pods) are non-toxic to humans and pets; large flowers attract bumblebees; somewhat invasive outside native range; culturally prominent street tree worldwide; produces extremely long (20–50 cm) cigar-like seed pods
