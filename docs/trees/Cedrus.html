Cedrus — Cedar (Cedrus)

An ancient mountain warlord who stood on Himalayan ridges long before Amsterdam existed — resin-armoured, slow to grow, impossible to rot, and absolutely certain he has seen it all before.

Combat flavor

Cedrus hits slow but each blow carries the weight of centuries — dense resinous wood and extreme durability translate to a high-armour, high-damage bruiser who barely notices small foes. Its age-gated power (slow early growth, then near invincibility) could manifest as a passive that scales up the longer Cedrus survives a wave.

Real-world facts

Typical / max height30–40 m typical; up to 65 m recorded (Cedrus deodara up to 50 m)
Lifespan800–1,000+ years; documented ring-counted specimens to 619 yr; widely reported over 1,000 yr
Wood~560 kg/m³ air-dry (Cedrus deodara); dense, resinous, naturally durable softwood
Growth speedMedium (fast early — rapid until ~45–50 yr, then extremely slow)
Ecology / notable traitsNative to western Himalayas and Mediterranean mountains (1,300–3,000 m altitude); strongly resinous wood resists decay and insects; flat-topped layered crown develops only with great age; culturally sacred in Lebanon (national symbol); narrow wild range — four species only; threatened in native habitat by climate change and overgrazing
