Carpinus — Hornbeam (Haagbeuk)

The toughest native hardwood in Europe — slow-growing, iron-dense, and utterly implacable; Roman chariot-builders chose hornbeam for a reason, and it hasn't forgotten.

Combat flavor

Carpinus is the Juggernaut in every sense — slow to advance but nearly impossible to stop once moving, absorbing attacks with wood so dense it was used for mill gears. Signature ability: an ironclad passive that reduces incoming damage and reflects a portion back on melee strikers.

Real-world facts

Typical / max height15–25 m (Carpinus betulus); up to ~30 m
Lifespan300+ years; coppiced specimens can persist far longer
Wood~830 kg/m³ air-dry (range 530–830 kg/m³ depending on site); Janka values for close relatives (hop hornbeam) 4,810–6,903 N; widely cited as the hardest wood of any European native tree
Growth speedslow
Ecology / notable traitsHardest timber in Europe; historically used for chariot wheels, mill cogs, butchers' blocks, and tool handles; excellent coppice species — regenerates vigorously after cutting; tolerates shade and is a common woodland understory dominant; important for cavity-nesting birds; name "horn-beam" literally means "hard tree" (Old English)
