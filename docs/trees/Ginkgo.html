Ginkgo — Ginkgo (Japanse notenboom)

A 270-million-year-old living fossil that survived the Cretaceous extinction and the Hiroshima atomic blast — ancient, imperturbable, and carrying chemical weapons in its smelly seeds that have been deterring predators since before the dinosaurs.

Combat flavor

The paradox of Ginkgo: legendary age and indestructibility, but soft wood means slow, weak direct strikes — its power lies in chemical warfare (toxic seed stench, ginkgolide anticoagulants) and near-unkillable HP. Signature ability candidate: Ancient Immunity — resists all status debuffs; passive Butyric Barrage poisons nearby enemies with fetid seed pulp.

Real-world facts

Typical / max height20–35 m typical; max ~40 m
Lifespan1000–3500 years recorded; theoretically unlimited vascular cambium renewal — exceptionally long-lived, flag as living fossil
Wood~465 kg/m³ air-dry density — soft, light, weak wood (classed as softwood); little structural strength; Janka not widely reported, very low
Growth speedFast in first 30 years (>30 cm/year); medium thereafter
Ecology / notable traitsSole surviving species of division Ginkgophyta, unchanged for 200+ million years (living fossil); dioecious — female trees produce butyric-acid-reeking seeds used in Chinese medicine; highly disease- and pest-resistant (survived Hiroshima, 1945); ginkgolides in leaves are natural insect repellents and anticoagulants used in medicine; allelopathic seed pulp; urban pollution-tolerant; wild population is endangered (Zhejiang, China) though cultivated globally — wild status: rare, cultivated: common
World rarity noteWild Ginkgo is critically rare; cultivated globally — recorded as "notable" overall
