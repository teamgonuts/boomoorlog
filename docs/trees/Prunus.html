Prunus — Cherry (Kers)

Amsterdam's most beloved soldier — she shows up in absurd numbers every spring, detonates the entire city in pink, and quietly poisons anyone who gnaws on her bark.

Combat flavor

The most numerous unit on the board and versatile — medium-hard wood gives solid balanced attack speed, while cyanide-laced bark is a natural poison aura that damages melee attackers. Ornamental cherry's short lifespan trades longevity for sheer swarm numbers.

Real-world facts

Typical / max height5–15 m ornamental cherries; wild black cherry (P. serotina) up to 33 m
Lifespan15–50 years ornamental; P. serotina up to 250 years
Wood~560 kg/m³ air-dry density; Janka ~950–995 lbf — medium-hard, prized fine-furniture timber
Growth speedfast (P. serotina is a fast-growing forest tree; ornamentals moderate–fast)
Ecology / notable traitsCyanogenic glycosides in bark, leaves, and seeds — toxic to mammals and insects; P. serotina is invasive in European forests (designated noxious weed in Netherlands); extremely high wildlife value for Lepidoptera (200+ species), birds, and pollinators; allelopathic leaf litter; culturally iconic (Japanese hanami); largest genus in Amsterdam by far
