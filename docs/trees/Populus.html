Populus — Poplar (Populier)

The Poplar is a reckless, sky-hungry brawler who shoots up faster than anyone else on the battlefield and hits with a flurry of light, rapid blows — never pausing, never patient, never meant to last.

Combat flavor

The Poplar's feather-light wood means it strikes fast and cheap — high attack speed, low hit per blow. Its phytoremediation trait could become a passive "contamination cleanse" aura on occupied ground.

Real-world facts

Typical / max height25–35 m typical; up to 45 m in some species (black cottonwood can reach ~45 m)
Lifespan30–150 years depending on species; cultivated poplars often only 30–50 years; native balsam poplars up to 150–200 years; some wild specimens reportedly to 400 years
WoodAir-dry density ~385–450 kg/m³; Janka hardness ~2,400 N (540 lbf). One of the lightest commercial hardwoods — soft, easy to split, rapid to ignite.
Growth speedFast (1.5–3 m/year in good conditions)
Ecology / notable traitsProlific wind-borne cotton seed dispersal; used for phytoremediation (absorbs heavy metals from soil); allelopathic root exudates suppress some competitors; highly invasive in riparian zones; monoecious; short-rotation coppice crop; cultural symbol of speed and transience in European tradition
