Acer — Maple (Esdoorn)

The workhorse of the Amsterdam canopy — thick-limbed, dependable, and hits hard enough that you'll feel it for days, though never in a hurry.

Combat flavor

Acer's dense hardwood translates to powerful but measured strikes — a Bruiser that charges in and stays in the fight. Its samaras hint at a passive area-scatter ability; its thick canopy could manifest as a damage-reduction shield.

Real-world facts

Typical / max height15–30 m typical; up to 45 m (Bigleaf maple) depending on species
Lifespan80–200 years; some individuals (e.g. Bigleaf maple) reach 300 years
WoodAir-dry density ~620–720 kg/m³ (hard maple); Janka hardness ~6,450 N (1,450 lbf) for Sugar Maple; soft maples ~700–950 lbf. Dense and tough — classic heavy-blow wood.
Growth speedMedium (height gains 30–60 cm/yr for most species)
Ecology / notable traitsExcellent fall-colour display; winged samaras for wide seed dispersal; some species have mild allelopathic leaf litter; Sugar Maple is tapped for syrup production; very wide genus (~130 species) across Northern Hemisphere — culturally iconic (Canadian flag).
