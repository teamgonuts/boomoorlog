Ostrya — Hop hornbeam (Ostrya)

The understory bruiser who never gets credit — tougher wood than oak, shrugging off the shade of bigger trees for a century, with papery hop-like seed clusters that rattle like armour as it waits for its moment.

Combat flavor

Dense wood means slow, powerful strikes that ignore light armour. The hop-like seed pods could inspire a "scatter shot" special — flinging clusters of hard nutlets to slow or distract enemy waves.

Real-world facts

Typical / max height10–15 m; up to 20 m in favourable sites
Lifespan100–150 years
WoodAir-dry density ~890 kg/m³ — one of the densest European hardwoods; Janka side hardness ~5,630 N (1,260 lbf); categorised as very hard and heavy, comparable to hornbeam (Carpinus)
Growth speedMedium (slow-to-medium; shade-tolerant understory species)
Ecology / notable traitsNative to southern Europe and southwest Asia; characteristic of warm, dry limestone slopes; highly drought-tolerant once established; no thorns or significant toxicity; seed clusters resemble hops (hence common name); very dense wood used historically for mallets, tool handles and cogs; not invasive; globally uncommon but not threatened
