Thuja — Arborvitae (Thuja)

An ancient, resin-soaked sentinel who has been standing guard for centuries and hits with slow, ponderous precision — its rot-proof heartwood makes it nearly impossible to wear down over time.

Combat flavor

Low wood density means Thuja is no powerhouse on individual strikes, but its legendary rot-resistance and extreme longevity give it exceptional staying power — the Bruiser that simply refuses to die. Thujone toxicity is a natural seed for a passive poison-aura ability.

Real-world facts

Typical / max height10–20 m typical (Thuja occidentalis); Thuja plicata can exceed 60 m
Lifespan150–400+ years (Thuja occidentalis confirmed to 400 yr; some old-growth specimens estimated older)
Wood~310–356 kg/m³ air-dry (Thuja occidentalis; specific gravity ~0.31); very light and soft, but exceptionally decay-resistant due to natural thujaplicin preservatives
Growth speedMedium (Thuja occidentalis 30–60 cm/yr; cultivated Green Giant 90–150 cm/yr)
Ecology / notable traitsEvergreen conifer; foliage contains thujone (mildly toxic if ingested); historically sacred — "Arborvitae" means "Tree of Life" in Latin, named by French explorers after Indigenous use as scurvy cure; dense evergreen canopy provides year-round cover; slow-decaying fallen logs are keystone habitat features; deer browse the foliage heavily in winter
