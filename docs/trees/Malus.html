Malus — Apple (Appel)

The orchard patriarch — tough, productive, and stubbornly moderate, with wood harder than walnut and a cultural footprint spanning every temperate civilization on Earth.

Combat flavor

Malus fights with measured, heavy precision — its dense, hickory-comparable wood drives slow powerful attacks — while its mythological weight (forbidden fruit, golden apples) hints at a "Temptation" debuff ability that lures enemy units out of formation.

Real-world facts

Typical / max height5–10 m cultivated; standard trees up to 12 m; wild M. sylvestris up to 15 m
Lifespan25–100 years for cultivated varieties; espaliered specimens productive for 150+ yr; wild trees up to 100 yr
Woodspecific gravity 0.73–0.83 (approx. 780 kg/m³ air-dry); Janka ~7,700 N (1,730 lbf) — harder than cherry and walnut, comparable to hickory
Growth speedmedium
Ecology / notable traitsMalus sylvestris (wild crab apple) is a light-demanding pioneer species and notably rare in native European range; ancestor of all domestic apples is Malus sieversii from Central Asian mountains; supports diverse insects, birds, and mammals; no allelopathy; some crab-apple species have mild seed toxicity (amygdalin/cyanogenic glycosides in seeds — classic lore); deeply embedded in human mythology (Norse, Greek, Arthurian)
