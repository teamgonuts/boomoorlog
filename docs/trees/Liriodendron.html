Liriodendron — Tulip tree (Liriodendron)

The tallest hardwood in North America rockets skyward like a living siege tower — column-straight, absurdly fast, and covered in flowers that look like it's already celebrating victory.

Combat flavor

Light wood means fast attack speed despite its massive size — a Bruiser that trades armor for reach and speed, and its living-fossil status could unlock a "ancient lineage" legendary-tier ability.

Real-world facts

Typical / max height20–30 m in urban settings; 50–60 m in native Appalachian cove forests; exceptional specimens approach 58 m
Lifespan250–300 years typical; maximum around 450–500 years
Wood~455 kg/m³ (air-dry); Janka ~540 lbf; classified as a hardwood but one of the lightest and softest — workable, straight-grained; commercially sold as "yellow poplar"
Growth speedFast (60–90 cm/year, up to 1.2 m in ideal conditions)
Ecology / notable traitsLiving fossil — genus dates to the Cretaceous; only two living species (L. tulipifera in North America, L. chinense in China), a classic Gondwana disjunct distribution; large, nectar-rich tulip-shaped flowers are a major pollinator resource; straight trunk with no lower branching gives extraordinary height-to-base ratio; intolerant of waterlogged soil; fast growth without the typical brittle-wood penalty; state tree of three US states
