Betula — Birch (Berk)

Scrappy and light on its feet for a Bruiser — birch hits fast, colonises ground no one else wants, and burns so bright in autumn you almost miss that it died young.

Combat flavor

Lighter wood than Acer means quicker strikes at lower impact — a fast-swinging Bruiser who trades longevity for aggression. Its pioneer ecology could manifest as an ability to spawn on contested ground; its flammable bark is a natural liability (fire-damage multiplier).

Real-world facts

Typical / max height20 m typical; up to 40 m (Paper birch, exceptional specimens)
Lifespan30–100 years (short-lived; 30 years in warm zones, 100+ in cold climates)
WoodAir-dry density ~510–600 kg/m³ (B. pendula ~512 kg/m³, B. pubescens ~478 kg/m³); Janka ~4,400 N (990 lbf, Yellow birch) — medium-hard, relatively light for a hardwood
Growth speedMedium to fast (pioneer species, rapid early growth)
Ecology / notable traitsClassic pioneer species — colonises bare ground, burns easily (bark used as natural firestarter since prehistory), fixes disturbed soils; host to >300 insect species in UK; allelopathic leaf litter mildly inhibits competitors; bark has natural antiseptic (betulin); culturally sacred in Norse and Celtic traditions (symbol of new beginnings).
