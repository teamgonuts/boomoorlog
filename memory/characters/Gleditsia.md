# Gleditsia — Honey locust (Valse christusdoorn)

**Archetype (draft):** Skirmisher
**Amsterdam trees:** 3936

## Personality
A fast-growing street brawler armed with branching thorns up to 8 cm long, rot-resistant shock-proof wood, and the audacity to thrive on compacted urban rubble — it fights dirty and it fights first.

## Real-world facts
- **Typical / max height:** 20–30 m typical; up to ~45 m
- **Lifespan:** up to 125 years (moderate for a large tree)
- **Wood:** 755 kg/m³ air-dry; Janka 1,580 lbf (7,030 N) — dense, shock-resistant, rot-resistant; used for fence posts and railroad ties
- **Growth speed:** fast
- **Ecology / notable traits:** Trunk and branches armed with stout branched thorns (modified stems, up to 8 cm, occasionally leaf-bearing); native to moist North American river valleys; now introduced worldwide and invasive in parts of Europe, Australia, and South America; exceptional tolerance of urban stressors — compaction, drought, flooding, salt, heat; pods contain sweet edible pulp (food for megafauna historically); thornless cultivars dominate urban planting but wild form is formidably spiny

## Combat flavor
Gleditsia is the classic skirmisher: fast growth means early deployment, dense wood delivers hard hits, and its branching thorns can deal damage to anything that tries to push past it. Signature ability: a thorn-wall counter-attack that damages melee units on contact.

## Living creatures
Non-native North American legume — far fewer NW European insect associates than a native broadleaf of similar size, but its pollen-rich flowers and seed pods do attract a specific (and growing) crew.

- **Honeybees & bumblebees (Apis, Bombus spp.)** — dozens of foragers when in bloom; flowers are nectar-rich and bee-pollinated. [Illinois Wildflowers pollinators of honey locust](https://www.illinoiswildflowers.info/trees/plants/honey_locust.html)
- **Halictid sweat bees (Lasioglossum, Halictus spp.)** — common minor pollinators, dozens across flowering. [Illinois Wildflowers pollinators of honey locust](https://www.illinoiswildflowers.info/trees/plants/honey_locust.html)
- **Asian honey-locust seed beetle (Megabruchidius dorsalis, Bruchinae)** — up to 94% of pods infested where established; multiple larvae per pod, can mean thousands per mature tree. [Megabruchidius dorsalis in Slovenia (Natura Sloveniae)](https://journals.uni-lj.si/NaturaSloveniae/article/view/16068)
- **Honey locust plant bug (Blepharidopterus chlorionis, Miridae)** — hundreds on a fully-foliated tree in outbreak years. [Illinois Wildflowers honey locust herbivores](https://www.illinoiswildflowers.info/trees/plants/honey_locust.html)
- **Honey locust leafhopper (Macropsis fumipennis)** — abundant on leaflets, easily hundreds per tree. [Illinois Wildflowers honey locust herbivores](https://www.illinoiswildflowers.info/trees/plants/honey_locust.html)
- **Armored scale (Chionaspis gleditsiae and others)** — colonies in the hundreds-to-thousands on bark of stressed urban trees. [Illinois Wildflowers honey locust herbivores](https://www.illinoiswildflowers.info/trees/plants/honey_locust.html)
- **Spider mites (Tetranychidae)** — boom in hot dry urban summers, easily 10,000+ on a stressed tree. [RHS thornless honey locust profile](https://www.rhs.org.uk/plants/80437/gleditsia-triacanthos-f-inermis/details)
- **Honey locust borer (Agrilus difficilis, Buprestidae)** — rare in Europe; a handful of larvae in declining trees. [Illinois Wildflowers honey locust herbivores](https://www.illinoiswildflowers.info/trees/plants/honey_locust.html)
- **Generalist tit pair (Cyanistes caeruleus / Parus major)** — typically 1 nesting pair on a mature street tree; gleans plant bugs and leafhoppers. [iNaturalist Gleditsia triacanthos observations](https://www.inaturalist.org/taxa/54797-Gleditsia-triacanthos)
- **Crow / magpie roost (Corvus, Pica)** — 1–3 birds may use the thorny crown as a safe overnight perch in cities. [iNaturalist Gleditsia triacanthos observations](https://www.inaturalist.org/taxa/54797-Gleditsia-triacanthos)
