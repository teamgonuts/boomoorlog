# Prunus — Cherry (Kers)

**Archetype (draft):** Support
**Amsterdam trees:** 11853

## Personality
Amsterdam's most beloved soldier — she shows up in absurd numbers every spring, detonates the entire city in pink, and quietly poisons anyone who gnaws on her bark.

## Real-world facts
- **Typical / max height:** 5–15 m ornamental cherries; wild black cherry (P. serotina) up to 33 m
- **Lifespan:** 15–50 years ornamental; P. serotina up to 250 years
- **Wood:** ~560 kg/m³ air-dry density; Janka ~950–995 lbf — medium-hard, prized fine-furniture timber
- **Growth speed:** fast (P. serotina is a fast-growing forest tree; ornamentals moderate–fast)
- **Ecology / notable traits:** Cyanogenic glycosides in bark, leaves, and seeds — toxic to mammals and insects; P. serotina is invasive in European forests (designated noxious weed in Netherlands); extremely high wildlife value for Lepidoptera (200+ species), birds, and pollinators; allelopathic leaf litter; culturally iconic (Japanese hanami); largest genus in Amsterdam by far

## Combat flavor
The most numerous unit on the board and versatile — medium-hard wood gives solid balanced attack speed, while cyanide-laced bark is a natural poison aura that damages melee attackers. Ornamental cherry's short lifespan trades longevity for sheer swarm numbers.

## Living creatures
Prunus is one of the most wildlife-loaded genera planted in NW Europe — wild cherry alone supports 200+ Lepidoptera species, and the spring bloom is a critical early-season nectar shock for pollinators.

- **Aphids (cherry blackfly, Myzus cerasi)** — 10,000s per tree at peak; dense black colonies curl young leaves every May–June. - **Mason bees, bumblebees & honeybees (Osmia, Bombus, Apis)** — 100s foraging per tree during the ~2-week bloom; ornamental cherries are a keystone urban pollinator resource. [RHS: cherries for wildlife](https://www.rhs.org.uk/plants/types/trees/for-wildlife) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/osmia.jpg)
- **Bird-cherry ermine moth (Yponomeuta evonymella / Y. padella, Yponomeutidae)** — 100s–1000s of caterpillars in silk-webbed colonies that can shroud an entire small tree. [Butterfly Conservation: bird-cherry ermine](https://butterfly-conservation.org/moths/bird-cherry-ermine) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/yponomeuta-evonymella.jpg)
- **Winter moth (Operophtera brumata, Geometridae)** — 100s of looper caterpillars per tree in spring; the staple food that times blue tit breeding. [RHS: winter moth](https://www.rhs.org.uk/biodiversity/winter-moth-caterpillars) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/operophtera-brumata.jpg)
- **Brimstone moth, buff-tip & short-cloaked (Opisthograptis luteolata, Phalera bucephala, Nola cucullatella)** — tens of caterpillars across the season. [Woodland Trust: wild cherry](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/wild-cherry/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/opisthograptis-luteolata.jpg)
- **Cherry bark tortrix & cherry fruit moth (Enarmonia formosana, Argyresthia pruniella)** — 10s of larvae inside bark and fruit. [Woodland Trust: wild cherry](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/wild-cherry/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/enarmonia-formosana.jpg)
- **Blackbird & song thrush (Turdus merula / T. philomelos)** — 1–3 nesting pairs across a row of mature cherries; gorge on the fruit drop in midsummer. [Woodland Trust: wild cherry](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/wild-cherry/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/turdus-merula.jpg)
- **Wood mouse / yellow-necked mouse (Apodemus sylvaticus / A. flavicollis)** — 1–5 individuals working a single fruiting tree at night, caching stones. [Woodland Trust: wild cherry](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/wild-cherry/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/apodemus-sylvaticus.jpg)
- **Hazel dormouse (Muscardinus avellanarius)** — 1 pair where Prunus is part of mixed scrub; eats flowers in spring, fruit in autumn (rare in Amsterdam itself). [Woodland Trust: wild cherry](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/wild-cherry/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/muscardinus-avellanarius.jpg)
- **Badger (Meles meles)** — 1 sett visits ground beneath a fruiting Prunus serotina to hoover fallen cherries. [Woodland Trust: wild cherry](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/wild-cherry/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/meles-meles.jpg)
