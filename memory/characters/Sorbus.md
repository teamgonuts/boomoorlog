# Sorbus — Rowan (Sorbus)

**Archetype (draft):** Support
**Amsterdam trees:** 3968

## Personality
The wiry mountain sentinel who survives where nothing else will — ancient folklore calls it a witch-ward, and its fiery berries feed half the bird population through winter.

## Real-world facts
- **Typical / max height:** 5–15 m typical; up to 25 m in lowland conditions
- **Lifespan:** 120–200 years
- **Wood:** ~737 kg/m³ air-dry (S. aucuparia); Janka ~7,520 N (estimate) — described as hard, tough, and heavy, comparable to European hop-hornbeam
- **Growth speed:** medium
- **Ecology / notable traits:** Thrives at high altitudes where few trees survive; berries are a vital winter food source for thrushes, waxwings, and dozens of bird species; flowers provide nectar for insects; deeply embedded in Celtic folklore as a protective tree against evil spirits; not invasive; no allelopathy or toxicity to mammals (berries mildly toxic raw due to parasorbic acid, neutralised by cooking or frost)

## Combat flavor
Sorbus is a resilient support fighter — its high-altitude toughness translates to strong defensive stats, while its berry-laden boughs can trigger a "Winter Feast" aura that sustains allied units. The folklore ward-against-evil could manifest as a magic-resistance buff.

## Living creatures
Rowan is a Rosaceae generalist host — moderately rich invertebrate fauna (~28 phytophagous insects in Britain per Kennedy & Southwood) but disproportionately important to over-wintering thrushes and finches because of its lipid-rich autumn berry crop.

- **Aphids (Aphidoidea — Dysaphis sorbi, Eriosoma spp.)** — leaf-curl aphid colonies of hundreds to low thousands per shoot in early summer. [RHS rowan aphids](https://www.rhs.org.uk/plants/sorbus/growing-guide)
- **Apple fruit moth (Argyresthia conjugella)** — primary specialist of Sorbus berries; in mast-poor years moves to apple. Hundreds of larvae can occupy a single fruiting tree's berry crop. [Woodland Trust rowan](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/rowan/)
- **Larger Welsh wave & autumn green carpet moths (Venusia cambrica / Chloroclysta miata)** — dozens of caterpillars per tree on rowan foliage in summer. [Woodland Trust rowan](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/rowan/)
- **Solitary bees & hoverflies (Andrena, Eristalis spp.)** — hundreds of visits/day at peak May flowering; rowan is a useful early-summer nectar bridge. [Species of Britain rowan](https://speciesofbritain.wordpress.com/2021/11/17/321-sorbus-aucuparia-rowan/)
- **Mistle Thrush (Turdus viscivorus)** — single birds aggressively defend a berry-laden rowan as a winter "larder," eating thousands of berries per tree. [Woodland Trust rowan](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/rowan/)
- **Blackbird & Song Thrush (Turdus merula / T. philomelos)** — several individuals strip a mature tree over days; key autumn calorie source. [Woodland Trust rowan](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/rowan/)
- **Fieldfare & Redwing (Turdus pilaris / T. iliacus)** — flocks of 20–100 Scandinavian migrants descend on a single fruiting tree in October–November. [DAERA rowan & wildlife](https://www.daera-ni.gov.uk/news/trees-are-delightful-eye-are-also-useful-humans-and-haven-wildlife)
- **Bohemian Waxwing (Bombycilla garrulus)** — invasion-year flocks of 30–200 birds can strip a town rowan in an afternoon. [Northern Bullfinch & rowanberry study](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/237440228_Northern_Bullfinch_Pyrrhula_p_pyrrhula_irruptive_behaviour_linked_to_rowanberry_Sorbus_aucuparia_abundance)
- **Bullfinch (Pyrrhula pyrrhula)** — 1 to a few resident pairs per fruiting tree; Northern Bullfinch irruptions south are directly driven by rowan crop failure. [Bullfinch–rowan irruption study](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/237440228_Northern_Bullfinch_Pyrrhula_p_pyrrhula_irruptive_behaviour_linked_to_rowanberry_Sorbus_aucuparia_abundance)
- **Pine Marten / Red Fox / Badger (Martes martes / Vulpes vulpes / Meles meles)** — single mammals routinely fed on fallen berries beneath rowans; rowan seed turns up regularly in marten scat surveys. [UCC Sorbus aucuparia](https://www.ucc.ie/en/tree-explorers/trees/a-z/sorbusaucuparia/)
