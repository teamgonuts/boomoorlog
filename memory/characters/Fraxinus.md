# Fraxinus — Ash (Es)

**Archetype (draft):** Bruiser
**Amsterdam trees:** 19757

## Personality
A hard-swinging workhorse forged into sports bats and tool handles, now swinging back — tough, shock-resistant, and furious about ash dieback tearing through the ranks.

## Real-world facts
- **Typical / max height:** 18–25 m typical; max ~43 m (Fraxinus excelsior)
- **Lifespan:** 150–250 years typical; rarely beyond 250
- **Wood:** 660 kg/m³ air-dry density; Janka ~1480 lbf — hard, dense, highly shock-resistant
- **Growth speed:** Fast
- **Ecology / notable traits:** Pioneer on fertile neutral soils; poor decay resistance (must fight in the open); beloved for tool handles, oars, and baseball bats due to shock absorption; catastrophically threatened by ash dieback (Hymenoscyphus fraxineus fungus, ~70% European mortality) — a warrior species fighting extinction

## Combat flavor
Dense, shock-absorbing wood means powerful, punishing blows; its fast growth feeds a large troop count, but the looming ash-dieback threat could translate to a "plague vulnerability" debuff. Signature ability candidate: **Resilient Swing** — absorbs incoming damage briefly before counter-striking harder.

- **Aphids on ash (Prociphilus spp., Aphis fabae)** — ~1000s per tree on leaf-nest aphids forming distinctive curled-leaf colonies. - **Ash bark beetle (Hylesinus / Leperisinus fraxini)** — ~100s–1000s of galleries per dead/stressed tree; the dominant ash subcortical beetle in Europe. [Hylesinus fraxini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hylesinus_fraxini) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/hylesinus-leperisinus-fraxini.jpg)
- **Ash weevil (Stereonychus fraxini)** — ~10–100 adults defoliating per tree in outbreak years. [Stereonychus on stressed ash](https://resjournals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.1365-2311.1994.tb00251.x) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/stereonychus-fraxini.jpg)
- **Moth caterpillars — coronet, brick, centre-barred sallow, privet hawk-moth (Lepidoptera)** — ~100s of larvae per mature tree across the season. [Woodland Trust ash](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/ash/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/lepidoptera.jpg)
- **Lesser stag beetle (Dorcus parallelipipedus)** — ~1–5 adults emerging from rotten ash heartwood per veteran tree; ash is one of its top hosts. [Woodland Trust ash](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/ash/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/dorcus-parallelipipedus.jpg)
- **High brown / silver-washed fritillary butterflies (Argynnis spp.)** — adults nectaring in ashy clearings, ~5–20 per tree on warm days. [Woodland Trust ash](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/ash/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/argynnis.jpg)
- **Bullfinch (Pyrrhula pyrrhula)** — 1–5 birds per fruiting tree; ash keys are a known winter staple for bullfinches. [Woodland Trust ash](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/ash/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/pyrrhula-pyrrhula.jpg)
- **Tawny Owl, Redstart, Nuthatch, Woodpeckers (Strix aluco / Phoenicurus phoenicurus / Sitta / Dendrocopos)** — 1–2 cavity-nesting pairs per veteran tree; ash forms rot-cavities readily. [Woodland Trust ash](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/ash/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/strix-aluco-phoenicurus-phoenicurus-sitta-dendrocopos.jpg)
- **Lichens & bark mosses (Lobarion, Graphidion communities)** — ~30–100+ epiphytic lichen species per veteran ash; ash bark is famously lichen-rich because it's near-neutral pH. [Kew European ash](https://www.kew.org/plants/european-ash) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/lobarion-graphidion-communities.jpg)
- **Common Pipistrelle / brown long-eared bat (Pipistrellus / Plecotus auritus)** — 1–10 bats per cavity-bearing veteran ash; rot-pockets and split limbs are key roosts. [Fraxinus excelsior Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fraxinus_excelsior) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/pipistrellus-plecotus-auritus.jpg)
