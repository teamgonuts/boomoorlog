# Acer — Maple (Esdoorn)

**Archetype (draft):** Bruiser
**Amsterdam trees:** 26746

## Personality
The workhorse of the Amsterdam canopy — thick-limbed, dependable, and hits hard enough that you'll feel it for days, though never in a hurry.

## Real-world facts
- **Typical / max height:** 15–30 m typical; up to 45 m (Bigleaf maple) depending on species
- **Lifespan:** 80–200 years; some individuals (e.g. Bigleaf maple) reach 300 years
- **Wood:** Air-dry density ~620–720 kg/m³ (hard maple); Janka hardness ~6,450 N (1,450 lbf) for Sugar Maple; soft maples ~700–950 lbf. Dense and tough — classic heavy-blow wood.
- **Growth speed:** Medium (height gains 30–60 cm/yr for most species)
- **Ecology / notable traits:** Excellent fall-colour display; winged samaras for wide seed dispersal; some species have mild allelopathic leaf litter; Sugar Maple is tapped for syrup production; very wide genus (~130 species) across Northern Hemisphere — culturally iconic (Canadian flag).

## Combat flavor
Acer's dense hardwood translates to powerful but measured strikes — a Bruiser that charges in and stays in the fight. Its samaras hint at a passive area-scatter ability; its thick canopy could manifest as a damage-reduction shield.

## Living creatures
Acer supports a modest but locally dense fauna — about 16 aphid species in Britain alone, and sycamore in particular is famous for its summer aphid clouds and for hosting both blue tits and squirrels in the canopy.

- **Sycamore aphid (Drepanosiphum platanoidis / Aphididae)** — thousands per mature sycamore in peak season; the dominant herbivore of NW European maples and a long-running study system for population ecology. [Influential Points: Aphids on Acer](http://influentialpoints.com/Gallery/Aphids_on_maple_and_sycamore_Acer_in_Britain.htm)
- **Eriophyid gall mites (Aceria pseudoplatini, A. eriobia / Eriophyidae)** — easily tens of thousands per tree; producers of the red nail-galls and felt-galls that pepper sycamore leaves by June. [RHS: Gall mites](https://www.rhs.org.uk/biodiversity/gall-mites)
- **Wood ants & sugar-tending ants (Formica, Lasius / Formicidae)** — a single ant column may run hundreds-strong on one tree, farming the aphid honeydew. [Influential Points: Aphids on Acer](http://influentialpoints.com/Gallery/Aphids_on_maple_and_sycamore_Acer_in_Britain.htm)
- **Sycamore moth caterpillar (Acronicta aceris / Noctuidae)** — usually a handful per tree, but unmistakable "punk" tufted larvae; one of the signature Lepidoptera of urban maples. [UKmoths: Acronicta aceris](https://www.ukmoths.org.uk/species/acronicta-aceris/)
- **Maple-feeding moths (multiple Geometridae & Noctuidae)** — ~20–50 caterpillars across many species on a mature tree in spring; the protein bank that drives the blue-tit nesting season. [Woodland Trust: Sycamore](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/sycamore/)
- **Honey bees & bumblebees (Apis / Bombus)** — dozens of foragers at any moment when Acer is in flower; sycamore is a top early-summer nectar source in NW Europe. [Woodland Trust: Sycamore](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/sycamore/)
- **Hoverflies & lacewings (Syrphidae, Chrysopidae)** — dozens per tree, drawn in by the aphid glut as predators of the honeydew producers. [Influential Points: Aphids on Acer](http://influentialpoints.com/Gallery/Aphids_on_maple_and_sycamore_Acer_in_Britain.htm)
- **Blue & Great Tits (Cyanistes caeruleus / Parus major)** — typically 1 nesting pair per mature tree with a suitable cavity; nestlings inhale aphid- and caterpillar-fed crops. [Woodland Trust: Sycamore](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/sycamore/)
- **Eurasian Grey Squirrel (Sciurus carolinensis)** — 1–3 individuals using a mature canopy for dreys and bark-stripping; sycamore is a favourite drey tree in Dutch parks. [Woodland Trust: Sycamore](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/sycamore/)
- **Common Pipistrelle (Pipistrellus pipistrellus)** — 1 roosting bat (or small colony) where mature maples develop trunk cavities and loose bark. [Bat Conservation Trust: Bats and woodland](https://www.bats.org.uk/our-work/landscapes-for-bats/bats-and-woodland)
