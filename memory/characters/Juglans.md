# Juglans — Walnut (Juglans)

**Archetype (draft):** Skirmisher
**Amsterdam trees:** 866

## Personality
Patient, calculating, and chemically hostile — Juglans poisons the ground around itself before the fight even starts, then hits you with premium hardwood when you're already weakened.

## Real-world facts
- **Typical / max height:** 10–40 m depending on species (Black Walnut up to 40 m)
- **Lifespan:** 130–250 years (Black Walnut); English Walnut similar range; long-lived with slow maturation
- **Wood:** Air-dry density ~610–650 kg/m³; Janka hardness ~4,490 N (1,010 lbf Black Walnut; 1,220 lbf English Walnut) — premium semi-heavy hardwood
- **Growth speed:** Medium (40–50 ft in 20 years under good conditions)
- **Ecology / notable traits:** Produces juglone (5-hydroxy-1,4-naphthoquinone) — a potent allelopathic toxin released by roots, bark, and leaf litter; kills or stunts tomato, apple, birch, and many other plants within 15–18 m radius; nuts edible and calorie-dense; culturally prized for premium furniture and gunstocks; mildly toxic to horses (causes laminitis from shavings).

## Combat flavor
The Skirmisher's hallmark is zone-denial: juglone is essentially a deployable poison field that weakens enemy units before melee contact. Dense, premium hardwood means slow but heavy strikes — a patient fighter who has already poisoned the battlefield.

## Living creatures
Walnut is famously allelopathic (juglone in roots, leaves, husks) — its understory is impoverished, but the canopy itself hosts a small, dedicated specialist guild plus the nut-eaters that make the whole evolutionary deal work.

- **Large walnut aphid (Panaphis juglandis, Aphididae)** — rows along leaf veins; thousands per mature tree in summer, almost exclusively on Juglans regia. [Influentialpoints: Panaphis juglandis](https://influentialpoints.com/Gallery/Panaphis_juglandis_Large_walnut_aphid.htm)
- **Small walnut aphid (Chromaphis juglandicola, Aphididae)** — leaf undersides; tens of thousands on a heavily infested tree. [Influentialpoints: Chromaphis juglandicola](https://influentialpoints.com/Gallery/Chromaphis_juglandicola_Small_walnut_aphid.htm)
- **Walnut husk fly (Rhagoletis completa, Tephritidae)** — can infest up to 80% of nuts; hundreds of maggots in a fruiting tree. [PMC: defense dynamics in walnut fruit during husk fly infestation](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12604324/)
- **Codling moth (Cydia pomonella, Tortricidae)** — dozens of larvae per fruiting tree, sharing the nuts with the husk fly. [Oxford Academic: walnut aphid and codling moth control](https://academic.oup.com/jee/article-abstract/57/6/950/2208016)
- **Ladybirds & lacewings (Coccinellidae, Chrysopidae)** — hundreds drawn in by the aphid blooms. [Influentialpoints: Chromaphis juglandicola](https://influentialpoints.com/Gallery/Chromaphis_juglandicola_Small_walnut_aphid.htm)
- **Honeybees & flies on aphid honeydew** — dozens to hundreds during peak aphid season; juglone keeps most direct flower visitors away (wind-pollinated). [Influentialpoints: Panaphis juglandis](https://influentialpoints.com/Gallery/Panaphis_juglandis_Large_walnut_aphid.htm)
- **Eurasian red squirrel / introduced grey (Sciurus vulgaris / S. carolinensis)** — 1–3 individuals cache nuts; major dispersers. [Gardenia: English walnut](https://www.gardenia.net/plant/juglans-regia-walnut)
- **Eurasian Jay (Garrulus glandarius)** — typically 1–2 birds, hides hundreds of nuts per autumn. [Gardenia: English walnut](https://www.gardenia.net/plant/juglans-regia-walnut)
- **Carrion Crow / Hooded Crow (Corvus corone)** — 1–4 birds dropping nuts on roads to crack them; a documented Amsterdam behavior. [Gardenia: English walnut](https://www.gardenia.net/plant/juglans-regia-walnut)
- **Great Tit / Blue Tit (Parus major / Cyanistes caeruleus)** — 1 nesting pair, feeding heavily on the aphid colonies. [Influentialpoints: Chromaphis juglandicola](https://influentialpoints.com/Gallery/Chromaphis_juglandicola_Small_walnut_aphid.htm)
