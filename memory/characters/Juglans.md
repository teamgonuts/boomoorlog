# Juglans — Walnut (Juglans)

**Archetype (draft):** Skirmisher
**Amsterdam trees:** 866

## Personality
Patient, calculating, and chemically hostile — Juglans poisons the ground around itself before the fight even starts, then hits you with premium hardwood when you're already weakened.

## Real-world facts
- **Typical / max height:** 10–40 m depending on species (Black Walnut up to 40 m)
- **Lifespan:** 130–250 years (Black Walnut); English Walnut similar range; long-lived with slow maturation
- **Wood:** Air-dry density ~610–650 kg/m³; Janka hardness ~4,490 N (1,010 lbf Black Walnut; 1,220 lbf English Walnut) — premium semi-heavy hardwood
- **Growth speed:** Medium (40–50 ft in 20 years under good conditions)
- **Ecology / notable traits:** Produces juglone (5-hydroxy-1,4-naphthoquinone) — a potent allelopathic toxin released by roots, bark, and leaf litter; kills or stunts tomato, apple, birch, and many other plants within 15–18 m radius; nuts edible and calorie-dense; culturally prized for premium furniture and gunstocks; mildly toxic to horses (causes laminitis from shavings).

## Combat flavor
The Skirmisher's hallmark is zone-denial: juglone is essentially a deployable poison field that weakens enemy units before melee contact. Dense, premium hardwood means slow but heavy strikes — a patient fighter who has already poisoned the battlefield.
