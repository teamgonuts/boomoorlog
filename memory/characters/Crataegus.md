**Archetype (draft):** Support

## Personality
A battle-scarred hedgerow veteran bristling with thorns, it fights dirty with a laugh — the original barbed-wire barrier, ancient enough to have watched empires rise and fall from behind its spiky ramparts.

## Real-world facts
- **Typical / max height:** 5–10 m typical; up to 14 m in favourable conditions
- **Lifespan:** 250 years typical; up to 400 years on good sites — notably long-lived for a small tree
- **Wood:** ~865 kg/m³ air-dry (based on Crataegus mexicana; genus average similar — dense, tough hardwood); name literally derives from Greek for "hardness"
- **Growth speed:** Slow to medium
- **Ecology / notable traits:** Dense, multi-branched thorns up to 10 cm long — classic defensive barrier plant; berries (haws) feed birds and small mammals; important nectar source for pollinators; highly adaptable to poor and dry soils; culturally revered in Celtic tradition as a fairy tree; used in herbal medicine for the heart

## Combat flavor
Dense, slow-growing, and armored — Crataegus is a defensive specialist whose thorns make melee attackers think twice. Its long lifespan hints at a passive regeneration or fortification ability: old hedges that just won't die.
