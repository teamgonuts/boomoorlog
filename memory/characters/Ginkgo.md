# Ginkgo — Ginkgo (Japanse notenboom)

**Archetype (draft):** Bruiser (Legendary)
**Amsterdam trees:** 1463

## Personality
A 270-million-year-old living fossil that survived the Cretaceous extinction and the Hiroshima atomic blast — ancient, imperturbable, and carrying chemical weapons in its smelly seeds that have been deterring predators since before the dinosaurs.

## Real-world facts
- **Typical / max height:** 20–35 m typical; max ~40 m
- **Lifespan:** 1000–3500 years recorded; theoretically unlimited vascular cambium renewal — exceptionally long-lived, flag as living fossil
- **Wood:** ~465 kg/m³ air-dry density — soft, light, weak wood (classed as softwood); little structural strength; Janka not widely reported, very low
- **Growth speed:** Fast in first 30 years (>30 cm/year); medium thereafter
- **Ecology / notable traits:** Sole surviving species of division Ginkgophyta, unchanged for 200+ million years (living fossil); dioecious — female trees produce butyric-acid-reeking seeds used in Chinese medicine; highly disease- and pest-resistant (survived Hiroshima, 1945); ginkgolides in leaves are natural insect repellents and anticoagulants used in medicine; allelopathic seed pulp; urban pollution-tolerant; wild population is endangered (Zhejiang, China) though cultivated globally — wild status: rare, cultivated: common
- **World rarity note:** Wild Ginkgo is critically rare; cultivated globally — recorded as "notable" overall

## Combat flavor
The paradox of Ginkgo: legendary age and indestructibility, but soft wood means slow, weak direct strikes — its power lies in chemical warfare (toxic seed stench, ginkgolide anticoagulants) and near-unkillable HP. Signature ability candidate: **Ancient Immunity** — resists all status debuffs; passive **Butyric Barrage** poisons nearby enemies with fetid seed pulp.

## Living creatures
Ginkgo is a living fossil with essentially no co-evolved European herbivores — the leaves' ginkgolides and bilobalide deter almost every insect, and wind-pollinated reproduction means no specialist pollinators either. Its fauna is mostly generalists using the tree as a perch or shelter.

- **Generalist aphids (Aphidoidea, occasional)** — ~10–100 per tree at most; Ginkgo is famously aphid-light because of its leaf chemistry. [Ginkgo and Insects (Springer)](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-4-431-68416-9_19)
- **Loopers / generalist caterpillars (Lepidoptera, rare)** — ~0–5 per tree per year; hand-pickable, an arborist's footnote rather than a real fauna. [Ginkgo insect problems](https://www.gardeningknowhow.com/ornamental/trees/ginkgo/ginkgo-insect-problems.htm)
- **Spider mites (Tetranychidae)** — ~10–100 per stressed urban tree; one of the few groups that occasionally bothers ginkgo. [What eats Ginkgo biloba](https://greg.app/what-eats-ginkgo-biloba/)
- **Bark-crevice springtails & psocids (Collembola, Psocoptera)** — ~100s per tree; living on lichens, mosses, and bark microflora rather than the tree itself. [Ginkgo biloba International Biological Flora](https://besjournals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/1365-2745.13856)
- **Spiders & harvestmen (Arachnida)** — ~10–50 per tree; generalist predators using foliage as web/ambush substrate (qualitative; no per-tree count in literature). [Ginkgo biloba Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ginkgo_biloba)
- **House Sparrow / Eurasian Tree Sparrow (Passer domesticus / montanus)** — 1–10 birds perching and dust-bathing in the canopy; using ginkgo as urban shelter, not food. [Ginkgo as urban tree](https://besjournals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/1365-2745.13856)
- **Wood Pigeon / Eurasian Magpie / Hooded Crow (Columba palumbus / Pica pica / Corvus cornix)** — 1–2 nesting pairs per mature tree in cities; the open architecture is fine for stick nests. [Ginkgo biloba International Biological Flora](https://besjournals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/1365-2745.13856)
- **Squirrels (Sciurus vulgaris / S. carolinensis)** — 1–3 individuals per female tree during seed-fall; will eat the inner kernel despite the reeking butyric-acid pulp. [Yale Nature Walk: Ginkgo](https://naturewalk.yale.edu/trees/ginkgoaceae/ginkgo-biloba/ginkgo-41)
- **Brown rats (Rattus norvegicus)** — 1–5 per fruiting female ginkgo on an Amsterdam street; will scavenge fallen seeds despite the stench. [Yale Nature Walk: Ginkgo](https://naturewalk.yale.edu/trees/ginkgoaceae/ginkgo-biloba/ginkgo-41)
- **Lichens & mosses on bark (cryptogams)** — ~5–20 species per mature urban ginkgo trunk; the tree's living surface community is mostly non-animal. [Ginkgo biloba International Biological Flora](https://besjournals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/1365-2745.13856)
