# Betula — Birch (Berk)

**Archetype (draft):** Bruiser
**Amsterdam trees:** 11715

## Personality
Scrappy and light on its feet for a Bruiser — birch hits fast, colonises ground no one else wants, and burns so bright in autumn you almost miss that it died young.

## Real-world facts
- **Typical / max height:** 20 m typical; up to 40 m (Paper birch, exceptional specimens)
- **Lifespan:** 30–100 years (short-lived; 30 years in warm zones, 100+ in cold climates)
- **Wood:** Air-dry density ~510–600 kg/m³ (B. pendula ~512 kg/m³, B. pubescens ~478 kg/m³); Janka ~4,400 N (990 lbf, Yellow birch) — medium-hard, relatively light for a hardwood
- **Growth speed:** Medium to fast (pioneer species, rapid early growth)
- **Ecology / notable traits:** Classic pioneer species — colonises bare ground, burns easily (bark used as natural firestarter since prehistory), fixes disturbed soils; host to >300 insect species in UK; allelopathic leaf litter mildly inhibits competitors; bark has natural antiseptic (betulin); culturally sacred in Norse and Celtic traditions (symbol of new beginnings).

## Combat flavor
Lighter wood than Acer means quicker strikes at lower impact — a fast-swinging Bruiser who trades longevity for aggression. Its pioneer ecology could manifest as an ability to spawn on contested ground; its flammable bark is a natural liability (fire-damage multiplier).

## Living creatures
Betula is one of the top insect-supporting trees in NW Europe — 300+ associated insect species and a winter seed-bank that keeps siskins, redpolls and lesser redpolls alive through the cold months.

- **Silver birch aphid (Euceraphis betulae / Aphididae)** — thousands per mature tree; pale-green aphids drifting off leaves are the signature summer rain of a birch canopy. [Influential Points: Euceraphis betulae](https://influentialpoints.com/Gallery/Euceraphis_betulae_Silver_birch_aphid.htm)
- **Other birch aphids (Callipterinella, Symydobius / Aphidoidea)** — 18 aphid species use silver/downy birch in Europe; combined populations easily reach the tens of thousands per tree. [Influential Points: Aphids on birch](https://influentialpoints.com/Gallery/Aphids_on_birch_betula.htm)
- **Birch leaf-mining sawflies (Heterarthrus, Fenusa / Tenthredinidae)** — typically thousands of mines per mature tree by July; the spreading brown blotches are the giveaway. [Woodland Trust: Silver birch](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/silver-birch/)
- **Birch moths (Geometridae & Notodontidae, incl. Endromis versicolora)** — 100+ moth species in the UK use birch; dozens of caterpillars per tree in spring drive the woodland food web. [Woodland Trust: Silver birch](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/silver-birch/)
- **Birch shieldbug (Elasmostethus interstinctus / Acanthosomatidae)** — dozens per tree; one of the more conspicuous true bugs in any Betula canopy. [NatureSpot via Woodland Trust](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/silver-birch/)
- **Birch polypore & associated fungus gnats (Fomitopsis betulina + Mycetophilidae)** — a single bracket can house hundreds of larvae; birch polypore is host-specific to Betula and is the larder for an entire community of saproxylic flies and beetles. [Woodland Trust: Silver birch](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/silver-birch/)
- **Eurasian Siskin (Spinus spinus)** — winter flocks of 10–50 per fruiting birch; birch and alder seed are their staple cold-season fuel. [Cornell: Redpoll Life History](https://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/Redpoll/lifehistory)
- **Lesser Redpoll (Acanthis cabaret)** — 5–20 per tree in winter, mixed with siskins, dangling acrobatically from the catkins. [Wildlife Trusts: Redpoll](https://www.wildlifetrusts.org/wildlife-explorer/birds/finches-and-buntings/redpoll)
- **Great Spotted Woodpecker (Dendrocopos major)** — 1 pair per mature/dying tree; soft birchwood is a favourite for excavating nest holes that later house tits, nuthatches and bats. [Woodland Trust: Silver birch](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/silver-birch/)
- **Noctule Bat (Nyctalus noctula)** — 1 roosting bat (or small maternity colony) per mature tree with woodpecker holes; noctules are obligate tree-cavity roosters and birch is a classic host in Dutch parks. [Bat Conservation Trust: Woodland specialists](https://www.bats.org.uk/our-work/landscapes-for-bats/bats-and-woodland/woodland-specialists)
