# Betula — Birch (Berk)

**Archetype (draft):** Bruiser
**Amsterdam trees:** 11715

## Personality
Scrappy and light on its feet for a Bruiser — birch hits fast, colonises ground no one else wants, and burns so bright in autumn you almost miss that it died young.

## Real-world facts
- **Typical / max height:** 20 m typical; up to 40 m (Paper birch, exceptional specimens)
- **Lifespan:** 30–100 years (short-lived; 30 years in warm zones, 100+ in cold climates)
- **Wood:** Air-dry density ~510–600 kg/m³ (B. pendula ~512 kg/m³, B. pubescens ~478 kg/m³); Janka ~4,400 N (990 lbf, Yellow birch) — medium-hard, relatively light for a hardwood
- **Growth speed:** Medium to fast (pioneer species, rapid early growth)
- **Ecology / notable traits:** Classic pioneer species — colonises bare ground, burns easily (bark used as natural firestarter since prehistory), fixes disturbed soils; host to >300 insect species in UK; allelopathic leaf litter mildly inhibits competitors; bark has natural antiseptic (betulin); culturally sacred in Norse and Celtic traditions (symbol of new beginnings).

## Combat flavor
Lighter wood than Acer means quicker strikes at lower impact — a fast-swinging Bruiser who trades longevity for aggression. Its pioneer ecology could manifest as an ability to spawn on contested ground; its flammable bark is a natural liability (fire-damage multiplier).
