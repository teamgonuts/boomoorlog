# Ilex — Holly (Hulst)

**Archetype (draft):** Support
**Amsterdam trees:** 1612

## Personality
A prickly, berry-bearing evergreen that shelters birds in its dense canopy through the darkest winters and punctures the hands of anyone foolish enough to underestimate it — the eternal sentinel of the understory.

## Real-world facts
- **Typical / max height:** 10–15 m typical; up to 25 m in old specimens (I. aquifolium)
- **Lifespan:** ~300 years; older records exist for large specimens
- **Wood:** ~610 kg/m³ air-dry; Janka ~1,020 lbf — pale, almost white, finely grained, hard and dense; historically used for inlay, piano keys, and engraving blocks due to its fine, uniform texture; must be well-dried or warps badly
- **Growth speed:** Slow to medium
- **Ecology / notable traits:** Spiny leaf margins deter browsing (leaves on lower branches spinier, smoother higher up where herbivores can't reach — well-documented trait); berries toxic to humans but vital winter food for birds (especially thrushes); extremely shade-tolerant; dioecious — only female plants bear berries; I. aquifolium classified as invasive in parts of North America; deeply embedded in European midwinter/Christmas symbolism; provides dense nest cover for birds

## Combat flavor
A slow-growing but resilient defender with double-edged ecology: its dense spiny canopy is a natural barrier, while toxic red berries could function as a passive area-denial poison drop. The "spinier at the bottom" trait hints at a mechanic where its defense scales inversely with threat proximity.

## Living creatures
Evergreen dense canopy + winter berries = an unusually concentrated wildlife magnet for its size; specialist associates (holly leaf miner, Holly Blue) are tied to no other tree.

- **Holly leaf miner (Phytomyza ilicis, Agromyzidae)** — typically 5–25% of leaves mined; a mature tree can carry several thousand mines simultaneously. [Wikipedia: Phytomyza ilicis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phytomyza_ilicis) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/phytomyza-ilicis.jpg)
- **Holly Blue butterfly (Celastrina argiolus, Lycaenidae)** — 1–5 caterpillars per tree on flower buds in spring; holly is the primary spring host. [Woodland Trust holly](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/holly/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/celastrina-argiolus.jpg)
- **Holly tortrix and yellow-barred brindle moths (Rhopobota / Acasis)** — several dozen larvae per tree across the season. [Woodland Trust holly](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/holly/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/rhopobota.jpg)
- **Honeybees and solitary bees (Apis, Andrena spp.)** — dozens to low hundreds visit the small white flowers in May. [Woodland Trust holly](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/holly/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/apis.jpg)
- **Parasitoid wasps of the leaf miner (Chalcidoidea, esp. Chrysocharis, Pleurotropis)** — hundreds per tree, tracking the miner population. [Edinburgh: holly leaf-miner and its parasites](https://era.ed.ac.uk/handle/1842/27748) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/chalcidoidea.png)
- **Mistle Thrush (Turdus viscivorus)** — 1 individual often defends a berry-laden holly as a winter larder. [Wikipedia: Mistle thrush](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mistle_thrush) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/turdus-viscivorus.jpg)
- **Blackbird, Song Thrush, Redwing, Fieldfare (Turdus spp.)** — 1–10 birds at a time stripping berries in midwinter. [BTO ID guide to berries](https://www.bto.org/learn/about-birds/behaviour/bird-behaviour-eating-berries-and-fruit/id-guide-berries) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/turdus.jpg)
- **Blue & Great Tits (Cyanistes / Parus)** — leave characteristic V-tears in mined leaves; 1 nesting pair often uses the dense spiny canopy. [Field Studies Council holly leaf miner](https://www.field-studies-council.org/resources/14-16-biology/food-chains/holly-leaf-miner/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/cyanistes.jpg)
- **Wren / Dunnock / Robin (Troglodytes, Prunella, Erithacus)** — 1–2 nests in the spiny lower canopy; the prickly leaves deter predators. [Woodland Trust holly](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/holly/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/troglodytes.jpg)
- **Woodpigeon (Columba palumbus)** — a few birds gulping whole berries; a major late-winter disperser. [BTO ID guide to berries](https://www.bto.org/learn/about-birds/behaviour/bird-behaviour-eating-berries-and-fruit/id-guide-berries) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/columba-palumbus.jpg)
