# Fagus — Beech (Beuk)

**Archetype (draft):** Bruiser
**Amsterdam trees:** 2937

## Personality
A brooding, shadow-casting sovereign who poisons the ground beneath her so nothing else dares grow — slow to wake, impossible to move.

## Real-world facts
- **Typical / max height:** 25–35 m typical; up to 50 m maximum
- **Lifespan:** 150–300 years; exceptional specimens exceed 500 years
- **Wood:** ~720 kg/m³ (air-dry); dense, hard, ring-porous hardwood; excellent for heavy structural and furniture use
- **Growth speed:** Medium
- **Ecology / notable traits:** Strong allelopathic effect — slowly decomposing leaf and mast litter releases compounds that suppress germination of competing plants; beechnuts (beechmast) contain fagin (trimethylamine) and saponins, mildly toxic to livestock; dense canopy blocks light so thoroughly that few plants survive beneath; mast seeding years dramatically reshape local fungal communities; culturally iconic in northern European forests

## Combat flavor
Dense wood and allelopathic litter make the Beech a slow but crushing fighter who debuffs nearby enemies just by existing — a Bruiser who wins by denying the enemy ground before the first blow is even struck.

## Living creatures
Beech hosts a deep canopy aphid fauna and a thick litter-layer arthropod community — the dense canopy sterilises the ground above but the slow-rotting mast feeds millions of soil invertebrates below.

- **Woolly beech aphid (Phyllaphis fagi)** — ~1000s–10,000s per mature tree; the diagnostic white woolly fluff on undersides of beech leaves. [Phyllaphis fagi profile](https://influentialpoints.com/Gallery/Phyllaphis_fagi_Woolly_beech_aphid.htm) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/phyllaphis-fagi.jpg)
- **Springtails & oribatid mites in litter (Collembola, Oribatida)** — ~100,000s under one mature beech; beech mast and leaf litter sustain extremely dense soil microfauna. [Beech leaf litter fauna study](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3116155/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/collembola-oribatida.jpg)
- **Beech leaf-mining weevil (Rhynchaenus fagi)** — ~100s–1000s of leaf mines per tree in outbreak years; specialist beech curculionid. [Rhynchaenus fagi notes](https://www.rhs.org.uk/biodiversity/woolly-beech-aphid) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/rhynchaenus-fagi.jpg)
- **Moth caterpillars — barred hook-tip, clay triple-lines, olive crescent, winter moth (Lepidoptera)** — ~1000s of larvae per mature tree in spring leaf-out (winter moth densities famously hit five-figure counts on individual trees). [Woodland Trust beech](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/common-beech/) / [winter moth densities](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/agricultural-and-biological-sciences/operophtera-brumata) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/lepidoptera.jpg)
- **Hericium & beech wood-decay beetles (Coleoptera saproxylica)** — ~10–50 saproxylic species per veteran beech; old beeches are key European deadwood habitat. [Woodland Trust beech](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/common-beech/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/coleoptera-saproxylica.png)
- **Chaffinch & Brambling (Fringilla coelebs / montifringilla)** — 10s–100s per tree in mast years; bramblings irrupt into European beech forests specifically to eat beechmast. [Beech ecology PDF](https://forest.jrc.ec.europa.eu/media/atlas/Fagus_sylvatica.pdf) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/fringilla-coelebs-montifringilla.jpg)
- **Great Spotted Woodpecker & Nuthatch (Dendrocopos major / Sitta europaea)** — 1–2 pairs per veteran tree, nesting in cavities and wedging beechnuts into bark. [Beech cavity nesting](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/common-beech/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/dendrocopos-major-sitta-europaea.jpg)
- **Common Pipistrelle / Noctule (Pipistrellus pipistrellus / Nyctalus noctula)** — 1–20 bats roosting in cavities of veteran beeches; large old beeches are prime maternity roost trees. [Beech veteran tree habitat](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/common-beech/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/pipistrellus-pipistrellus-nyctalus-noctula.jpg)
- **Wood mouse / Bank vole / Grey & Red Squirrel (Apodemus, Myodes, Sciurus)** — 5–30 small mammals per tree in mast autumns, gorging on beechnuts. [Beech wildlife profile](https://www.best4hedging.co.uk/hedge-plants/beech-hedging-fagus) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/apodemus-myodes-sciurus.jpg)
- **Wild boar (Sus scrofa)** — 0–1 per tree in continental Europe (not Amsterdam itself); beechmast is a historic pannage food. [Beech ecology PDF](https://forest.jrc.ec.europa.eu/media/atlas/Fagus_sylvatica.pdf) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/sus-scrofa.jpg)
