# Ulmus — Elm (Iep)

**Archetype (draft):** Bruiser
**Amsterdam trees:** 37505

## Personality
Amsterdam's most common street warrior — scarred by plague, stubbornly resilient, hitting harder than anyone expects from a tree that should have died decades ago.

## Real-world facts
- **Typical / max height:** 21–35 m; up to ~40 m in ideal conditions
- **Lifespan:** Typically 200 years; some specimens exceed 300 years; European field populations dramatically reduced by Dutch elm disease (Ophiostoma novo-ulmi)
- **Wood:** ~550 kg/m³ (air-dry); interlocked grain makes it very tough and resistant to splitting; soft/rock elm species range 560–660 kg/m³; Janka ~800–1320 lbf depending on species
- **Growth speed:** Fast (3–6 feet/year in ideal conditions)
- **Ecology / notable traits:** Highly tolerant of wet, compacted, and urban soils; interlocked grain historically prized for wheel hubs and coffins precisely because it will not split under impact; devastated across Europe and North America by Dutch elm disease spread by bark beetles; resistant cultivars now deployed widely; no significant toxicity or allelopathy; keystone riparian species

## Combat flavor
Fast growth means quick leveling and rapid deployment; interlocked grain gives exceptional toughness (hard to one-shot), and the scars of Dutch elm disease could manifest as a unique plague-resistance or disease-immunity mechanic.
