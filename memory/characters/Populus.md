# Populus — Poplar (Populier)

**Archetype (draft):** Bruiser
**Amsterdam trees:** 12981

## Personality
The Poplar is a reckless, sky-hungry brawler who shoots up faster than anyone else on the battlefield and hits with a flurry of light, rapid blows — never pausing, never patient, never meant to last.

## Real-world facts
- **Typical / max height:** 25–35 m typical; up to 45 m in some species (black cottonwood can reach ~45 m)
- **Lifespan:** 30–150 years depending on species; cultivated poplars often only 30–50 years; native balsam poplars up to 150–200 years; some wild specimens reportedly to 400 years
- **Wood:** Air-dry density ~385–450 kg/m³; Janka hardness ~2,400 N (540 lbf). One of the lightest commercial hardwoods — soft, easy to split, rapid to ignite.
- **Growth speed:** Fast (1.5–3 m/year in good conditions)
- **Ecology / notable traits:** Prolific wind-borne cotton seed dispersal; used for phytoremediation (absorbs heavy metals from soil); allelopathic root exudates suppress some competitors; highly invasive in riparian zones; monoecious; short-rotation coppice crop; cultural symbol of speed and transience in European tradition

## Combat flavor
The Poplar's feather-light wood means it strikes fast and cheap — high attack speed, low hit per blow. Its phytoremediation trait could become a passive "contamination cleanse" aura on occupied ground.

## Living creatures
Populus is one of the richest insect hosts in NW Europe (Southwood/Kennedy & Southwood ranked it near the top, ~90+ associated phytophagous insect species in Britain) — a soft, fast-growing, sap-leaky tree that catkin-feeders, leaf-miners and bark-borers all exploit.

- **Aphids (Aphidoidea, esp. Chaitophorus, Pterocomma)** — 10,000s per mature tree at summer peak; honeydew rains from poplar canopies. [Influential Points: Pterocomma populeum](https://influentialpoints.com/Gallery/Pterocomma_populeum_Large_poplar_aphid.htm)
- **Springtails & oribatid mites (Collembola, Oribatida) in bark crevices** — 100,000s per tree (extrapolated from bark microfauna densities); the unseen majority of the canopy fauna. [Soil & bark microarthropods overview](https://www.britannica.com/animal/springtail)
- **Poplar hawk moth (Laothoe populi, Sphingidae)** — 1–5 caterpillars per tree in good years; one of the most reliable Populus specialists in NW Europe. [Woodland Trust: black poplar](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/black-poplar/)
- **Hornet moth (Sesia apiformis, Sesiidae)** — 1–3 larvae boring in the lower trunk of a mature poplar; Britain's largest clearwing, near-Populus-obligate. [Butterfly Conservation: hornet moth](https://butterfly-conservation.org/moths/hornet-moth)
- **Wood leopard moth (Zeuzera pyrina, Cossidae) and figure of eight (Diloba caeruleocephala)** — handful of larvae chewing inside wood or on leaves each year. [Woodland Trust: black poplar](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/black-poplar/)
- **Puss moth & sallow kitten (Cerura/Furcula, Notodontidae)** — 2–10 dramatic caterpillars per tree, especially on white and grey poplars. [Woodland Trust: white poplar](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/white-poplar/)
- **Poplar leaf beetles (Chrysomela populi, C. tremula)** — 100s of metallic adults and grubs skeletonising young leaves on regrowth. [GBIF: Chrysomela populi](https://www.gbif.org/species/4474923)
- **Aspen hoverfly (Hammerschmidtia ferruginea)** — 1 small population per old aspen with dead wood; a UK Priority Species saproxylic specialist. [Buglife: aspen hoverfly](https://www.buglife.org.uk/bugs/bug-directory/aspen-hoverfly/)
- **Blue & Great Tits (Cyanistes caeruleus / Parus major)** — 1–2 nesting pairs per mature trunk cavity; gorge on the poplar caterpillar flush to feed chicks. [BTO: Blue Tit](https://www.bto.org/understanding-birds/birdfacts/blue-tit)
- **Common pipistrelle (Pipistrellus pipistrellus)** — 1 maternity roost (10–50 bats) in a hollow old poplar is typical along Amsterdam canals. [Vleermuis.net: pipistrelle](https://www.vleermuis.net/vleermuissoorten/gewone-dwergvleermuis)
