# Koelreuteria — Golden Rain Tree (Koelreuteria)

**Archetype (draft):** Support
**Amsterdam trees:** 532

## Personality
A flashy showboat who rains golden flowers on the battlefield and quietly seeds the entire map while everyone is distracted by the spectacle.

## Real-world facts
- **Typical / max height:** 7–12 m typical; up to ~15 m
- **Lifespan:** ~50 years
- **Wood:** ~620 kg/m³ air-dry density — moderate density but notably brittle; susceptible to storm damage
- **Growth speed:** fast (reaches 3–4 m in 5–7 years)
- **Ecology / notable traits:** Invasive in parts of eastern USA — self-seeds prolifically and outcompetes natives; drought- and heat-tolerant; thrives in poor, alkaline urban soils; bee-pollinated; papery lantern seed pods disperse widely; not nitrogen-fixing; no thorns; pollution-tolerant urban survivor

## Combat flavor
Fast growth and prolific seeding make Koelreuteria a rapid-deployment support unit that floods adjacent tiles — but brittle wood means it shatters under concentrated fire. Its invasive seeding could manifest as a passive that plants saplings in empty lane slots.

## Living creatures
East-Asian ornamental in the soapberry family — very few European insects feed on it, but its late-summer flower spike is a genuine bee bonanza when little else is in bloom.

- **Honeybees (Apis mellifera)** — hundreds at a time on the panicles in July–August; major late-season nectar source. [Bee Happy Plants: Koelreuteria paniculata](https://beehappyplants.co.uk/bee-plants/koelreuteria-paniculata/)
- **Short-tongued bumblebees (Bombus terrestris, B. lapidarius)** — dozens working the open flowers. [Balkan Ecology Project: Koelreuteria paniculata](https://www.balkep.org/koelreuteria-paniculata.html)
- **Hoverflies (Syrphidae)** — dozens of nectar-feeders, supplementing the bees. [Balkan Ecology Project: Koelreuteria paniculata](https://www.balkep.org/koelreuteria-paniculata.html)
- **Solitary bees (Andrena, Lasioglossum spp.)** — tens visiting the easy-access shallow flowers. [Bee Happy Plants: Koelreuteria paniculata](https://beehappyplants.co.uk/bee-plants/koelreuteria-paniculata/)
- **Aphids (Aphidoidea, generalist colonisers)** — hundreds on young shoots; no Koelreuteria-specialist aphid is known in Europe. [Gardenia: Koelreuteria paniculata](https://www.gardenia.net/plant/koelreuteria-paniculata)
- **Spider mites (Tetranychidae)** — common on stressed urban specimens, easily thousands in hot dry summers. [Gardenia: Koelreuteria paniculata](https://www.gardenia.net/plant/koelreuteria-paniculata)
- **Soapberry / "goldenrain tree" bug (Jadera haematoloma, Rhopalidae)** — its signature herbivore in the US, but absent from NW Europe; pods stay largely uneaten here. [Bug of the Week: Jadera haematoloma on goldenrain tree](https://bugoftheweek.com/blog/2016/9/26/rain-tree-surprise-golden-rain-tree-bug-ijadera-haematolomai)
- **Wasps (Vespula spp., parasitoid wasps)** — dozens drawn to honeydew and small prey on the panicles. [Bee Happy Plants: Koelreuteria paniculata](https://beehappyplants.co.uk/bee-plants/koelreuteria-paniculata/)
- **House Sparrow / Great Tit (Passer / Parus)** — 1 nesting pair will use the dense crown; they glean aphids and small visitors. [Gardenia: Koelreuteria paniculata](https://www.gardenia.net/plant/koelreuteria-paniculata)
- **Magpie / Carrion Crow (Pica / Corvus)** — 1–2 birds using mature trees as urban perches; corvid territory marker. [Gardenia: Koelreuteria paniculata](https://www.gardenia.net/plant/koelreuteria-paniculata)
