# Platanus — Plane (Plataan)

**Archetype (draft):** Bruiser
**Amsterdam trees:** 15962

## Personality
Amsterdam's most abundant warrior is a battle-hardened urban giant with peeling camouflage bark and pollution-proof lungs — the dependable heavy infantry that holds the line while flashier trees get the glory.

## Real-world facts
- **Typical / max height:** 25–35 m typical; up to ~50 m in exceptional specimens (Oriental plane, P. orientalis, regularly reaches 30 m+)
- **Lifespan:** Centuries; P. orientalis specimens verified at 2,000+ years in the Caucasus; London plane (P. × acerifolia) typical urban lifespan 100–200+ years
- **Wood:** Air-dry density ~560–610 kg/m³ (specific gravity 0.52–0.61 at 12% MC); Janka hardness ~1,300 lbf (5,780 N) for American sycamore (P. occidentalis); medium-hard, interlocked grain produces attractive lacewood figure
- **Growth speed:** Fast (early-successional; rapid establishment especially for London plane)
- **Ecology / notable traits:** Exceptional pollution and urban heat tolerance; distinctive exfoliating bark exposes bright cream/olive patches — acts as particulate matter trap; large palmate leaves create dense shade; allelopathic leaf litter can inhibit some understory plants; widely planted street tree globally; London plane is a sterile hybrid (P. orientalis × P. occidentalis); hairs from seed balls can cause respiratory irritation (airway irritant — a subtle toxicity angle); deep tap roots resist wind throw

## Combat flavor
Medium-hard dense wood plus massive urban presence makes the Platanus a reliable front-line bruiser — moderate attack speed, solid hit. Its pollution-absorption and bark-shedding could become a passive armor-refresh ability, or its airborne seed-hair irritant a short-range area-of-effect debuff.

## Living creatures
Platanus is the dominant street tree of Amsterdam but a poor host to native fauna — the Woodland Trust notes "very little wildlife is associated with London plane." Its ecology is dominated by introduced pests, urban birds and roosting bats exploiting cavities.

- **Sycamore lace bug (Corythucha ciliata, Tingidae)** — 1,000s per tree across a season; an American invader now widespread in continental European cities, stippling every leaf. [Forest Research: plane lace bug](https://www.forestresearch.gov.uk/tools-and-resources/fthr/pest-and-disease-resources/plane-lace-bug-corythucha-ciliata/)
- **Plane scale & aphids (Pulvinaria regalis, Phyllaphis fagi-type aphids)** — 100s–1,000s per tree on lower trunk and leaves; honeydew is the source of the sticky pavement under Amsterdam plataans. [CABI: Corythucha ciliata](https://www.cabi.org/isc/datasheet/16264)
- **Anthracnose fungus (Apiognomonia veneta, asexual Discula platani)** — not an animal, but its fruiting bodies and resulting leaf damage host secondary fungivorous mites and springtails in the 1,000s. [PNW Handbooks: sycamore anthracnose](https://pnwhandbooks.org/plantdisease/host-disease/sycamore-platanus-spp-anthracnose)
- **Bark-crevice springtails & oribatid mites (Collembola, Oribatida)** — 100,000s per mature trunk; the flaking camouflage bark is a microhabitat goldmine even when the foliage isn't. [Britannica: springtails](https://www.britannica.com/animal/springtail)
- **Winter moth & mottled umber (Operophtera brumata, Erannis defoliaria)** — 10s–100s of generalist caterpillars per tree; the few Lepidoptera that bother with plane. [RHS: winter moth](https://www.rhs.org.uk/biodiversity/winter-moth-caterpillars)
- **Carpenter bees & solitary bees in dead wood (Xylocopa, Osmia, Megachilidae)** — handfuls of nesting individuals in old branch stubs of veteran trees. [Buglife: solitary bees](https://www.buglife.org.uk/bugs/bug-directory/solitary-bees/)
- **Rooks & carrion crows (Corvus frugilegus / C. corone)** — 1–10 nesting pairs per Amsterdam plane row; the canopy structure is ideal corvid rookery habitat. [Woodland Trust: London plane](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/london-plane/)
- **Rose-ringed parakeet (Psittacula krameri)** — 1–5 pairs per cavity-bearing mature plane in Amsterdam; one of the dominant cavity-users in the city's plataans. [Sovon: halsbandparkiet](https://www.sovon.nl/nl/soort/15820)
- **Grey squirrel (Sciurus carolinensis) — or Eurasian red where present** — 1–2 individuals working a single fruiting plane; seed balls are eaten in late winter. [Woodland Trust: London plane](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/london-plane/)
- **Common pipistrelle (Pipistrellus pipistrellus)** — 1 roost (5–30 bats) in a cavity of a mature urban plane is typical along Amsterdam grachten. [Vleermuis.net: pipistrelle](https://www.vleermuis.net/vleermuissoorten/gewone-dwergvleermuis)
