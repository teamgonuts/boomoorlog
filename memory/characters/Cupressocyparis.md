# Cupressocyparis — Leyland Cypress (Cupressocyparis)

**Archetype (draft):** Juggernaut
**Amsterdam trees:** 101

## Personality
A hybrid wall of green that grows faster than its enemies can plan around it, shrugs off salt spray and shade, and has outlived the arborists sent to manage it.

## Real-world facts
- **Typical / max height:** 15–21 m typical in cultivation; can reach 40 m in ideal conditions
- **Lifespan:** 20–50 years (short for a conifer; structurally unstable at old age in confined planting)
- **Wood:** Air-dry density ~510 kg/m³; pale yellow-brown, fine even texture, straight-grained — moderately stiff, comparable to Scots Pine but less stiff; durable heartwood
- **Growth speed:** Fast — up to 90 cm/yr; can reach 15 m in 15 years
- **Ecology / notable traits:** Sterile hybrid (Cupressus macrocarpa × Chamaecyparis nootkatensis), so no invasive seed spread; forms impenetrable windbreaks and screens; poor wildlife habitat (little food for native fauna); highly tolerant of salt, wind, and a wide pH range; notorious as a "neighbour dispute" tree due to rapid screening height gain.

## Combat flavor
The Juggernaut archetype fits perfectly: blistering advance speed but a shorter operational lifespan than ancient rivals. Its sterile hybrid nature (no offspring) could read as a "no respawn" mechanic — devastating when alive, gone for good when it falls.

## Living creatures
Leyland is a non-native sterile hybrid with a famously thin native-insect fauna — its wildlife value is almost entirely as dense evergreen shelter, especially for nesting birds and the smallest songbird in Europe.

- **Cypress aphid (Cinara cupressi)** — ~1000s per tree on infested specimens; the main herbivore that has actually adapted to Cupressaceae in Europe. [RHS cypress aphid](https://www.rhs.org.uk/biodiversity/cypress-aphid) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/cinara-cupressi.jpg)
- **Scale insects (Carulaspis spp., juniper scale)** — ~100s per branch on infested trees; common Cupressaceae pest. [Leyland cypress pests](https://hgic.clemson.edu/factsheet/leyland-cypress-diseases-insects-related-pests/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/carulaspis-juniper-scale.jpg)
- **Cypress tip moth (Argyresthia spp.)** — ~10–100 mined shoots per tree; small moths whose larvae bore into cypress tips. [Leyland cypress pests](https://hgic.clemson.edu/factsheet/leyland-cypress-diseases-insects-related-pests/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/argyresthia.jpg)
- **Spider mites (Tetranychidae)** — ~1000s per tree in hot dry summers, causing diagnostic bronze foliage. [Leyland cypress pests](https://hgic.clemson.edu/factsheet/leyland-cypress-diseases-insects-related-pests/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/tetranychidae.jpg)
- **Goldcrest (Regulus regulus)** — 1 nesting pair per mature tree typical; Europe's tiniest bird famously favours Leyland and other conifers for its tiny hammock-nest. [Forestry Journal: goldcrests in Leyland cypress](https://www.forestryjournal.co.uk/features/20230338.investigating-relationship-goldcrests-conifers-arborists/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/regulus-regulus.jpg)
- **Greenfinch / Dunnock / Wren / Robin (Chloris chloris / Prunella modularis / Troglodytes troglodytes / Erithacus rubecula)** — 1–3 nesting pairs per mature hedge tree; dense foliage is core suburban nesting habitat. [Wildlife Trusts Leyland cypress](https://www.wildlifetrusts.org/wildlife-explorer/trees-and-shrubs/leyland-cypress) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/chloris-chloris-prunella-modularis-troglodytes-troglodytes-erithacus-rubecula.jpg)
- **Wood Pigeon / Collared Dove (Columba palumbus / Streptopelia decaocto)** — 1–2 pairs per tree; build flimsy stick nests deep in the foliage. [Woodland Trust Leyland cypress](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/leyland-cypress/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/columba-palumbus-streptopelia-decaocto.jpg)
- **Roosting garden birds in winter** — ~10–50 birds per tree on cold nights; evergreen mass traps warmth (qualitative; no per-tree count in literature). [Woodland Trust Leyland cypress](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/leyland-cypress/)
- **Spiders, harvestmen, woodlice (Arachnida / Isopoda)** — ~100s per tree among the litter and bark crevices; chief food for the resident goldcrest. [Goldcrest diet](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/animals/birds/goldcrest/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/arachnida-isopoda.png)
- **Red Fox / Hedgehog (Vulpes vulpes / Erinaceus europaeus)** — 0–1 sheltering at the base in urban areas, using dense skirts as cover. [Woodland Trust Leyland cypress](https://www.woodlandtrust.org.uk/trees-woods-and-wildlife/british-trees/a-z-of-british-trees/leyland-cypress/) [pic](../../data/creature_pics/vulpes-vulpes-erinaceus-europaeus.jpg)
