Wood pigeons and collared doves are both canopy-roosting and canopy-nesting species frequently observed together in the dense evergreen foliage of x Cupressocyparis (Leyland cypress) in Amsterdam. Both species spend long periods sitting motionless in the canopy, making idle-only the dominant animation, with tree-flitter as a secondary for short movements between adjacent trees. Tree-canopy is the correct habitat; the bird category default movement of tree-flitter is demoted to secondary because the defining observation context for this combination is roosting in dense cypress foliage, not active flitting.
