Pear psyllids are jumping plant-lice that feed exclusively on Pyrus (pear) foliage and shoots, inserting their stylets into leaf tissue and secreting honeydew that attracts sooty mould. In Amsterdam ornamental pear trees (Pyrus calleryana and cultivars) line many streets and they host both Cacopsylla pyri and C. pyricola in summer. The nymphs are entirely sessile on leaves and shoots, while adults jump short distances within the canopy rather than flying any meaningful distance.
