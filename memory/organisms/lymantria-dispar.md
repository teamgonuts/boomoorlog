Gypsy moth larvae are voracious defoliators of Ostrya and many other broadleaved trees, feeding openly on leaves in the tree canopy during the day and descending to the bark to hide at night. In Amsterdam the species is a notifiable pest; outbreaks on hop-hornbeam are rare but significant defoliation events. Tree-canopy is dominant because feeding and most visible life stages occur in the foliage; tree-bark is secondary for the nocturnal bark-resting behaviour. The caterpillars barely move during feeding bouts — idle-only correctly captures this suspended-on-leaves animation.
