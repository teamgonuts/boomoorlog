Cerambycidae (longhorn beetles) are secondary colonisers that bore into the wood of stressed or dying Aesculus (horse-chestnut) trees, a situation increasingly common in Amsterdam where horse-chestnuts are weakened by leaf miner pressure and bleeding canker. Larvae spend one to several years tunnelling through sapwood and heartwood; adults emerge briefly to mate and lay eggs. The dominant life-stage in any tree at any given time is the sedentary larval borer, pinning this entry to tree-bark / idle-only; override from the insect default as a tree-feeding microorganism on Aesculus.
