This row covers the full Turdus guild associated with Nyssa trees in Amsterdam — including resident Blackbirds and winter-visiting thrushes. They are attracted to the dark berry-like drupes of Nyssa (tupelo) and other fruiting trees. The group splits its time between feeding in tree canopies and foraging for invertebrates on the ground. Both secondary tags reflect this dual-zone behavior that all Turdus thrushes share in Amsterdam's urban parks.
