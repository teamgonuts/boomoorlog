The Willow Beauty is a well-camouflaged geometrid moth that rests with wings spread on rough bark, relying on its grey-brown cryptic patterning to disappear against tree trunks and wooden fences across Amsterdam parks and gardens. Its larvae feed on a broad range of trees and shrubs including yew, privet, ivy, and various Rosaceae, so it can be found anywhere mature trees or dense shrubby garden hedging exists. Adults are nocturnal and come to light traps; during the day they are pressed flat against bark and essentially immobile. Tree-bark is the dominant habitat for its cryptic bark-resting adults; tree-canopy is secondary for the larval feeding stage. Movement is idle-only: it is the archetypal bark-resting moth.
