This grouping combines two large woodland butterflies whose larvae feed on Salix (Purple Emperor on willow, White Admiral on honeysuckle but often near willow). Adults rarely visit flowers — Purple Emperor is famous for ignoring blooms and instead descending to ground for minerals or perching high in willow canopies. Both spend most visible time aloft in tree canopy, so tree-canopy + flower-bobber (for general adult flight) better matches the dominant member, Apatura iris.
