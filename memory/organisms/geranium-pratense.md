Meadow Crane's-bill is a handsome perennial with large violet-blue flowers and deeply divided leaves, typical of Amsterdam's parks, cemetery verges, and damp meadow remnants. It flowers from June to September and is an important nectar source for bumblebees and other long-tongued insects. The beak-like seed capsules that give cranesbills their name eject seeds explosively when ripe. It prefers calcareous or neutral, moderately fertile soils, and in Amsterdam it is most often found in the richer sward of older parklands and maintained verges. The default ground-park placement is entirely appropriate.
