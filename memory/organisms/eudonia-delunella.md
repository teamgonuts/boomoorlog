Eudonia delunella is a small crambid moth whose larvae feed on mosses and liverworts growing on old walls, stone surfaces, and rooftops in urban areas — it is effectively a wall-specialist. The adult rests cryptically on the same mossy wall surfaces during the day. This is a genuine wall-and-roof organism; Amsterdam's older brick canal-side buildings, bridges, and stone walls provide ideal habitat.
