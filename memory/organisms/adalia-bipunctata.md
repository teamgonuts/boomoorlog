The 2-spot Ladybird is among the most familiar beetles in Amsterdam, found throughout the city in parks, gardens, and along tree-lined canals. Both adults and larvae are voracious predators of aphids, so they concentrate in the canopy and on the bark of trees and shrubs where aphid colonies build up. The default insect placement of flower-visitor does not fit: this species is an aphid hunter, not a pollinator. Adults overwinter in sheltered bark crevices and emerge in spring to climb into canopy foliage, where idle-only captures their slow crawling behavior and tree-flitter represents the short flights between adjacent trees. It is threatened in many parts of Europe by the invasive harlequin ladybird.
