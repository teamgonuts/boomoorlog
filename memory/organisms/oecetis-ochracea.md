Oecetis ochracea is a leptocerid caddisfly whose aquatic larvae live in still and slow-moving water, constructing portable cases from plant material on canal and pond bottoms. Adults emerge in summer and rest during the day on waterside vegetation close to their emergence site. As an aquatic insect the default flower-visitor / flower-bobber is wrong; water-edge captures both the adult's bankside resting behaviour and the connection to the aquatic larval habitat. Idle-only is appropriate for the motionless daytime resting posture on canal-edge reeds and willowherb in Amsterdam.
