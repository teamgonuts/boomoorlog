Elatobium abietinum (green spruce aphid) and Cinara pilicornis (large spruce aphid) are two major aphid pests of Norway spruce (Picea) found in Amsterdam's parks and avenues. Elatobium colonises the older needles of lower branches in dense, inconspicuous colonies that cause yellowing and premature needle drop; Cinara forms larger visible clusters on young shoots. Both are sedentary needle-feeders that move negligibly at sprite scale. Tree-canopy (the needle zones of the spruce crown) and idle-only are the correct overrides from the insect flower-visitor defaults.
