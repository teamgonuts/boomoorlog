Great Willowherb is a tall, robust perennial herb that grows almost exclusively in wet places — canal banks, ditch margins, pond edges, and other permanently damp or waterlogged ground throughout Amsterdam. Its strong association with water margins means the plant default of ground-park is replaced with water-edge, which better describes where it is reliably found in the city. The species spreads vigorously by rhizomes along canal banks and is one of the most conspicuous bankside plants in summer. Movement remains none as it is a static plant.
