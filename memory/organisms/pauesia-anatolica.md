Pauesia anatolica and related braconid parasitoid wasps are specialist parasitoids of aphids living on cedar (Cedrus) trees in Amsterdam. The adult wasps search the canopy for aphid colonies in which to lay their eggs, spending their active time within the tree canopy rather than visiting flowers. While adults have short flight bursts between searching bouts, the observation context is the parasitised aphid colony on the host tree. Tree-canopy and idle-only are the correct overrides, as these wasps are not flower-foragers but tree-canopy hunters whose presence is only detectable by the mummified aphids they leave behind.
