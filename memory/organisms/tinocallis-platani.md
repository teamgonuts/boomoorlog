Tinocallis platani and related woolly elm aphids colonize the upper surfaces of Ulmus leaves in large colonies, producing characteristic leaf curl and copious honeydew that drips onto surfaces below the tree — a familiar nuisance under Amsterdam's many elm street trees. Eriosoma species produce woolly galls on elm leaf margins. Both genera are confined to the canopy foliage and do not visit flowers. Tree-canopy is the correct habitat; idle-only replaces flower-bobber because aphid colonies are sessile feeders anchored to their leaf sites.
