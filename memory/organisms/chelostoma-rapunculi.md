Chelostoma rapunculi is a slender solitary bee strongly associated with bellflowers (Campanula species), from which it collects pollen almost exclusively. It is an oligolectic specialist, and in Amsterdam it turns up wherever wild or garden Campanula grows in parks and verges. Females nest in hollow plant stems and small pre-existing cavities. The default insect placement of flower-visitor and flower-bobber captures its foraging behaviour well, and no override is needed.
