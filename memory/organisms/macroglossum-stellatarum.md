Hawkmoths such as the hummingbird hawk-moth (Macroglossum stellatarum) and other sphingids are powerful hovering pollinators that visit the trumpet flowers of Catalpa and other nectar-rich plants in Amsterdam gardens and parks. While feeding they hover motionless at blooms — flower-bobber — before making fast direct transit flights — sky-looper — between flower patches. Flower-visitor is the dominant habitat because their activity is tightly coupled to blooms; tree-canopy is the secondary resting habitat for adults that roost by day in foliage. In Amsterdam they are mainly summer migrants and passage visitors, most frequent in warm years.
