The Field Grasshopper is one of the more heat-tolerant British and northwest European grasshoppers, found in dry, short-grassland patches in Amsterdam parks, dunes, and sunny roadside verges during summer. It stridulates loudly on warm afternoons and makes short, powerful jumps when disturbed, covering considerable ground between resting on bare earth or low stems. The insect default flower-visitor / flower-bobber does not fit: this is a ground-dwelling grass-feeding species with no meaningful flower association. Ground-park / idle-only captures the resting-and-stridulating posture; park-roamer as secondary reflects its short bounding hops across the sward.
