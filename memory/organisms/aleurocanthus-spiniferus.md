The spiny whitefly is an invasive sap-feeding hemipteran that has been spreading on Ailanthus and citrus in southern Europe and has reached the Netherlands as a quarantine pest. On Amsterdam's Ailanthus it produces honeydew and sooty mould on leaf undersides. Larvae are sessile; adults are weak flutterers but largely stay on the host.
