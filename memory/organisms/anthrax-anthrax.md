The Anthracite Bee-fly is a striking black bee-fly with a dense coat of dark hair and white wing patches; it is a cleptoparasite of solitary bees and wasps that nest in sandy or bare soil. In Amsterdam it is associated with sunny patches of bare ground in parks, sandy paths, and south-facing embankments where its hosts nest, and it basks motionless on warm soil or stone surfaces (idle-only dominant). Adults also visit flowers for nectar, but ground-perching is the more characteristic visible behaviour, so ground-park takes the dominant habitat slot and idle-only the dominant movement. Overridden from flower-visitor default because the species spends most visible time on warm bare ground, not on blooms.
