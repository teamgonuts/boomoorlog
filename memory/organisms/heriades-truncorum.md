The Large-headed Resin Bee is a small, cavity-nesting solitary bee found in Amsterdam's parks and gardens wherever composite flowers — particularly black-eyed Susan, cornflower, and other Asteraceae — are present to provide pollen. It collects pollen almost exclusively from composites and is highly flower-faithful, visiting the same flower patches repeatedly in short bobbing flights between blooms. It nests in pre-existing cavities in dead wood and hollow plant stems, placing resin plugs between cells — cavity nesting is placement-equivalent to tree-canopy per the taxonomy, but the observable adult behaviour is pure flower-visitor / flower-bobber.
