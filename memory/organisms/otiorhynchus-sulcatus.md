The Black Vine Weevil is a dull black, flightless weevil that is one of the most damaging horticultural pests in Amsterdam, with larvae attacking the roots of a very wide range of ornamental plants — rhododendron, Euonymus, strawberry, and many others — in parks and gardens. Adults are nocturnal, hiding in soil or leaf litter by day and emerging at night to feed on notched leaf margins of their host plants. Since it is completely flightless all movement is on foot, crawling slowly through the leaf litter and up stems; ground-park dominates as the larval soil habitat and adult daytime shelter, with tree-canopy secondary for its nocturnal leaf-feeding on shrubs. Idle-only is dominant for the resting daytime posture; park-roamer secondary covers the slow nocturnal foraging walk.
