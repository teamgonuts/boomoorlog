This aggregate covers aphid species — primarily Dysaphis sorbi and woolly aphids (Eriosoma) — recorded on rowan and whitebeam (Sorbus) trees in Amsterdam. Dysaphis sorbi colonises leaf undersides producing leaf curls, while Eriosoma species create the distinctive fluffy white woolly masses on bark and shoots. Both are sedentary feeders anchored to the plant surface; woolly aphids can occupy either bark or canopy zones but canopy is dominant for the observable leaf-curling species. Tree-canopy and idle-only override the insect defaults, as with all other aphid slugs in this batch.
