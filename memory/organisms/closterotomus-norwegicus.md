The Potato Mirid is a plant bug (Miridae) that feeds on the soft stems, flowers, and developing seeds of a wide range of herbaceous plants, including nettles, thistles, and potatoes. In Amsterdam it is found in parks, rough grassland, road verges, and overgrown urban corners wherever tall herbaceous vegetation grows. Adults are moderately mobile, moving between plant stems rather than true flower-visiting. The dominant habit is ground-park herbaceous vegetation with some movement between plants; flower-visitor is a weak secondary because it is found on flowering herbs, but it is a piercing-feeding bug rather than a true pollinator.
