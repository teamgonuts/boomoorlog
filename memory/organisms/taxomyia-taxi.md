The yew gall midge induces distinctive artichoke-like galls in the growing tips of Taxus shoots; the larva develops entirely within the gall in the canopy of the yew tree and overwinters inside it. Adults are tiny and short-lived, emerging to mate and lay eggs without any meaningful flower-visiting activity. Taxus is widely planted in Amsterdam as a hedge and ornamental tree in parks and cemeteries. Tree-canopy is the correct habitat because the galls form on the shoot tips in the foliage; idle-only replaces flower-bobber because the gall-bound larva and resting adult are both effectively motionless.
