This combined entry covers the goat moth (Cossus cossus) and clearwing moths (Synanthedon spp.) whose larvae bore through the wood and bark of Castanea and Salix in Amsterdam. Both species spend several larval years tunnelling inside the trunk, and adults rest briefly on bark surfaces after emergence before dispersing. The insect category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is overridden in favour of tree-bark / idle-only because the larval stage — the ecologically dominant phase — is entirely within the bark and wood, and the adult stage on-tree is also a resting posture. The goat moth is notable for the pungent goat-like odour of its larvae, which can sometimes be detected at infested willows along Amsterdam canals.
