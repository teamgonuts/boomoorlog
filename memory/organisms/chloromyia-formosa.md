The broad centurion is a striking soldier fly with metallic green and bronze iridescence; adults are regular visitors to umbellifers and other open flowers in parks and gardens across Amsterdam, where they bask and feed on pollen and nectar. Larvae develop in moist, decaying plant matter in soil. Despite being in family Stratiomyidae rather than Megachilidae or Apidae, the adults' flower-visiting behaviour matches the insect default of flower-visitor and flower-bobber well. No override is needed.
