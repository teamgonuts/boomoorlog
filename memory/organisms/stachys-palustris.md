Marsh Woundwort is a tall, hairy perennial herb of damp ground, growing characteristically along canal banks, ditch edges, and wet meadow margins throughout Amsterdam. Its preference for waterlogged or seasonally wet soil means water-edge is a more accurate dominant habitat than the plant-default ground-park, though it does also colonise moist patches in parks and rough ground. The pink-purple flowers on whorled spikes are popular with bumblebees in summer. It is rhizomatous and can form dense stands along canal margins, making it a visually distinctive bankside plant.
