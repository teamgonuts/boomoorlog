The Green-eyed Hawker is a striking dragonfly with brilliant emerald eyes and a triangular yellow marking on the abdomen, associated with well-vegetated, shallow, standing or slow-moving water in Amsterdam's nature reserves, larger park ponds, and canal margins with emergent reeds. Larvae develop submerged over two years in reed-fringed ponds and ditches, making the species strongly dependent on good water quality and aquatic vegetation. Adults are fast, tireless fliers that patrol long territorial circuits over water surfaces and adjacent reed-beds, pausing only to perch vertically in reeds or sunlit vegetation. Water-edge dominates for both adult patrolling and larval emergence zones, with water-body for the aquatic larval stage; sky-looper captures the sustained aerial patrol flight, and idle-only covers perched sunning behaviour.
