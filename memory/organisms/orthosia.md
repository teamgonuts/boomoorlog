Orthosia moths (Common Quaker, Hebrew Character, and their relatives in the family Noctuidae) are early-spring moths recorded on Salix in Amsterdam. Their caterpillars feed on the fresh foliage of willows and other broadleaved trees in the canopy during April and May. Adults are nocturnal and rest motionless on bark or foliage by day, while caterpillars also remain largely still while feeding. The default flower-visitor/flower-bobber tags do not apply to these foliage-feeding noctuids, so tree-canopy and idle-only are the correct overrides.
