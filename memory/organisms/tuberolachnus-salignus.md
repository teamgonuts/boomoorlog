Tuberolachnus salignus is one of the world's largest aphids, forming dense colonies on the bark of willow (Salix) stems and branches in Amsterdam's canal-side and park willows. Chaitophorus spp. feed on willow leaves in the canopy. Both are essentially stationary organisms — colonies cluster in one spot for days or weeks. Tree-bark is the dominant habitat for Tuberolachnus which prefers woody stems, with tree-canopy secondary for the leaf-feeding Chaitophorus component. Idle-only overrides the flower-bobber default since these aphids do not visit flowers.
