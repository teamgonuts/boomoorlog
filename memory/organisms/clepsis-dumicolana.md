Clepsis dumicolana is a small tortrix moth whose larvae feed in rolled or tied leaves of shrubs and low trees — blackthorn, hawthorn, and various Rosaceae — making it a canopy-edge insect in Amsterdam hedgerows and park shrub layers. Adults are relatively small, pale-straw to ochre moths that rest motionlessly on foliage during the day and fly at dusk. The insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is inappropriate: this species is not a flower visitor and spends most of its life cycle in leaf-rolled feeding shelters. Tree-canopy / idle-only reflects the larval feeding niche and the adult resting behaviour; ground-park is a secondary for occasional adults found resting low in vegetation.
