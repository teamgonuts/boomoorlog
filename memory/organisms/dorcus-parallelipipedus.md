The lesser stag beetle is a saproxylic beetle whose larvae develop inside the rotting heartwood of dead or dying ash (Fraxinus) stumps and roots in Amsterdam's parks. Adults are chunky, black beetles that are most often found clinging motionless to dead wood or stumps at ground level and on lower trunks. Tree-bark is the dominant habitat because the beetle is tightly associated with decaying wood surfaces, with ground-park as a secondary since adults are frequently found on the soil surface near decaying roots. Idle-only replaces flower-bobber as these beetles are essentially sedentary at the scale relevant to the animation system.
