Elm bark beetles bore directly into the bark and cambium of Ulmus trees, spending almost their entire adult and larval life beneath or on the bark surface rather than visiting flowers. In Amsterdam they are associated with the many surviving elm street trees, where the galleries they carve under the bark are the primary sign of their presence. They are also the main vector of Dutch elm disease fungus, making them ecologically significant beyond their feeding habits. The insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber does not apply; tree-bark captures their anchor point and idle-only reflects the minimal surface movement between gallery entrances.
