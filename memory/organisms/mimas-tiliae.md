The lime hawk-moth is one of Amsterdam's most familiar sphingids, whose large green caterpillars feed exclusively on the foliage of Tilia (lime/linden) — the dominant street tree of the city. Larvae live and feed openly in the canopy, hanging from twigs in a characteristic oblique posture and barely moving during feeding bouts. Tree-canopy is the correct dominant habitat; no secondary is needed since even resting adults settle in tree foliage or nearby walls. Idle-only captures both the near-motionless caterpillar and the perched adult moth; the moth's nocturnal flight is short-lived and not the primary visible state.
