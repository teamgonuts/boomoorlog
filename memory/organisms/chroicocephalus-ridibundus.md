The Black-headed Gull is the commonest and most urban-adapted small gull in Amsterdam, present in vast numbers on the IJ, the Amstel, park ponds, and across the city's built fabric throughout winter and in smaller numbers in summer. It loafs and rests on open water surfaces, drifting with currents, but also wheels in noisy flocks over the city, patrols market streets for scraps, and follows canal boats. Water-surface is the dominant habitat where it spends most of its resting time, while anywhere captures its true generalist urban character. Sky-looper covers the aerial circling behaviour common when the species is agitated or prospecting, while water-drifter covers stationary loafing. The default bird tag of tree-canopy / tree-flitter is entirely wrong.
