Orange Mullein is a tall biennial herb of dry, sandy, and disturbed soils, favouring roadsides, embankments, and open park edges where competition is low. In Amsterdam it appears mainly on the sandy river-dune soils along the IJ-corridor and in post-industrial disturbed sites. The large woolly rosette in year one gives way to a tall spike of orange-yellow flowers in year two that attract specialist mullein bees and hoverflies. The default ground-park / none pairing is correct for this rooted non-woody plant.
