Hedge Bindweed is a vigorous climbing perennial that scrambles through hedges, fences, and waterside vegetation across Amsterdam's parks and canal banks. It produces large white trumpet-shaped flowers through summer that attract pollinators. The default ground-park placement is appropriate as its roots anchor in vegetated soil even while its stems climb; the map sprite would show the plant at its ground origin. It is one of the commoner tall-herb species along the edges of Vondelpark and the Amstel river bank.
