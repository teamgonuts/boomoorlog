The water veneer is a semi-aquatic micro-moth whose larvae live submerged, feeding on aquatic plants including pondweeds (Potamogeton) and water-milfoil; wingless females never leave the water and mated males are found skimming across the surface. In Amsterdam it is recorded on canals and park ponds with abundant submerged vegetation. Water-surface is the appropriate habitat, and adults are effectively stationary or in very short flights just above the surface, making idle-only the best animation match. The insect defaults of flower-visitor and flower-bobber are entirely inapplicable.
