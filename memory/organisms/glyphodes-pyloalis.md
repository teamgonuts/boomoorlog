The mulberry leaf-webber is a crambid moth whose larvae spin silk webs around Morus foliage, feeding within the webbed nest in the canopy. Amsterdam has a small but growing population of Morus trees as ornamentals and this specialist moth has been noted on them. Larvae are entirely canopy-resident and sedentary within their web shelters, making idle-only the correct movement. The override from the insect default is warranted: this is a leaf-webbing tree specialist, not a flower-visitor.
