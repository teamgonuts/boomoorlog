This Metasequoia-specialist bark beetle (Curculionidae: Scolytinae) is a close relative of Phloeosinus aubei that bores into the bark of dawn redwood trees in Amsterdam. Like all scolytine bark beetles, it spends its entire life cycle — from egg laying through larval gallery construction to pupation — inside the bark, with only brief surface exposure during dispersal flights. Tree-bark is the sole relevant habitat class, and idle-only represents the typical invisible-in-bark lifestyle captured by tree observations.
