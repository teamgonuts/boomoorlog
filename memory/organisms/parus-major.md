Great tit and blue tit are among the most familiar birds in Amsterdam, observed year-round in parks, gardens, and street trees including Juglans. They forage actively through the canopy for caterpillars, insects, and seeds, moving frequently between nearby trees with short arcing flights. The default bird tags — tree-canopy habitat and tree-flitter movement — are a perfect fit for these small, agile canopy birds. Both species readily use nest boxes in Amsterdam, but their visible behavior is canopy-foraging and inter-tree flitting.
