Brown Knapweed is a perennial wildflower of dry to moderately moist meadows, park margins, and grassy road verges in and around Amsterdam. Its purple flower-heads are a key late-summer nectar source for bumblebees and butterflies. The ground-park default fits well, as it grows in established grassland rather than on bare paved ground. It is among the target species for Amsterdam's urban meadow restoration projects in parks and along cycling paths.
