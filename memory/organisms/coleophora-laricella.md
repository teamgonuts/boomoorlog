The larch casebearer moth is a tiny micro-moth whose larva mines individual larch needles from inside a portable silken case, spending the entire larval period on the needles of Larix without ever visiting flowers. In Amsterdam it is associated with the limited Larix stock and is detected mainly through the characteristic needle-mining damage. The insect category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is overridden because the organism is a strict foliage miner; tree-canopy and idle-only correctly place the sprite in the needle zone of the host larch and animate it as a near-stationary larval case.
