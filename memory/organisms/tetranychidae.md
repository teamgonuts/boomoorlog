Spider mites of the family Tetranychidae colonize the leaves of a very wide range of host trees — in this dataset including Cupressocyparis, Ginkgo, Gleditsia, Koelreuteria, Liquidambar, Metasequoia, Pterocarya, and Thuja — feeding by piercing leaf cells and causing characteristic bronzing or stippling. They are too small to be visible to the naked eye individually but their silken webbing and feeding damage on foliage are diagnostic. The arachnid default of anywhere / idle-only needs adjustment; tree-canopy is the correct habitat because all of these taxa are strict leaf-feeders in the foliage layer, and idle-only is retained because spider mites are effectively motionless at sprite scale.
