The hawthorn shieldbug feeds on the developing fruits and leaves of Crataegus and related rosaceous trees, and is regularly recorded on hawthorn hedges and isolated trees across Amsterdam parks. It is a slow-moving, sit-and-suck feeder rather than a flower-visiting pollinator, so the insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber does not fit. Adults overwinter and emerge to bask on sunlit foliage in spring. Tree-canopy + idle-only better captures its real on-leaf, mostly-stationary behavior.
