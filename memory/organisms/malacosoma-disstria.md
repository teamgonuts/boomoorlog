The forest tent caterpillar is a North American lasiocampid whose Dutch presence is limited to ornamental Nyssa (tupelo) plantings; it would be an introduced curiosity rather than an established pest. Like other Malacosoma species, the larvae feed communally in foliage and rest on bark in silken mats. Tree-canopy is dominant for the feeding stage; tree-bark is secondary for the resting silk mats. The caterpillars are slow-moving leaf-feeders best represented by idle-only. Any Amsterdam observation would be exceptional and likely on an isolated ornamental tree.
