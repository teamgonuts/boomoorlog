Bark-crevice springtails and psocids (bark lice) recorded on Ginkgo in Amsterdam occupy the fissured bark microhabitat, feeding on algae, lichens, and fungal spores on the bark surface. Psocids are tiny and move slowly and cryptically across bark; springtails in crevices show minimal net displacement at sprite scale. The insect category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is overridden because both groups are strictly bark-surface organisms. Tree-bark habitat places the sprite correctly on the trunk area of the host Ginkgo.
