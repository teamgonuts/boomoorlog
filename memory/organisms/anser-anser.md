The Greylag Goose is a large wetland bird that breeds and winters in Amsterdam's parks and waterways in large, partially habituated flocks. It rests and preens on water but spends a substantial part of each day grazing on short grassland in parks and canal banks, making it genuinely dual-habitat. Water-surface is dominant but ground-park captures the heavy grazing that visually defines the species in Vondelpark and Oosterpark. Movement alternates between slow water-drifting and leisurely park-roaming; the default tree-canopy / tree-flitter bird assignment is entirely wrong for this large goose.
