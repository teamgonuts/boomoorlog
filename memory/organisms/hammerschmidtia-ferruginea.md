The aspen hoverfly is a Red Data Book hoverfly whose larvae develop in wet decaying wood of Populus (aspen and related poplars), particularly in sap-runs beneath the bark. Adults emerge to visit flowers for pollen and nectar, making them a dual-habitat organism: the larval stage is tree-bark anchored while adults are flower-visitors. In Amsterdam they are associated with older Populus trees in parks with some decay. Tree-bark is dominant because the breeding microhabitat drives placement, with flower-visitor secondary for adult feeding. Accordingly idle-only (larvae in bark) dominates, with flower-bobber for adults.
