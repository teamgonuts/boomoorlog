The Common Froghopper is a small sap-sucking homopteran whose nymphs produce the well-known "cuckoo spit" foam on plant stems, while adults are capable of remarkable leaping. Nymphs feed on a very wide range of herbaceous plants and are a familiar sight in Amsterdam parks, roadsides, and rough ground from late spring. Adults are cryptically brown or patterned and sit motionless on plant stems and flower heads. Habitat overridden to ground-park (dominant) with flower-visitor secondary for adults found on flower stems; movement overridden to idle-only as adults sit still despite their jumping ability.
