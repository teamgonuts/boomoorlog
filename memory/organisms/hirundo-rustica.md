The barn swallow is an aerial insectivore that spends almost all daylight hours in continuous flight, hawking insects over open water, parks, and farmland; it rarely perches in open view and never forages on the ground or in tree canopies. In Amsterdam it is a common summer visitor seen sweeping low over canals and open park spaces. Sky-only and sky-looper replace the bird defaults of tree-canopy and tree-flitter entirely, as this species is functionally synonymous with aerial, never-landing movement in the simulation context.
