Limnephilus marmoratus is a caddisfly (order Trichoptera) whose larvae are entirely aquatic, building portable cases from plant fragments in still or slow-moving water — Amsterdam's canals and ponds are ideal habitat. Adults are moth-like and nocturnal, resting during the day on waterside vegetation, tree trunks, or walls near water (idle-only as adults). Habitat is overridden from the insect default: water-edge is dominant (adults rest at water margins; larvae emerge there) with water-body secondary for the larval aquatic phase. The flower-visitor default is inappropriate for a caddisfly with non-feeding adults.
