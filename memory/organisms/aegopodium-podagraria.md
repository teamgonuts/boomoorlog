Ground-elder is a vigorous rhizomatous perennial that colonises shaded and semi-shaded spots in Amsterdam's parks, canal-side verges, and building margins, often forming extensive carpets that exclude other ground flora. Its flat-topped white umbels flower in late spring and are freely visited by a wide range of hoverflies, beetles, and small bees. The plant spreads aggressively by underground rhizomes and is considered a difficult garden weed throughout the Netherlands.
