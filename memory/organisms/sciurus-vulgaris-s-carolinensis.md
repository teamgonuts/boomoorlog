This entry records squirrel activity in a single Ginkgo tree in Amsterdam, likely Grey Squirrel given the current Amsterdam population distribution. Squirrels use Ginkgo for its fruit (the nut inside the malodorous outer seed coat) in autumn, foraging in the canopy and on the ground beneath the tree. Tree-canopy is dominant as the feeding and travel phase; ground-park is secondary for the nut-caching and retrieval on park lawns. Tree-flitter and park-roamer cover the respective movement phases. The mammal default of ground-park / park-roamer alone omits the arboreal component, requiring the same override applied to other squirrel entries.
