Wood Pigeon, Magpie, and Carrion Crow are three of Amsterdam's most abundant and adaptable urban birds, all found around Pterocarya (wingnut) trees for seeds and insects. All three are true urban generalists that use pavements, rooftops, lawns, and trees interchangeably. The anywhere habitat tag fits this genuinely multi-context group; tree-canopy secondary captures their regular tree use for nesting and perching. Urban-walker leads movement since all three species regularly walk on paved and grassy ground; tree-flitter secondary captures canopy movement between perches.
