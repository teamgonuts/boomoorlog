The Bee Moth is a pyralid moth whose larvae are kleptoparasites in the nests of bumblebees and social wasps, feeding on stored pollen, honey, wax, and nest debris underground or in cavities. Adults are nocturnal and rest by day in vegetation near known bumblebee nest sites, typically in unmown grass, hedgerows, and park margins rather than visiting flowers. The default flower-visitor placement does not fit this species' biology: it is nest-associated, not bloom-associated. Adults fly from June to August and are attracted to light. Amsterdam observations are most likely near older parks and gardens with established bumblebee populations and suitable ground-nesting sites.
