The Common Rough Woodlouse is a terrestrial isopod crustacean — often mislabelled an insect in GBIF taxon groups — that thrives in damp, decaying organic matter across Amsterdam's parks, cemeteries, and urban gardens. It shelters under bark, rotting logs, leaf litter, and stones during the day, becoming marginally more active at night to feed on decomposing plant material. Although biologically a crustacean it is catalogued under the insect taxon group here; the category is kept as insect per the GBIF source. Ground-park is dominant because most sightings are under leaf litter and soil; tree-bark is a genuine secondary because it commonly hides beneath loose bark on fallen or standing dead wood. Movement is idle-only since its crawl is below the renderer's frame-rate threshold (folded in per taxonomy rules).
