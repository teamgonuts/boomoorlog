The Mistle Thrush is the largest European thrush and is particularly associated with mistletoe and berry-bearing trees like Ilex and Sorbus, which it guards aggressively. In Amsterdam it frequents large parks, defending berry-laden trees through winter. It also forages on open lawns for earthworms and large invertebrates, striding across the ground with an upright, bold posture. Tree-canopy leads as habitat for its berry-defending territory, ground-park secondary for its substantial ground foraging. Both movement tags capture its dual lifestyle.
