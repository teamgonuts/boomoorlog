This three-species group is observed on Corylus (hazel) in Amsterdam, a plant whose nuts are heavily exploited by all three mammals. Wood mice and bank voles forage primarily on the ground, caching hazel nuts in shallow burrows, while red squirrels (Sciurus vulgaris) spend significant time in the canopy leaping between hazel stems. Red squirrel is rare in central Amsterdam but present in larger suburban parks and Amsterdamse Bos. The dual habitat tags reflect the genuine split between ground-foraging rodents (dominant) and the arboreal squirrel, with tree-flitter as a meaningful secondary movement for the squirrel component.
