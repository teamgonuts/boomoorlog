Common Toadflax is a slender perennial with pale yellow snapdragon-like flowers that colonises dry, disturbed, and open ground throughout Amsterdam — roadsides, railway embankments, waste ground, and park margins. It spreads by both seed and rhizome and tolerates poor, calcareous soils, making it equally common on urban rubble and in sandy park edges. Ground-park is dominant but ground-urban is a genuine secondary because it is conspicuously found on pavement edges, railway track ballast, and urban brownfield sites. The plant default of none movement is correct.
