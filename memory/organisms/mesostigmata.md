These phoretic mesostigmatid mites ride on bark beetles (particularly Ips typographus) that breed under the bark of Picea (Norway spruce) and are carried passively into new galleries when their beetle host colonises a new tree. Their own movement is negligible — they cling to the beetle's body or walk slowly within the bark gallery — making idle-only the correct animation. Tree-bark is the dominant habitat because the entire life cycle occurs in the subcortical space. In Amsterdam this mite community appears wherever spruce bark beetles attack stressed ornamental spruces.
