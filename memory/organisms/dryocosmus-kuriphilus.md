The Oriental chestnut gall wasp is an invasive cynipid from East Asia now established in parts of the Netherlands, including Amsterdam where it attacks sweet chestnut (Castanea). It induces distinctive green-and-red galls on buds, leaves and catkins in the canopy, where the larva develops entirely concealed inside the gall tissue. Adults emerge in summer as tiny, inconspicuous wasps that mate and oviposit rapidly before dying; the visible manifestation is always the stationary gall in the canopy.
