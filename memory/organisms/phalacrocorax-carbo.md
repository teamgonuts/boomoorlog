The Great Cormorant is a large piscivorous bird that dives into Amsterdam's canals and the IJ to catch fish, then hauls out on jetties, canal walls, or low branches to dry its wings with a characteristic outstretched pose. Water-edge is the dominant habitat for map placement, with water-body as secondary because it spends significant time diving below the surface. The tree-canopy / tree-flitter default is entirely inappropriate; water-edge-stalker captures its slow patrol of canal edges, and idle-only reflects the prolonged wing-drying posture that is the species' most visible behaviour.
