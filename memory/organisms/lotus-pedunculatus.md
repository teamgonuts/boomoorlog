Greater Bird's-foot-trefoil is a larger, more robust relative of common bird's-foot-trefoil that is strongly associated with wet, marshy habitats — it grows at pond edges, canal banks, wet meadow margins, and in damp ditches. Unlike the common species it is rarely found in dry grassland. Water-edge replaces the plant default of ground-park to reflect its preference for permanently damp to waterlogged bankside ground in Amsterdam. Movement remains none.
