The scalloped oak is a medium-sized geometrid moth whose larvae feed on the foliage of oaks, hawthorn, blackthorn, and other broadleaved trees; adults rest by day in a characteristic leaf-mimicking posture pressed against bark or among dead leaves. In Amsterdam it occurs in parks with mature trees. Tree-bark replaces the insect defaults because adults spend daylight hours motionless on bark surfaces, not visiting flowers. Idle-only is the appropriate animation for a cryptically resting moth.
