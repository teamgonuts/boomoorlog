Maiden Pink is a low-growing perennial of dry, sunny grassland, road verges, and sandy park edges, producing bright pink flowers with toothed petals in summer. It is a ground-level forb that is firmly in the ground-park category; it has no aquatic or wall associations. In Amsterdam it is found in the drier, sun-exposed corners of parks and along the edges of cycle paths and verges where soil competition is low. The plant default (ground-park, none) is the correct assignment and no override is needed.
