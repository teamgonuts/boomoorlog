Spruce sawflies (Gilpinia hercyniae and relatives) are specialist defoliators of Picea, with larvae feeding gregariously on spruce needles in the canopy. In Amsterdam, isolated ornamental Picea in parks and gardens can host these sawflies, though outbreaks are rare in urban settings. The larvae are essentially sedentary on foliage and move only to adjacent needle clusters, making idle-only the correct movement tag. The override from the insect default places them in tree-canopy rather than as flower-visitors since they are strict conifer foliage specialists.
