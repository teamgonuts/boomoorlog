This grouping covers two very small birds — the Goldcrest and the Coal Tit — that are closely associated with conifers, particularly Larix in Amsterdam parks. Both species are restless canopy foragers, working through needle clusters hunting tiny insects and spiders, and making short flitting hops between branches and nearby trees at high frequency. The Coal Tit joins mixed tit flocks in autumn and winter, while the Goldcrest is an active year-round conifer specialist; both are well served by the bird default of tree-canopy / tree-flitter without any modification.
