Cherry blackfly (Myzus cerasi) forms dense, conspicuous black colonies on the curled shoot tips and leaf undersides of Prunus trees — ornamental cherries are among the most widely planted street trees in Amsterdam. The aphids remain stationary during feeding, with apterae virtually welded to the plant tissue, justifying idle-only movement. Tree-canopy is the dominant habitat because all feeding and colony growth happens in the upper leaf and shoot layer. The colonies are most spectacular in May–June on ornamental cherry avenues throughout the city, before natural enemies reduce populations.
