The Dwarf Cream Wave is a tiny pale geometrid moth whose larvae feed on low herbaceous plants and possibly algae on bark in damp, shaded habitats. Adults rest with wings flat against bark or dead wood in parks and garden edges. Like other small wave moths, the flower-visitor / flower-bobber defaults are inappropriate; ground-park is dominant for the larval foodplant context and tree-bark covers adult resting sites. Idle-only is the correct movement as this moth is cryptically still by day and only moves at night.
