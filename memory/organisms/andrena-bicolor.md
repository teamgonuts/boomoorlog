Gwynne's Mining Bee is a small solitary bee with two broods per year (spring and late summer), among the most frequently observed Andrena species in Amsterdam parks and gardens. Females nest in bare or sparsely vegetated soil patches in parks, lawns, and path edges, making ground-park a genuine secondary habitat for nesting activity alongside the dominant flower-visitor foraging tag. Adults visit a wide variety of flowers but show a preference for Ranunculus and Taraxacum. The movement profile keeps flower-bobber dominant with idle-only for resting at the nest entrance; no sky-looper transit is added as this species makes very short foraging trips.
