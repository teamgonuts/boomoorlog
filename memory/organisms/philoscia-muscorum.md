The Common Striped Woodlouse is a terrestrial isopod crustacean, not a true insect, and is recorded in the GBIF category row as taxon group 13 (Crustacea). It lives under leaf litter, bark, and damp logs in parks and gardens, placing it firmly in ground-park. Its movement is negligible at any sprite-resolution — it shuffles a few millimetres when disturbed, which folds into idle-only per the taxonomy note on slow-creep. The category is overridden from insect to other because woodlice are crustaceans; the habitat default for other (anywhere) is further overridden to ground-park since this species has a clear damp-litter preference.
