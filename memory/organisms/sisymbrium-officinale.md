Hedge Mustard is a tall, wiry annual mustard with small yellow flowers and slender upward-pointing seed pods, one of the most characteristic weeds of Amsterdam's pavements, building bases, and disturbed urban ground. It thrives on compacted, nitrogen-rich urban soil — appearing in pavement cracks, at the foot of walls, and on trodden path edges — making ground-urban the more accurate dominant habitat than the plant default ground-park. It is edible and was historically used as a remedy for voice problems (the "singers' plant"). Overridden to swap the dominant habitat to ground-urban reflecting its strongly urban ecology.
