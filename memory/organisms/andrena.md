Andrena mining bees are a diverse genus of solitary spring-active pollinators that nest in bare or sparsely vegetated soil and forage on early-blooming trees and shrubs — exactly the host list here: Amelanchier, Salix, Sorbus, Laburnum, Koelreuteria, Paulownia. They are abundant in Amsterdam parks, cemeteries, and unmown verges during March–May. The flower-visitor / flower-bobber default fits without modification.
