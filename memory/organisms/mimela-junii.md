Mimela junii is a shiny green scarab beetle whose larvae develop in the soil of lawns and grassy areas, feeding on grass roots in much the same way as the related garden chafer — it is a grassland and park-lawn species tied to vegetated, well-drained soil. Adults emerge in summer and are active at dusk, flying to deciduous trees and shrubs, but they spend most observable daylight hours resting on the ground or low herbage. The insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber does not fit a chafer beetle with soil-dwelling larvae and a brief adult flight period; ground-park captures the larval and resting habitat and idle-only the daytime stillness of the adult.
