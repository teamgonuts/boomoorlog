Giant Hogweed is a massive invasive umbellifer from the Caucasus that reaches 3–5 metres in height, established along Amsterdam's canal banks, park margins, and river-adjacent wasteground where it dominates the herbaceous layer. Its huge white flower plates in June–July attract extraordinary numbers of insects — beetles, hoverflies, wasps, and flies — making individual plants temporary biodiversity hotspots. Contact with its sap in sunlight causes severe phytophotodermatitis and the plant is subject to active management programmes in the Netherlands. Ground-park is the dominant habitat; water-edge is a genuine secondary because it colonises canal banks and water-adjacent disturbed ground preferentially.
