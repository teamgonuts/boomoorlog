The red-barred tortrix is a small leaf-rolling moth whose larvae feed inside rolled leaves of a wide range of broadleaved shrubs and trees including Prunus, Rosa, and Crataegus; adults rest flat against foliage or bark in the tree canopy during the day. It is not a flower-forager, so the insect defaults of flower-visitor and flower-bobber are replaced with tree-canopy and idle-only, which reflect where adults are reliably found in Amsterdam parks. The species is polyphagous on woody plants, making tree-canopy a stable dominant habitat.
