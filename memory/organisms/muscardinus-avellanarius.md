The hazel dormouse is a highly arboreal small mammal that climbs and forages among the branches of Corylus, Crataegus, and Prunus in Amsterdam's wooded parks, rarely descending to the ground except during autumn nut-gathering or hibernation. Tree-canopy is the dominant habitat because dormice move primarily through the shrub and tree layer; ground-park is secondary for ground-level foraging in autumn. Movement alternates between nimble canopy hops between branches (tree-flitter) and brief ground foraging bouts (park-roamer). The dormouse is a red-listed species in the Netherlands and any Amsterdam record would be exceptional, most likely from the Amsterdamse Bos.
