The Silver Y is an unusual noctuid moth in that unlike most of its family it is partially diurnal and actively visits flowers in daylight, hovering at blooms to feed on nectar with its long proboscis — a behaviour observed daily in Amsterdam parks on lavender, red valerian, and knapweed during summer migration pulses. It is a strong migrant arriving from southern Europe in variable numbers each year. The insect category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber happens to align with this moth's real behaviour, but the reason is specific: the Silver Y genuinely is a flower visitor in the same sense as a butterfly, not a generic insect.
