The Common Kingfisher is one of Amsterdam's most beloved canal birds, perching motionless on overhanging branches or posts above the water before making a vertical plunge-dive. Its entire ecology revolves around clear water edges — it needs a perch above water and a fishing zone below — so water-edge overrides the default tree-canopy convincingly. Movement is a mix of prolonged still-perching (idle-only) punctuated by position changes along the canal bank (water-edge-stalker), mirroring the Grey Heron combination. It is regularly observed along the Amstel, Singelgracht, and the older inner canals.
