Liquidambar styraciflua (sweetgum) produces extrafloral nectaries on its stems and petioles that attract honeybees and hoverflies seeking sugar outside of the flowering period. This Amsterdam street tree is increasingly planted as a climate-adapted species and its EFNs are actively exploited by pollinators visiting the canopy zone. The habitat is tree-canopy because nectary secretions occur on foliage and young stems rather than flowers, but flower-bobber movement fits the short flit-and-feed pattern these insects show between EFN sites. The override distinguishes this non-flower canopy foraging from the standard flower-visitor default.
