The hawfinch is a large, powerful finch recorded on Carpinus (hornbeam) in Amsterdam, where it exploits the hard nutlets with its outsized bill. It is a shy, upper-canopy species that moves between trees in short direct flights, fitting the bird category defaults of tree-canopy and tree-flitter well. In Amsterdam it appears mainly during autumn and winter passage, favouring parks and old avenues with mature hornbeam and cherry. The default tags are appropriate without modification.
