The larger Welsh wave (Venusia cambrica) and autumn green carpet (Chloroclysta miata) are geometrid moths whose caterpillars feed on Sorbus (rowan) foliage. Both are nocturnal fliers that spend the day resting in complete stillness on bark or foliage, beautifully camouflaged against the tree surface. Tree-canopy is dominant for larval feeding; tree-bark is secondary for daytime adult resting. Idle-only overrides the flower-bobber default — these moths rest motionless by day and their night flight is not represented in the animation system.
