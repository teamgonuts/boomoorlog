The Clouded Border is a geometrid moth with distinctive white wings boldly edged and blotched with dark brown, closely associated with willows and poplars — both abundant along Amsterdam's canals. Adults rest during the day with wings spread flat against leaves or bark (idle-only), and are remarkably well-camouflaged against dappled light through willow canopy. Larvae feed on Salix and Populus foliage in the tree canopy, making tree-canopy the dominant habitat with tree-bark as secondary for resting adult placement.
