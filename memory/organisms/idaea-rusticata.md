The Least Carpet is a small geometrid moth whose pale straw-and-brown wings make it near-invisible when resting against old masonry, bark, or dry plant stems in summer. In Amsterdam it inhabits warm, sun-exposed urban habitats — old walls, railway cuttings, and overgrown urban lots — where its larval foodplants (Galium, Rumex, and low herbs) grow. Resting adults are exclusively idle-only, sitting flattened against surfaces during the day. Wall-and-roof is the dominant habitat because it is most often found pressed against old brick and stone; ground-park is secondary for the larval foodplant zone.
