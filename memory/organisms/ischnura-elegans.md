The Common Bluetail is the most abundant and ubiquitous damselfly in Amsterdam, found along virtually any canal, pond, or slow-moving ditch with emergent vegetation. Adults perch on waterside plant stems — the distinctive blue tail tip making males easy to spot — then make short hunting flights over the water surface. Water-edge is the dominant habitat as they cling to reed stems and bank vegetation; water-surface captures their patrolling flights just above the canal. The idle-only movement represents the characteristically long, motionless perching bouts between hunts. Larvae are aquatic predators that develop over one or two seasons in submerged vegetation. Adults fly from May to September.
