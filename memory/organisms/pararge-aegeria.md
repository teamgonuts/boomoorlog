The Speckled Wood is a woodland-edge butterfly perfectly adapted to Amsterdam's park interiors and tree-lined canal paths, where males famously occupy sun-flecks on leaf litter or low foliage and spiral upward in territorial chases with rivals. It is one of the few Amsterdam butterflies strongly associated with shade rather than open flowers, and does not need nectar as an adult, relying instead on honeydew from aphids. Its movement alternates between sustained perching in a sun-fleck (idle-only) and short spiralling sorties between trees (tree-flitter), so both values are genuinely needed. The dominant habitat is tree-canopy for the adult's perching spots and larval grass tussock feeding; ground-park is secondary for sun-fleck perching on leaf litter.
