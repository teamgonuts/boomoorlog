Phytoptus avellanae, known as the big bud mite or hazelnut gall mite, infests the buds and developing shoots of Corylus (hazel) in Amsterdam, causing the characteristic abnormally swollen "big bud" symptom. These eriophyid mites are microscopic and spend their lives inside buds or beneath bud scales in the tree canopy, never descending to the ground or visiting flowers. The default arachnid tags (anywhere, idle-only) are partially overridden: idle-only stays, but anywhere is replaced with tree-canopy for precise placement on the host Corylus. The mites' entire ecology is bound to the aerial parts of their host tree.
