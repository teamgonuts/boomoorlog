The wood mouse (Apodemus sylvaticus) and the closely related yellow-necked mouse (A. flavicollis) are common small rodents in Amsterdam's Vondelpark, Amsterdamse Bos, and larger cemetery parks. They are associated here with Prunus (cherry / plum) whose fallen fruits and seeds form a major part of their diet in autumn. Both species are nocturnal ground-foragers, living in burrows or dense undergrowth and rarely visible; the ground-park habitat and park-roamer movement default are accurate. They occasionally climb low branches to reach fruit but this is incidental and does not warrant a secondary tag.
