The Camberwell Beauty is a large, striking nymphalid butterfly whose larvae feed gregariously on Celtis (hackberry) and Salix foliage, while adults are long-lived and frequently visit willow catkins and tree sap for nourishment rather than flowers. In Amsterdam it is a scarce but regular autumn migrant and occasional breeder on riverside willows. Tree-canopy is the dominant habitat because larvae feed in foliage and adults bask and roost on tree bark and branches; flower-visitor is secondary for adults seeking nectar and sap sources. Movement alternates between gliding flights between canopy trees (tree-flitter) and brief landings at food sources (flower-bobber).
