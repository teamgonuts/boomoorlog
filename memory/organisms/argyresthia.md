Argyresthia tip moths associated with Cupressocyparis and Thuja in Amsterdam are small tortricid-like moths whose larvae mine into the growing tips (shoots) of cypress and arborvitae, causing the characteristic brown dead-tip dieback visible on these trees across Dutch gardens and parks. The larvae live entirely within the shoot tissue and the adults, when present, rest cryptically on foliage. Tree-canopy is the correct habitat because both larval and adult stages are anchored to the tree's foliage tips rather than the bark or ground. Idle-only movement reflects the larval stage's complete immobility within the mine and the adult's resting behavior; this is a tree-feeding microorganism on Cupressocyparis/Thuja.
