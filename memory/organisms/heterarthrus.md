Heterarthrus and Fenusa are leaf-mining sawflies whose larvae feed between the leaf layers of Betula, creating blotch mines that turn leaves brown by late summer. Birch trees are among the most common urban trees in Amsterdam and these sawflies are widespread on them. As with all leaf-miners, the larvae are sessile within the mine and idle-only is the correct movement class. The override from the insect default anchors them correctly to tree-canopy rather than the flower-visitor default.
