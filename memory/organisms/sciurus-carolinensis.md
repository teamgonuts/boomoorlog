The Eastern Grey Squirrel has established itself in Amsterdam parks, particularly in areas with mature Quercus, Platanus, Aesculus, and Ulmus, where it is now the dominant squirrel in some locations. It is highly arboreal, leaping confidently between canopies and descending frequently to bury and retrieve food caches in park lawns, making tree-canopy dominant and ground-park secondary. Tree-flitter fits the arboreal phase well, and park-roamer captures the characteristic stop-start ground foraging. The mammal default of ground-park / park-roamer alone misses the equal arboreal importance; both habitat and movement need a two-value override.
