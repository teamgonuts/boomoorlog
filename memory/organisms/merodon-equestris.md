The Narcissus Bulb Fly is a stout, bumblebee-mimicking hoverfly whose larvae are internal parasites of narcissus, snowdrop, and bluebill bulbs. Adults are strong fliers that visit flowers for nectar, particularly in spring when their bulb-plant hosts are in bloom. In Amsterdam parks and gardens planted with narcissus, this species can be abundant in April and May. The bumblebee mimicry is convincing enough to fool casual observers. Default flower-visitor and flower-bobber tags fit the adult behaviour accurately.
