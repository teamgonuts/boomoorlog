Chalcidoid parasitoid wasps — primarily Chrysocharis and Pleurotropis species — are tiny natural enemies of the holly leaf miner (Phytomyza ilicis) and other leaf-mining insects on Ilex and Magnolia in Amsterdam. The adult females walk over leaf surfaces probing mines with their ovipositor to parasitise miner larvae within; their own larvae develop entirely inside the mine. At pixel-art scale they are effectively sessile within the canopy, their foraging movements too small to animate distinctly.
