Flesh flies (Sarcophaga spp.) are viviparous flies whose larvae develop in carrion, excrement, or rotting organic matter; adults are common on urban pavements, rubbish areas, sunlit walls, and any exposed organic material in the city. They are not flower foragers, so the insect defaults of flower-visitor and flower-bobber are replaced with ground-urban and urban-walker, reflecting the characteristic pattern of short walks across paved surfaces followed by pauses. The genus-level identification means this covers multiple sibling species, all with similar urban ecology.
