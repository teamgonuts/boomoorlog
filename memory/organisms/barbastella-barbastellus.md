This entry groups the barbastelle bat (Barbastella barbastellus), a rare and declining species in the Netherlands, with the common pipistrelle (Pipistrellus pipistrellus and relatives), which are abundant across Amsterdam foraging over canals, parks, and street-lit edges after dark. Both species are associated with Salix (willow) here, which provides roost sites in cavity-rich old willows along Amsterdam's waterways. Bats are aerial hunters that spend their active time in flight, making sky-only habitat and sky-looper movement the clear overrides from the mammal default (ground-park / park-roamer). They roost in tree cavities by day but their map presence should represent them in flight over open water and park polygons.
