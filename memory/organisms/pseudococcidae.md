Mealybugs are soft-bodied scale insects that settle in bark crevices and leaf axils of Catalpa, forming waxy white colonies that are essentially immobile once established. In Amsterdam they are occasional warm-season pests on ornamental Catalpa street trees, visible as woolly clusters on younger branches and bark. Because they feed anchored to the bark surface and do not visit flowers, the insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is entirely wrong. Tree-bark placement and idle-only movement correctly represent a sessile bark-sucking insect.
