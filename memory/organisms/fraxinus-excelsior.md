The Ash is one of Europe's most important broadleaved trees, historically widespread in Dutch parks, cemeteries, and streets. It is a fast-growing, light-demanding species with distinctive compound leaves and winged seeds (keys) that persist through winter. Amsterdam's ash population is under severe pressure from Ash Dieback (Hymenoscyphus fraxineus), a fungal disease causing crown dieback and mortality across the city.
