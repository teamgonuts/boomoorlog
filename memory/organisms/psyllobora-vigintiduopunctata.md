The 22-spot Ladybird is a tiny yellow-and-black species whose diet is unusual among ladybirds: it feeds almost exclusively on powdery mildew fungi rather than aphids, and is therefore found low on herbaceous plants, meadow vegetation, and umbellifers rather than in tree canopies. It is a frequent flower-head visitor in parkland and rough ground, where its mildew hosts grow on low plants. Its movement is best described as short bobs between nearby flower heads (flower-bobber dominant) with long idle-only resting periods on leaf surfaces. Overridden from the default flower-visitor/flower-bobber because the secondary habitat is genuinely ground-park rather than tree-canopy.
