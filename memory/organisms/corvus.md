This entry captures a crow and magpie roosting assembly recorded in Gleditsia (honey locust) in Amsterdam. Crows (Corvus corone / C. cornix) and Eurasian magpies (Pica pica) are Amsterdam's archetypal urban corvids, equally at home perching in tree canopies and foraging on pavements, lawns, and canal banks. Anywhere is the correct habitat because neither species has a single dominant microhabitat. The dual movement of tree-flitter and urban-walker reflects the roost-to-pavement commute these birds make daily; the bird category default of tree-canopy / tree-flitter is overridden to include the ground-foraging component that corvids demonstrably express.
