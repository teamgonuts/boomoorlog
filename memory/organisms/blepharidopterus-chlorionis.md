Blepharidopterus chlorionis is a plant bug (family Miridae) that feeds on Gleditsia triacanthos (honey locust), increasingly planted as a street tree in Amsterdam due to its salt and drought tolerance. This small green-brown bug feeds on leaf tissue and is found on the undersides of leaflets from early summer. Like most Miridae, it is cryptically colored and relatively sedentary on its host plant, making tree-canopy the correct habitat override and idle-only the appropriate movement class. This is a tree-feeding microorganism on Gleditsia; the insect default of flower-visitor does not apply to a specialized plant bug.
