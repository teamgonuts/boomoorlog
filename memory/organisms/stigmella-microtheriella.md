Stigmella microtheriella is a tiny nepticulid moth whose larvae mine the leaves of Carpinus (hornbeam), creating narrow sinuous galleries visible as pale trails through the leaf blade. The adult moths are minute, nocturnal, and rest motionless on leaf surfaces or bark during the day. Hornbeam is well-represented in Amsterdam parks and avenues, providing reliable habitat. Tree-canopy is the right class because the leaf-mining habit keeps the organism entirely in the foliage layer; idle-only reflects both the motionless adult and the slow creeping of the leaf-bound larva.
