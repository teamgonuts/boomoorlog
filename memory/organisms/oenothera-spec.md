Evening primroses are tall biennial or perennial herbs of disturbed sandy and gravelly ground, widely naturalised along Amsterdam's roadsides, rail embankments, dyke tops, and wasteland where their large yellow flowers open at dusk to attract moths. Multiple Oenothera species occur in Amsterdam, all sharing a preference for open, nutrient-poor, dry, and disturbed substrates rather than managed park lawns. Ground-urban is the dominant habitat reflecting the disturbed urban and peri-urban substrates where they are most frequent; ground-park is a secondary for drier sandy park edges and gravel paths. Movement is none.
