The Corn Poppy is an annual archaeophyte of disturbed, nutrient-poor soils — roadsides, park edges, building sites, and managed wildflower strips — that has been reintroduced extensively in Amsterdam's green corridors. Its brilliant scarlet petals are an important early-summer nectar source for bees and hoverflies, but the plant itself is stationary and rooted in the ground. The category-default ground-park / none assignment is accurate; the species is exactly the kind of ruderal herb these Amsterdam disturbed-soil habitats support.
