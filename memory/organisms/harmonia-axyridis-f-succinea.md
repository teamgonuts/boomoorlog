Harmonia axyridis f. succinea is one of the pale colour forms of the Harlequin Ladybird — biologically identical to the nominate form, differing only in elytral patterning. Its ecology in Amsterdam is the same: an aphid predator found abundantly in the canopies of linden, lime, and other deciduous street trees, moving slowly through foliage between aphid colonies. It gathers on walls, window frames, and tree bark in autumn for overwintering aggregations. The insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is wrong for this tree-canopy aphid predator, for the same reasons as the nominate form. Tree-canopy and idle-only are the correct tags.
