The Common Chiffchaff is one of the earliest spring migrants to arrive in Amsterdam, and its persistent two-note song is a fixture of the city's parks and tree-lined canals from March through October. It is a classic leaf-warbler, foraging almost exclusively in the canopy and mid-storey of deciduous trees and shrubs, gleaning small insects from foliage and making short aerial sallies between branches. Its movement pattern is exactly the tree-flitter archetype — constant short hops between branches and between nearby trees, rarely descending to the ground. The bird category default of tree-canopy / tree-flitter is a perfect fit.
