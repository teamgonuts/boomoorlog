Hoverflies are among the most important pollinators of Amsterdam's urban trees and flowering plants, hovering at the flowers of Acer, Tilia, Aesculus, and Magnolia in spring and summer and moving between blooms with characteristic hovering pauses. Lacewings share the flower-visitor niche as adults but are also predators of aphids in the canopy; their inclusion here is as co-observers on the same tree taxa. The insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is an accurate fit for the dominant syrphid behavior. Both groups are abundant across all green areas of Amsterdam throughout the warmer months.
