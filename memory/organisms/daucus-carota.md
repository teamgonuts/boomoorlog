Wild Carrot is a biennial umbelliffer with distinctive flat-topped white flower heads, often with a single dark purple floret at the centre, found in Amsterdam on dry, chalky or sandy verges, park meadows, and dike edges. It is the wild ancestor of the cultivated carrot, with a thin, woody, pale taproot. The compound umbel flowers from June to September and is a major resource for short-tongued insects including many hoverflies, small wasps, and beetles. When the seeds are set the umbel curls inward to form a characteristic bird's nest shape. The default ground-park placement suits the open, dry grassland and verge habitats where it is typically observed around Amsterdam.
