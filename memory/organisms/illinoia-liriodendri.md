Illinoia liriodendri is an aphid specialist on Liriodendron tulipifera (tulip tree) and Magnolia, feeding on the undersides of leaves in the canopy and excreting copious honeydew. Both Liriodendron and Magnolia appear in Amsterdam parks and as ornamental street trees, making this aphid a genuine urban Amsterdam species. Like all sedentary phloem-feeding aphids, it is idle-only in movement terms — colonies sit motionless on leaf undersides. The override from the insect default correctly places it in tree-canopy as a leaf-feeding specialist rather than a flower-visitor.
