Yponomeuta ermine moths are small white moths whose larvae are notorious for spinning dense communal silk tents over shrubs and small trees — most famously bird-cherry, spindle, and apple — entirely defoliating them during outbreaks visible across Amsterdam parks. Adults are nocturnal and rest during the day on bark or foliage, making them effectively stationary when observed. The caterpillar webs cling to tree-canopy and bark surfaces so tree-canopy is the dominant habitat with tree-bark as secondary. Resting adults warrant idle-only as the primary movement; the secondary flower-bobber covers the rare observation of adults at flowers at dusk.
