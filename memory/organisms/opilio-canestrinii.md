Opilio canestrinii is an invasive harvestman originating from the southern Alps that has spread rapidly through urban western Europe in recent decades, now very common in Amsterdam. Unlike many arachnids it is strongly synanthropic, favouring low vegetation, walls, fences, and the ground-level zone of urban gardens, parks, and building perimeters. Ground-urban is dominant over the arachnid default of anywhere because the species shows a clear preference for disturbed, built-up habitats; ground-park secondary covers its park margin presence. Idle-only fits: harvestmen sit motionless for long periods before making slow walks.
