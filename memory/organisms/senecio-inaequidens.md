Narrow-leaved Ragwort is an invasive South African neophyte that has spread rapidly across the Netherlands along railway lines, roadsides, and industrial sites, strongly favouring the dry, compacted, low-competition substrates of urban infrastructure. Its bright yellow daisy flowers appear almost year-round in mild winters, making it conspicuous on Amsterdam's pavement cracks, rail sidings, and tarmac margins. Ground-urban is the dominant habitat as it is fundamentally a pioneer of built environments rather than park vegetation. It can be mildly toxic to livestock but thrives unchecked in city environments away from grazing pressure. The default ground-park is retained as a secondary because it also occurs in neglected park margins.
