These hawthorn-feeding Lepidoptera caterpillars (lappet, lackey, vapourer, light emerald) live and feed directly on the foliage and bark of Crataegus trees growing in Amsterdam parks and street plantings. The larvae cling to branches and leaves, barely moving once settled on a feeding site, making idle-only the correct animation class. Tree-canopy is dominant because most species feed on foliage; tree-bark is added as secondary because egg batches and resting pupae are anchored to the bark surface. In Amsterdam these species are occasional visitors on ornamental hawthorn rows, most visible in spring and early summer when the caterpillars are active.
