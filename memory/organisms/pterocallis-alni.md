Pterocallis alni is a specialist aphid restricted to the undersides of Alnus leaves in Amsterdam canal-side and park plantings, where colonies feed on phloem sap from spring through summer. Like all tree-canopy aphids, colonies are sessile once settled — individuals may walk short distances between leaves but this movement is invisible at map scale. The secondary tree-bark tag covers the bark-overwintering phase when eggs are laid on Alnus twigs in autumn. The insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber does not fit a phloem-sucking leaf aphid, requiring an override to tree-canopy and idle-only.
