This entry covers bat species (Noctule, Leisler's, various Myotis) that roost in cavities and crevices of mature Castanea and other veteran trees in Amsterdam parks, emerging at dusk to hunt insects over open water and parkland. Tree-canopy is the dominant habitat placement because the roost is in the tree; sky-only is added for the nightly foraging flights over open ground and water. Sky-looper is the primary movement class — bats follow long curved aerial paths — with idle-only as secondary for the daytime roosting state hidden inside the tree. Amsterdam's park trees, especially older ones, host significant bat roost populations.
