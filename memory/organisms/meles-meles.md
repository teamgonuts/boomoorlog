The Eurasian badger is a nocturnal omnivore that digs setts in earthen banks and forages across parkland and woodland edges for earthworms, roots, and fruit — including fallen Prunus plums and cherries. In Amsterdam badgers occur at the city fringe, particularly in the Amsterdamse Bos where sett systems persist in the sandy wooded banks. Ground-park is the right habitat since their entire above-ground activity is on vegetated soil. Park-roamer fits their slow nocturnal shuffle across park polygons, pausing frequently to root in the ground.
