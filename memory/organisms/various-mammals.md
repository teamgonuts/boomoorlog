Mice, voles, foxes, and badgers are all ground-dwelling mammals that use Amsterdam's parks, gardens, and canal-side green spaces. They forage beneath fruit trees like Malus for fallen fruit, seeds, and invertebrates. The ground-park default fits all members of this heterogeneous group — all require vegetated soil habitat and move through it by walking or running. Park-roamer captures the random-walk foraging pattern common to all four species in Amsterdam's urban green network.
