Wall Barley is an annual grass of disturbed, nutrient-rich urban ground: pavement cracks, road verges, bases of walls, and waste places throughout Amsterdam. Its common name reflects its preference for urban habitats rather than managed park grassland, so ground-urban replaces the plant default of ground-park. The species is one of the most characteristic plants of Amsterdam's streets and is particularly abundant along canal-side pavements. Movement remains none.
