Dasineura pyri is a tiny gall midge whose larvae cause the characteristic inward rolling of pear leaf margins on Pyrus trees. The insect spends its larval and pupal stages entirely hidden within the curled leaf tissue in the canopy. Adult midges are extremely small and fragile, emerging briefly in spring to mate and oviposit but otherwise imperceptible during any visible window. Tree-canopy is the correct placement since all observable signs — the rolled, discoloured leaves — are in the canopy; idle-only replaces flower-bobber as there is no animated movement meaningful at sprite scale.
