This combined entry groups the wood mouse (Apodemus sylvaticus) and bank vole (Myodes glareolus) found on Cornus, Crataegus, and Ostrya in Amsterdam. Both species are small nocturnal ground mammals that shelter under shrubs and fallen wood, foraging on seeds, berries, and invertebrates. Bank voles are especially associated with scrubby undergrowth near water, while wood mice range through park interiors. Neither species is meaningfully arboreal despite their association with fruiting trees; ground-park placement and park-roamer movement are the accurate defaults. Their tree associations here reflect seed-predation on fallen fruit from Cornus and Crataegus rather than canopy use.
