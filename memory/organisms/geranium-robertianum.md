Herb-Robert is a small, strongly scented annual or biennial cranesbill that thrives in shaded, disturbed ground: it is a familiar sight at the base of walls, in cemetery corners, under hedges, and in the deep shade beneath canal-side trees in Amsterdam. It colonises gaps in tree-root zones and base-of-wall rubble (hence tree-bark as a secondary habitat — it literally grows pressed against wall and bark surfaces). The delicate pink flowers attract small solitary bees in summer. Ground-park is the dominant placement as it needs soil, but the secondary tree-bark/wall association is biologically real and visually distinctive.
