Hawthorn is a thorny deciduous shrub or small tree with white blossom in spring and red haws in autumn, widely planted in Amsterdam park hedgerows, cemetery borders, and scrubby urban green space. Although it can grow into a tree, it is far more frequently encountered as a shrubby hedging plant on the ground-park layer and is catalogued as a plant rather than a tree in this roster. Its dense thorny growth provides nesting cover for birds and its flowers and berries are valuable wildlife resources. The plant defaults of ground-park and none apply without modification.
