The Foxglove is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial with distinctive tubular purple-pink (occasionally white) flowers arranged on a long spike, poisonous throughout and the original source of digitalis heart medicines. In Amsterdam it is found in parks, woodland edges, and gardens, typically favouring slightly acidic, well-drained soils in light shade or woodland clearings.
