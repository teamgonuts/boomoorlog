The Lesser Black-backed Gull is a large, highly adaptable gull that breeds on Amsterdam rooftops and forages across the entire urban fabric — canals, parks, streets, markets, and rubbish sites — making it one of the city's most genuinely omnipresent birds. It soars on thermals above open water and city squares, and descends to walk and peck on pavements and waterfront areas with the characteristic pause-and-stride gait of larger gulls. The default bird tag of tree-canopy / tree-flitter is entirely inappropriate; anywhere captures its urban-generalist nature, with water-surface as a secondary reflecting regular loafing on the IJ and canal surfaces. Sky-looper covers its aerial searching, urban-walker its ground-foraging.
