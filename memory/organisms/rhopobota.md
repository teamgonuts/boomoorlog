This grouping covers two moth species associated with Ilex aquifolium in Amsterdam: Rhopobota naevana (holly tortrix), whose larvae spin and roll holly leaves in the canopy, and Acasis viretata (yellow-barred brindle), a geometrid whose caterpillars feed on holly flowers and foliage. Both are canopy-bound in their larval stage and rest cryptically on foliage as adults, barely moving at map scale. They are leaf- and flower-tissue feeders rather than flower visitors in the pollinator sense, so the insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber does not apply. Tree-canopy and idle-only are the correct override.
