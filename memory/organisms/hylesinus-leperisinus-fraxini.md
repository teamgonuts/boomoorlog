Hylesinus and Leperisinus fraxini are bark beetles that bore into the bark and cambium of Fraxinus ash trees to breed, creating distinctive D-shaped exit holes and gallery systems. Amsterdam has extensive street plantings of Fraxinus excelsior and F. pennsylvanica, and these bark beetles (along with the ash dieback pathogen Hymenoscyphus fraxineus) are significant threats to the urban ash stock. Adults are short-lived and rarely seen in flight; placement on tree bark is definitive and movement is idle-only for the sedentary bark-boring life stage. The override from insect default is necessary given their strict bark-dwelling ecology.
