The Small Magpie is a crambid moth whose larvae feed on nettles (Urtica dioica), making it strongly tied to nettle patches in park margins, canal banks, and waste ground. The adult rests motionless during the day on nettle leaves or nearby vegetation, only flying at dusk. Despite its striking black-and-white pattern it does not feed at flowers as an adult and is not a pollinator, so the default flower-visitor / flower-bobber pairing is wrong. Ground-park with idle-only captures its low-profile, ground-level resting life in Amsterdam's nettle-rich park edges.
