Auplopus carbonarius is a spider-hunting wasp (Pompilidae) that paralyses spiders and provisions mud-pot nests built in wall crevices, mortar gaps, or other sheltered cavities in urban environments. It forages actively on the ground and low vegetation hunting for spiders, and the female is often seen running erratically across pavements and park paths. Adults also visit flowers for nectar. The default flower-visitor/flower-bobber partially fits for adults but the key additional behaviour is active ground-hunting and wall-nesting, warranting a wall-and-roof secondary habitat and urban-walker secondary movement.
