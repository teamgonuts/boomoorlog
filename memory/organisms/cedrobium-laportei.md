Cedrobium laportei is a specialist woolly aphid that feeds on the new growth and needles of Cedrus (cedar) trees, producing conspicuous white waxy wool on shoots. Cedrus atlantica, C. libani, and their cultivars are planted as ornamental trees in Amsterdam parks and institutional grounds. The aphids are sedentary colonies on the current-year shoots and do not migrate to an alternate host, remaining on Cedrus throughout the year.
