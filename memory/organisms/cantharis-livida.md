Cantharis livida is a soldier beetle (Cantharidae) that is genuinely a flower visitor: adults are commonly found resting on umbellifers and other open flowers where they feed on pollen and small invertebrates. Unlike moths in this batch it does belong on flowers in season, so a secondary flower-visitor tag is biologically warranted. However, it also rests motionless on foliage and stems for long periods. Ground-park is dominant as its broader habitat (it overwinters in soil and leaf litter), with flower-visitor secondary; idle-only is dominant movement with flower-bobber secondary when blooms are available.
