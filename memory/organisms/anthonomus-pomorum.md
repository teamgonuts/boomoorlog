The apple blossom weevil lays eggs inside unopened Malus flower buds; the larva develops inside, killing the bud and leaving the characteristic capped-blossom symptom on ornamental and wild apples in Amsterdam parks and orchards. Although the adult does visit flowers briefly, the visible life of the species is on apple buds and shoots. Tree-canopy + idle-only better reflects its anchored-on-host behavior than the generic pollinator default.
