Nematinae sawflies — especially the Pontania gallers — induce distinctive bean-like leaf galls on Salix (willow) species growing along Amsterdam's canals and in wetland parks. The larvae develop entirely enclosed within the gall tissue, making them invisible and completely immobile at the sprite scale; adults are short-lived and rarely observed on the tree. Tree-canopy is the sole habitat since the galls form on leaves. Idle-only is correct: the most visible sign of these insects is the static gall on the leaf surface, which barely changes day to day.
