This entry covers Lasius and Formica ants observed specifically on the extrafloral nectaries of Ailanthus altissima (tree of heaven) and Liquidambar styraciflua in Amsterdam. Both trees are widespread urban species — Ailanthus is abundant on disturbed urban ground and Liquidambar is increasingly planted as a climate-adaptive street tree. Ants exploit the sugar secretions on stems and leaf petioles within the canopy zone rather than at ground level, making tree-canopy the correct dominant habitat. Their crawling on bark and stems is below animation threshold, so idle-only applies. The override is needed because EFN-tending ants are canopy-anchored, not flower-visitors.
