The double-striped pug is a small geometrid moth with two generations per year; larvae feed on the flowers and seed capsules of a wide range of plants including gorse, heather, and various herbaceous species. Adults rest motionless by day on foliage and bark, blending with the background. In Amsterdam it is recorded in parks and gardens with diverse shrubby vegetation. Tree-canopy and idle-only replace the insect defaults because adults spend daylight hours in cryptic rest on vegetation, not actively visiting flowers.
