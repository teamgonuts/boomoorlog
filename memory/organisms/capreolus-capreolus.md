Roe deer are the smallest native deer of Western Europe and occasional visitors to Amsterdam's larger green spaces, including the Amsterdamse Bos, where they browse on understorey shrubs and low branches of trees such as Pterocarya (Caucasian wingnut). They are active mainly at dawn and dusk and follow meandering paths through park vegetation, matching the mammal default of ground-park / park-roamer precisely. Roe deer do not typically venture into the dense urban core and sightings near city-centre Pterocarya would be exceptional, but the tag is appropriate for the Amsterdamse Bos context.
