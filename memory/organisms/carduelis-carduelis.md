The European Goldfinch is one of Amsterdam's most colourful year-round residents, nesting in parks and tree-lined streets and feeding on the seeds of Alnus, Liquidambar, Ulmus, and Zelkova — all well represented in the city's street-tree roster. Goldfinches move in small, twittering flocks between tree tops, characteristically clinging to thin seed-bearing branches and flitting short distances from crown to crown. The small-songbird default of tree-canopy / tree-flitter fits perfectly without any override needed.
