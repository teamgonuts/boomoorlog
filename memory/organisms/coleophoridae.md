Case-bearer moth caterpillars (family Coleophoridae) recorded on Cornus in Amsterdam are leaf-mining or leaf-fastening larvae that carry a portable case and graze foliage from a fixed position. They are firmly anchored to the host leaf surface and do not travel across the canopy, making idle-only the fitting movement class. The insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is overridden because case-bearers are foliage microhabitat specialists, never flower feeders. Tree-canopy habitat correctly resolves the sprite to the leafy layer of the Cornus host.
