The cypress aphid is a large, grey-brown phloem-feeding aphid that colonises the bark of Chamaecyparis, x Cupressocyparis, and Thuja in Amsterdam parks and garden streets. Colonies cluster tightly on stems and branchlets, barely moving except to probe with stylets, so idle-only is the correct animation. The insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber does not apply here; these aphids never visit flowers and spend their entire lives anchored to bark tissue. Tree-bark as the dominant habitat places them correctly on the trunk and branch areas of the host sprite.
