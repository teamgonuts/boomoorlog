The white wagtail is a characteristic bird of open urban and peri-aquatic habitats in Amsterdam: it forages by walking rapidly along pavements, car parks, and rooftops, as well as patrolling canal banks and water margins for invertebrates. Its constant tail-pumping walk is one of its most recognisable features. Ground-urban is the dominant habitat because it is most often observed on hard, open surfaces in the city; water-edge is secondary for canal-bank foraging. The bird default of tree-canopy and tree-flitter does not apply at all to this ground-walking species.
