This slug represents the same guild of armoured scales and mealybugs observed on Nyssa trees in Amsterdam. Nyssa (tupelo) is an uncommon ornamental in the city and the scales found on it behave identically to those on other genera: they are bark-anchored, sedentary sap-feeders that never move at a scale meaningful for animation. Tree-bark placement and idle-only movement are the appropriate overrides from the insect defaults of flower-visitor and flower-bobber, consistent with the diaspididae slug above.
