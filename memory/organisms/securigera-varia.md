Crown Vetch is a sprawling, scrambling perennial legume with attractive pink-and-white bicoloured flowers, originally from southern Europe but widely naturalised in Amsterdam on road verges, railway banks, and park edges. It fixes atmospheric nitrogen via root nodules, allowing it to thrive on poor soils and outcompete native flora in disturbed habitats. The flowers are visited by bumblebees and other long-tongued bees from June through August. Its vigorous rhizomatous spread means it can form extensive ground-covering patches in amenity grassland and urban green margins. The default ground-park placement is appropriate for most Amsterdam sightings.
