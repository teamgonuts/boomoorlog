The carrion crow (Corvus corone) and its sibling taxon the hooded crow (Corvus cornix) are the dominant large corvids of Amsterdam, recorded here on Juglans (walnut). These birds exploit almost every urban habitat: they roost in tree canopies, forage on pavements, lawns, canal banks, and rubbish, and cache food on rooftops. Anywhere is the correct habitat class, and the dual movement of tree-flitter and urban-walker captures the between-tree hops and the deliberate pavement stalking that characterise daily crow behaviour. The bird category default of tree-canopy / tree-flitter is overridden because the ground-foraging component is too significant to omit.
