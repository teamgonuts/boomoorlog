Phyllonorycter esperella and similar gracillariid leafminers are recorded on Carpinus betulus (hornbeam) in Amsterdam. Their larvae live inside the hornbeam leaf, forming the blotch mines that identify this family, remaining completely enclosed within the leaf tissue until pupation. The default flower-visitor and flower-bobber insect tags have no relevance to these concealed leafminers, so tree-canopy and idle-only are used instead. Carpinus-associated leafminers are often among the most abundant insects by biomass on hornbeam street trees, though individual larvae are tiny and invisible without leaf-dissection.
