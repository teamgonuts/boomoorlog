This combined entry covers honey bees and bumblebees observed on Pterocarya (Caucasian wingnut) in Amsterdam. Pterocarya is a large riparian tree sometimes planted along Amsterdam's waterways; its catkins offer pollen to early-season generalist bees. Both Apis and Bombus are common flower visitors across Amsterdam's urban green spaces, moving rapidly between flowering trees and shrubs. The flower-visitor habitat and flower-bobber movement class are the correct defaults for these pollinators: their map placement should snap to nearby flowering tree sprites and animate with short inter-flower bobs.
