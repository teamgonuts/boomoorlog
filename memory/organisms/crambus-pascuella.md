Crambus pascuella is a grass moth (family Crambidae) whose larvae feed on grass roots in park lawns, meadow margins, and grassy verges — exactly the habitat where adults are also flushed when walking through the turf. Adults rest in characteristic head-down posture on grass stems, wings rolled tight around the body like a cigarette. Ground-park is the clear dominant habitat for both larval and adult stages. The default flower-visitor / flower-bobber profile is entirely wrong; idle-only captures the crypsis-while-resting strategy correctly.
