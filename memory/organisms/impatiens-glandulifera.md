Himalayan Balsam is a tall, fast-growing annual from the Himalayas that has naturalised aggressively along Amsterdam's canal edges, river banks, and ditch margins. It forms dense stands up to two metres tall that shade out native waterside vegetation. The large pink-purple flowers are a rich nectar source for bumblebees from July to October. Its explosive seed pods are distinctive and audible in late summer. water-edge overrides the default ground-park because this species is almost exclusively a riparian coloniser, rooted at the margins of water bodies. Movement is none.
