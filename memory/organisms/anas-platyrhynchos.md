The Mallard is the archetypal Amsterdam canal duck, found on every waterway from the IJ to the smallest park pond throughout the year. It spends the majority of its time swimming and dabbling on the water surface, drifting along canals with the current in the water-drifter pattern. It is also one of the most confident urban foragers in the city, walking onto canal towpaths, pavements, and park paths to beg for bread — a behaviour that makes ground-urban a genuine secondary habitat. The taxonomy lists the Mallard explicitly as a multi-habitat example of water-surface + ground-urban / water-drifter + urban-walker, and this is the correct tagging. The default bird tag of tree-canopy / tree-flitter is wrong.
