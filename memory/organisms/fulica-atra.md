The Eurasian Coot is a defining waterbird of Amsterdam's canal and pond network, spending most of its time swimming and diving on open water across the city's parks and urban waterways. It regularly hauls out onto bank lawns and grassy verges to graze, making ground-park a genuine second habitat. On water the coot drifts with canal currents and paddles steadily, while on land it walks with the lurching gait characteristic of rail family members. The species is highly visible year-round and nests in emergent vegetation along water edges. The secondary park-roamer movement captures its frequent bank grazing, following the Coot example given explicitly in the taxonomy.
