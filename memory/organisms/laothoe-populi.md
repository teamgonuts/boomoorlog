The poplar hawk moth is a large sphingid moth whose larvae feed exclusively on Populus and Salix foliage in the canopy, resting motionless during the day on branches or leaves with their cryptic bark-grey wings. Adults are crepuscular and nocturnal, flying over tree canopies in long looping paths to locate mates and host trees. Tree-canopy is the dominant habitat for both larval feeding and adult daytime roosting, making it the correct anchor. Idle-only is dominant (capturing the motionless daytime resting posture that would be the primary visible state), with sky-looper secondary for nocturnal adult flight. The override from the insect default is necessary as this is a tree-canopy specialist rather than a flower-visitor.
