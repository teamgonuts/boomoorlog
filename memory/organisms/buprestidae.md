Wood-boring beetle larvae (jewel beetles and longhorns) tunnel beneath the bark and into the sapwood of weakened or stressed trees, including Celtis street trees in Amsterdam. The larvae spend most of their life cycle as sedentary borers inside the tree, emerging as adults only briefly; in a pixel-art simulation context the bark-boring stage is the dominant visible state. The override shifts them from the insect default of flower-visitor to tree-bark, and movement is idle-only because the burrowing itself is imperceptibly slow. Adults are very short-lived and rarely the focus of observations in Amsterdam.
