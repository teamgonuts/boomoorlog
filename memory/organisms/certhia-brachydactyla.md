The Short-toed Treecreeper is entirely bark-dependent: it creeps upward in spirals along tree trunks and major branches probing for invertebrates in bark crevices, then drops to the base of the next tree. The tree-bark habitat override is clear-cut — no other placement makes sense. Its movement is best rendered as slow idle-only creeping up a trunk with occasional short flights to the next tree (tree-flitter secondary), matching the Treecreeper example given in the taxonomy documentation. It is regularly recorded in Amsterdam's mature urban parks wherever old linden, oak, and plane trees stand.
