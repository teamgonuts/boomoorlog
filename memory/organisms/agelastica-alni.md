The alder leaf beetle is a small metallic-blue chrysomelid that skeletonises alder leaves; outbreaks are very visible on canal-side and park alders across Amsterdam. Adults and larvae are both leaf-bound feeders rather than flower visitors. Tree-canopy + idle-only captures their on-leaf existence better than the pollinator default; adults can short-fly between alders but most of the visible life is at rest on foliage.
