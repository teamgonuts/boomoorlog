The Lesser Redpoll is a small finch that winters in Amsterdam parks and tree-lined canals, often in flocks feeding on alder and birch catkins. It clings acrobatically to slender twigs in the upper canopy and flits short distances between trees, exactly matching the small-songbird default. Around the city it is most easily seen in Vondelpark, Oosterpark, and the Amsterdamse Bos where mature Alnus and Betula stand together. The standard tree-canopy / tree-flitter combination fits without modification.
