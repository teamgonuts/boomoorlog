Chionaspis gleditsiae (and related armoured scale species) infest the bark of Gleditsia triacanthos (honey locust), a popular Amsterdam street tree chosen for its tolerance of urban stress and compacted soils. Adult females are permanently sessile beneath flat, white, elongate scale covers on bark and small branches, drawing sap; infestations cause twig dieback and bark discolouration.
