Apoidea is the superfamily encompassing all bees (honey bees, bumblebees, solitary bees), recorded here on Cornus (dogwood) in Amsterdam. Cornus mas and C. sanguinea flower early in spring, offering some of the season's first pollen to queen bumblebees and early-flying solitary bees. Amsterdam's diverse urban planting provides year-round foraging niches for bee communities. The flower-visitor placement and flower-bobber movement are the appropriate category defaults for this superfamily grouping: all member species are most meaningfully placed at flowering trees and animated with inter-bloom transits.
