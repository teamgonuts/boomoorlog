The Eurasian Treecreeper spirals methodically up tree trunks and large branches, probing bark fissures for invertebrates with its slender decurved bill, then drops to the base of the next tree and repeats. In Amsterdam it is most reliably seen on large mature conifers such as Cedrus and Taxus in Vondelpark and larger cemeteries. Its entire foraging life is spent on bark surfaces, making tree-bark the correct dominant habitat; movement is best described as idle-only in animation terms because the slow spiral creep is below the threshold for a separate movement class.
