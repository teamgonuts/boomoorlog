The Goldcrest is Europe's smallest bird and a conifer specialist strongly associated with Cedrus, Chamaecyparis, Metasequoia, Picea, Taxus, and Thuja in Amsterdam parks and private gardens. It forages ceaselessly through dense needle canopies hunting micro-insects and spiders, making rapid short flits between branches every few seconds. Amsterdam records show it present year-round in mature conifer stands in Vondelpark, the Hortus Botanicus, and larger private gardens.
