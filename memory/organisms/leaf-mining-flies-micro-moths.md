This entry captures agromyzid fly larvae and various micro-moth caterpillars that produce leaf mines in Laburnum foliage, visible as pale blotch or linear trails on the leaflets. Laburnum anagyroides and L. x watereri are planted as ornamental trees in Amsterdam parks and private gardens, and leaf-miners frequently exploit them. As with all leaf-mining insects, larvae are entirely immobile within the mine tissue and idle-only is definitive. The override from the insect default correctly places them in tree-canopy rather than flower-visitor.
