Callipterinella and Symydobius species are specialist aphids that colonise Betula (birch) catkins, twigs, and undersides of leaves, producing large honeydew deposits that make birches sticky in summer. Amsterdam's many Betula pendula street trees and park trees in Oosterpark and Vondelpark host dense colonies of these aphids from spring through early autumn. They are entirely sedentary within the canopy, overriding the insect default; both genera are tree-feeding microorganisms on Betula, so tree-canopy / idle-only is the correct tag pair.
