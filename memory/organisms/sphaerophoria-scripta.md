The Common Globetail is one of Amsterdam's most abundant and readily-identified hoverflies — a slim, wasp-mimic syrphid with yellow-and-black banding. Adults are avid flower visitors throughout the season, hovering over and nectaring on umbellifers, composites, and open flowers across parks, gardens, and canal-side planting. The insect defaults (flower-visitor, flower-bobber) apply exactly. Larvae are aphidophagous in plant stems. It is present in Amsterdam from April through October and is typically the commonest hoverfly in any urban flower survey.
