The Box Bug is a medium-sized squash bug (Coreidae) strongly associated with box (Buxus sempervirens) but also feeding on hawthorn, dogwood, and other woody shrubs in parks and gardens. It has expanded its range northward in recent decades and is now regularly recorded in Amsterdam on ornamental shrubs, hedge plantings, and park shrubbery. Adults and nymphs sit motionless on stems and leaf surfaces, feeding by piercing the plant tissue.
