Gracillariid and nepticulid leaf-mining moths have larvae that tunnel between the upper and lower epidermis of Ostrya leaves, leaving characteristic blotch or linear mines visible to observers. Ostrya carpinifolia (hop-hornbeam) is a relatively rare street tree in Amsterdam but does appear in specialist plantings where these micromoths follow. The mining larvae are entirely immobile within the leaf tissue, making idle-only definitive. The override from the insect default correctly anchors them to tree-canopy foliage rather than flowers.
