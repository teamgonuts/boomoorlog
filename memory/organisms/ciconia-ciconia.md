The White Stork is a large migratory bird that passes through and occasionally breeds near Amsterdam, foraging in open grasslands, park meadows, and wet fields for large invertebrates and small vertebrates. In the Amsterdam context it is most often seen in open grassy areas such as Amsterdamse Bos and surrounding polder meadows rather than dense tree canopy. Water-edge is a genuine secondary habitat as it also wades in shallow water and damp margins. The park-roamer animation captures its slow, deliberate walking gait through open green. It returns from its African wintering grounds in late March and departs by August or September.
