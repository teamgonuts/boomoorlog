Cecidophyopsis psilaspis is a microscopic eriophyid mite that infests Taxus (yew) buds, causing them to swell into distinctive round galls — the "big-bud" symptom also known as yew bud mite infestation. Taxus is common in Amsterdam churchyards, formal gardens, and cemetery plantings. Thousands of mites live within a single galled bud; they are entirely sedentary at any scale visible to the naked eye.
