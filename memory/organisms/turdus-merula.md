The Common Blackbird is one of Amsterdam's most abundant and familiar birds, present year-round in every park, garden, and street with trees. It spends a majority of its time on the ground — flipping leaf litter, pulling earthworms, and foraging beneath Amelanchier, Cornus, Prunus, and Sorbus. Ground-park is the dominant habitat because Blackbirds are quintessential ground foragers; tree-canopy is secondary for roosting and berry feeding. Park-roamer is the dominant movement, with tree-flitter secondary for canopy movement between fruiting trees.
