This entry covers the assemblage of geometrid caterpillars — primarily winter moth but also related species — recorded on Ostrya carpinifolia (hop-hornbeam) in Amsterdam. These caterpillars feed in the leaf canopy during spring and early summer, moving only slowly between feeding sites. They have no flower-foraging role and do not visit the ground during their larval phase, making the default insect tags inappropriate. Idle-only is correct because caterpillars spend most daylight hours stationary, relying on camouflage rather than movement for survival.
