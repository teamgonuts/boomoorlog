The Common Reed Warbler is a summer migrant strongly tied to dense reedbed habitats along Amsterdam's waterways, canals, and lake margins. It builds its distinctive basket nest woven around reed stems just above the water surface, rarely venturing far from emergent vegetation. The tree-flitter animation works well to represent its rapid, agile movement between reed stems and waterside willows. Its loud, churring, repetitive song is a hallmark of Amsterdam's reed-fringed waters from May to August. Placing it at water-edge correctly represents both its reedbed breeding habitat and the canal margins where it is most frequently observed.
