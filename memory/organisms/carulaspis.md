Carulaspis scale insects and accompanying tetranychid spider mites colonise the bark and scale-like foliage of Chamaecyparis (Lawson cypress) planted in Amsterdam gardens and urban hedges. Adult scale females are permanently fixed under their waxy shells on bark and branchlets; spider mites spin fine webs on foliage and are for practical purposes stationary at pixel-art scale. Override from the insect default to tree-bark / idle-only as a tree-feeding microorganism on Chamaecyparis.
