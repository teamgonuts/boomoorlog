Oligia minors are small noctuid moths whose larvae bore into the stems of grasses and sedges at the base, making them essentially ground-level insects of park meadows, rough grassland, and reed-edge habitats in Amsterdam. Adults are compact, grey-brown moths that rest pressed flat against bark or stems during the day and fly at night. The insect default flower-visitor / flower-bobber is not appropriate for a grass-stem borer: ground-park / idle-only fits the larval microhabitat and the resting adult posture, with tree-canopy as secondary for adults that occasionally rest on tree trunks or low woody stems.
