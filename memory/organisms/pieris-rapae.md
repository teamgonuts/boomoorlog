The Small White is the most abundant white butterfly in Amsterdam, present in multiple generations from March to October across parks, allotments, and canal-side plantings. Adults are active nectar feeders visiting a wide range of flowers, while larvae feed on brassicas and Tropaeolum (nasturtium), which are common in Amsterdam's community gardens. Its flight between flowers is the classic flower-bobber pattern — purposeful short hops between blooms with occasional wider transits. The insect category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is correct for this species.
