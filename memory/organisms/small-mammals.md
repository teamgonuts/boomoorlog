This composite entry covers the small mammals most commonly associated with Morus and other fruiting trees in Amsterdam parks: urban foxes foraging beneath them, grey squirrels and red squirrels climbing into the canopy for fruit, and wood mice gleaning fallen mast from the ground. The dominant habitat is ground-park because foxes and mice are predominantly ground-level; a secondary tree-canopy tag captures the squirrel component that genuinely climbs. Park-roamer is the right movement class for the fox and mouse element and adequately represents the group; squirrel acrobatics can be folded into the same category given the small sprite scale.
