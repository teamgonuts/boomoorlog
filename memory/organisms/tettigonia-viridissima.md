The Great Green Bush-cricket is a large, predatory katydid that lives in tall herb margins, shrubs, and the lower canopy of trees in parks and urban green corridors. It is not a flower feeder: adults are partly predatory and partly herbivorous, spending most of their time sitting motionless in vegetation waiting to ambush prey or gnaw plant matter. The default flower-visitor / flower-bobber insect pairing is wrong. Ground-park is dominant (tall herbaceous vegetation and shrub margins), tree-canopy secondary (it regularly climbs into lower branches); idle-only captures the cryptic, still resting posture that makes it so hard to spot despite its size.
