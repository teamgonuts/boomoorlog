The Smoky Wainscot is a noctuid moth whose larvae feed on grasses and reeds, making it strongly associated with damp grassland, fen edges, and reed-bed margins along Amsterdam's canal and polder fringes. Adults are pale straw-coloured with indistinct streaking, resting pressed against grass stems during the day where they are nearly invisible. The species is attracted to light traps in large numbers in July and August. The insect default flower-visitor / flower-bobber is wrong for a grass-feeding moth: ground-park / idle-only reflects the adult resting behaviour in grassland; water-edge is a genuine secondary because it is particularly associated with reed beds and wet ditchside grass in Amsterdam's polder edges.
