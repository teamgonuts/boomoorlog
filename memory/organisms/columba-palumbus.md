This combined entry covers wood pigeons and roosting blackbirds and other passerines observed in the dense canopies of Cercidiphyllum, Chamaecyparis, Cornus, Ilex, Liquidambar, Taxodium, Thuja, and Zelkova across Amsterdam. The common observation context is roosting birds settled in dense foliage, making idle-only the dominant movement animation; tree-flitter is the secondary for when individuals move between adjacent trees. Tree-canopy is the correct habitat for all species in this group since the primary observation is roosting in the leafy layer, not ground-foraging. The default tree-flitter movement is demoted to secondary because the roosting focus makes idle-only the dominant animation.
