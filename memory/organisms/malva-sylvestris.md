Common Mallow is a robust, deep-rooted biennial or short-lived perennial typical of Amsterdam's urban margins: roadsides, pavement bases, waste ground, and sunny wall bases. Its large pink-purple veined flowers are attractive to bees and produced prolifically from June through October. Ground-urban better captures its preference for the disturbed, ruderal habitats of the built environment rather than managed parkland. The edible leaves and medicinal reputation have kept it near human settlements throughout history. Amsterdam observations cluster along sunny south-facing road edges and in community garden surrounds where soil disturbance is frequent.
