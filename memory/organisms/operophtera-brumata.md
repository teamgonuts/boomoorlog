Winter moth and mottled umber caterpillars are early-spring defoliators observed across a wide range of Amsterdam street trees including Amelanchier, Malus, Platanus, Prunus, Pyrus, and Quercus. The larvae hatch in sync with bud burst and feed exclusively in the canopy, looping slowly between leaves but never descending to the ground to forage. The default insect category tags (flower-visitor, flower-bobber) do not apply since these are foliage-feeding caterpillars, not pollinators. Idle-only captures the near-stationary caterpillar posture during the day, when they rest motionless on leaves to avoid predation.
