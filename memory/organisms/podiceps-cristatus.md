The Great Crested Grebe is a diving waterbird that is a regular presence on Amsterdam's larger park ponds, the Amstel, and the IJ margin, famous for its elaborate courtship dances in early spring. It spends the majority of its time on or in open water, diving repeatedly to pursue fish under the surface; water-body is the dominant habitat as it spends more time under water than at the surface when actively foraging. Between dives it rests and drifts on the surface (water-drifter), and when loafing between foraging bouts it sits virtually motionless (idle-only). The default bird tag of tree-canopy / tree-flitter is completely wrong; grebes are foot-propelled diving birds that cannot walk on land.
