The Common Crossbill is an irruptive finch that descends on Amsterdam in invasion years to exploit the cone crops of Larix and Pinus in the Amsterdamse Bos and Vondelpark conifers. It spends virtually all its time in the upper canopy, twisting cones open with its crossed bill, and moves between nearby conifers in short darting flights — a classic tree-flitter pattern. The default bird tags of tree-canopy and tree-flitter are an excellent fit. Crossbills are irregular visitors: absent in most years, then suddenly present in noisy flocks when continental cone crops fail.
