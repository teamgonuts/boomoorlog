The Heart and Dart is a common noctuid moth in Amsterdam, one of the most frequently recorded macro-moths at light traps across the city from May to August. Its larvae are typical noctuid cutworms, feeding at soil level on the roots and basal stems of a wide variety of herbaceous plants in parks and verges. Adults rest during the day on the ground, leaf litter, or low bark, entirely motionless and relying on the cryptic brown-and-black wing pattern for concealment; they only fly after dark. The insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is wrong for a ground-resting cutworm moth; ground-park and idle-only accurately reflect its daytime ecology in Amsterdam's parks and green corridors.
