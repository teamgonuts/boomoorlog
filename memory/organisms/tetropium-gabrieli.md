The larch longhorn beetle lays its eggs under the bark of weakened or recently felled Larix, and the larvae bore extensive galleries through the cambium and sapwood of the trunk. Adults emerge through round exit holes in the bark and rest on the bark surface or nearby timber; they do not visit flowers. Larix is a common planted conifer in Amsterdam parks including the Amsterdamse Bos. Tree-bark is the correct habitat class because the entire larval development and adult emergence is anchored to the bark surface; idle-only replaces flower-bobber as the adult's activity is limited to resting and short searching flights close to the host tree.
