The pine marten is a highly agile arboreal mustelid that climbs and leaps through the canopy of Pinus and Sorbus in woodland parks, using branches as travel routes and hunting sites. In Amsterdam it is an extreme rarity; occasional records come from the Amsterdamse Bos where mature pines exist. Tree-canopy is the dominant habitat because martens spend more time aloft than on the ground; ground-park is secondary for nightly terrestrial hunting forays. Movement alternates between tree-flitter (leaping between canopy trees) and park-roamer (ground-level hunting at night).
