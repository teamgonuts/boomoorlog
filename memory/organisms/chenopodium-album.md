Lamb's Quarters is one of Amsterdam's commonest annual weeds of disturbed, nutrient-enriched ground: allotment edges, building sites, pavement margins, and rubble dumps. As a classic nitrophilous ruderal it colonises freshly turned bare soil far more readily than established park turf, making ground-urban the dominant habitat. It also volunteers on the margins of park meadow areas and community gardens, so ground-park applies as a secondary. The mealy-white leaf surface is a recognisable field-mark in summer.
