The Wren, Dunnock, and Robin are common small passerines in Amsterdam parks, gardens, and woodland edges. All three forage close to the ground or in low shrubby vegetation, with the Robin and Dunnock particularly known for hopping along the ground beneath hedges and in leaf litter. While they perch in tree canopy and understorey, they regularly descend to park ground to hunt invertebrates. The ground-park secondary habitat captures their frequent ground-level foraging, and park-roamer secondary movement reflects their low-hopping style, distinguishing them from pure canopy flitters.
