Purple Loosestrife is a tall, magenta-flowered wetland perennial that lines the margins of Amsterdam's canals, ponds, and ditches, thriving with its roots in saturated or seasonally flooded soil. It is one of the most visually striking waterside plants in late summer and a major nectar source for bumblebees. The default ground-park placement is too dry; water-edge is the biologically correct dominant habitat. Ground-park serves as secondary for populations on damp park meadow edges away from open water.
