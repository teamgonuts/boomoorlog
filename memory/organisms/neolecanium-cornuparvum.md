Magnolia scale is one of the largest soft scales in Europe and a conspicuous pest of Magnolia trees in Amsterdam parks and botanical collections, forming glossy dome-shaped brown bodies directly on the bark of twigs and branches. The settled scale insects are entirely immobile — they fuse to the bark surface and feed by suctioning phloem sap through a fixed stylet. Tree-bark is the dominant habitat since the scales live on the woody stem surface rather than the leaf layer. No movement animation beyond idle-only is appropriate. The species is readily identified on ornamental magnolias in summer by the distinctive waxy bumps and honeydew drip beneath.
