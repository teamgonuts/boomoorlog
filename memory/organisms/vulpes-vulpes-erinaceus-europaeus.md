The Red Fox and Western Hedgehog are two of Amsterdam's most-watched urban mammals. Both are crepuscular/nocturnal animals that forage through parks, gardens, and canal-side green strips. They are associated with Cupressocyparis hedges (Leyland cypress), which provide dense cover for shelter and nesting. Ground-park fits both perfectly — they require soil and vegetation rather than paved surfaces. Park-roamer captures the wandering foraging style both species use as they patrol territory through Amsterdam's fragmented green network.
