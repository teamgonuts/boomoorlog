Hemp-agrimony is a tall perennial herb that grows almost exclusively in wet habitats — canal banks, pond margins, fens, and damp ditches. In Amsterdam it is a characteristic plant of water-edge environments and is one of the most important late-summer nectar sources for butterflies and bumblebees. Water-edge replaces the plant default of ground-park to reflect its obligate preference for damp, waterside ground. Movement remains none as it is a static plant.
