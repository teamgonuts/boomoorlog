Vespula (common and German wasps) and their parasitoid wasp associates are opportunistic predators and scavengers found across Amsterdam. Associated with Koelreuteria (golden rain tree), whose insect fauna provides prey for parasitoid wasps. Social Vespula visit flowers for sugar and hunt caterpillars in tree canopies; parasitoid wasps locate hosts in foliage and bark. Tree-canopy is dominant as the foraging zone; ground-park secondary captures ground-nesting Vespula and soil-pupating prey. Movement combines flower-bobber for nectar visits and sky-looper for the active hunting flights.
