The Eurasian Jay is one of Amsterdam's most characteristic park birds, strongly associated with Quercus, Aesculus, Juglans and Pterocarya — all mast-producing trees from which it harvests and caches nuts. Jays are conspicuous in the canopy during foraging bouts but spend substantial time on the ground in parks searching for cached acorns or gleaning fallen nuts, making a dual habitat tag essential. Tree-flitter captures their bold canopy hops between adjacent trees, while park-roamer reflects their deliberate ground walks between caching sites. The override from the bird default is necessary given the jay's distinctly bimodal canopy-ground lifestyle.
