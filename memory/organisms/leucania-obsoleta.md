The Obscure Wainscot is a noctuid moth whose larvae are specialist feeders on Common Reed (Phragmites australis), boring into the stems, making it tightly associated with reedbeds and canal margins throughout Amsterdam. Adults are straw-coloured, cryptically matching reed stems, and rest immobile on reed stalks during the day. The flower-visitor/flower-bobber defaults do not apply at all; habitat overridden to water-edge (dominant, for its reedbed association) with ground-park secondary for migration stops; movement overridden to idle-only.
