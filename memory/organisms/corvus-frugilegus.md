Rooks (Corvus frugilegus) and carrion crows together in Platanus (plane tree) avenues are a characteristic winter sight along Amsterdam's canal streets and in parks like the Vondelpark. Rooks nest colonially in tall trees and commute to short grassland and pavements to probe for invertebrates, while carrion crows are year-round urban generalists. Anywhere correctly covers both species' promiscuous habitat use; tree-flitter and urban-walker together capture the tree-roosting and pavement-foraging that alternate throughout the day. The bird category default is overridden because the ground-foraging component is essential to both species' ecology in the city.
