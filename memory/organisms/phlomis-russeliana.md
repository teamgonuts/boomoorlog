Turkish Sage is a robust ornamental perennial of Mediterranean origin, widely planted in Dutch public parks and road-side beds for its architectural whorled yellow flowers and large felted leaves. It is a ground-level herbaceous plant with no canopy or aquatic habitat component. In Amsterdam it appears commonly as a designed planting in park borders, cycle-path medians, and traffic-island beds, where its dried seed heads persist through winter and provide food for seed-eating birds.
