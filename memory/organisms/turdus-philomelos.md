Song Thrush and Mistle Thrush are medium-sized thrushes associated here with Amelanchier (serviceberry), whose small berries they consume eagerly. Both species hunt snails on the ground and are famous for using stones as anvils to crack snail shells — a definitive ground-park behavior. Tree-canopy leads as habitat because berry feeding in the canopy is the primary attractant to Amelanchier, but ground-park is essential as a secondary. Tree-flitter captures movement between fruiting trees and park-roamer the anvil-stone ground foraging.
