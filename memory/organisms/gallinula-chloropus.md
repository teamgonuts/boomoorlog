The Common Moorhen is a rail family bird abundant along Amsterdam's canals, in park ponds, and in any vegetated water margin across the city. Unlike the Coot it is more secretive and tied to emergent vegetation at the water's edge rather than open water, so water-edge is the dominant habitat. However it also ventures well into adjacent park lawns and verges to pick for food, making ground-park a genuine second habitat. Its movement along canal banks is a slow, nodding walk characteristic of water-edge-stalker, while its forays onto grass are covered by park-roamer. The default bird tag of tree-canopy / tree-flitter is entirely wrong for this ground-and-water species.
