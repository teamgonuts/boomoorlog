Ichneumon xanthorius is a parasitoid wasp that parasitises moth pupae; adults are commonly found on umbellifer flowers in parks and verges where they feed on nectar to fuel flight. Unlike the moths in this batch the adult does visit flowers as an energy source, warranting flower-visitor as a secondary habitat. Ground-park is dominant because the wasp hunts low in park vegetation for host pupae rather than being canopy-tied; idle-only is dominant movement (it perches and searches methodically) with flower-bobber secondary during nectar-foraging bouts.
