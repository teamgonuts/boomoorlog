Dune Ragwort is a subspecies of Common Ragwort adapted to dry, sandy soils — historically restricted to coastal dunes but increasingly found in Amsterdam on the sandy embankments, rail-side verges, and park edges derived from old dune material. Its bright yellow composite flower heads are an important late-summer nectar source for the Cinnabar moth and various pollinators. The default ground-park / none pairing holds; it is a rooted perennial of open ground that needs no habitat override.
