Eriophyes gall mites are microscopic arachnids observed on hawthorn (Crataegus) in Amsterdam, where they induce various leaf-surface galls and erinea (felt-like patches of abnormal leaf hairs). These mites are far too small to see individually; their presence is indicated by the visible gall structures on leaves in the canopy. The arachnid default of anywhere and idle-only is partially correct in movement but wrong in habitat — tree-canopy is far more specific and accurate for these strictly leaf-surface mites. Habitat is overridden from anywhere to tree-canopy; movement idle-only is retained from the default.
