Atteva aurea, the ailanthus webworm moth, is a brightly colored ermine-like moth native to tropical America that has spread into Europe alongside its secondary host Ailanthus altissima (tree of heaven). In Amsterdam, Ailanthus colonises disturbed urban sites and wasteland; the webworm moth larvae feed communally in silk webs on Ailanthus foliage through summer. The adults are day-active and striking — orange with white and black spots — but rest on foliage rather than foraging at flowers. Tree-canopy is the correct habitat override because larvae live and feed in canopy webs; idle-only reflects their sedentary larval feeding behavior. This is a tree-feeding organism on Ailanthus.
