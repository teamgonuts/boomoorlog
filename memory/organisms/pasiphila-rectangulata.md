The Green Pug is a delicate geometrid moth with fresh green patterning that fades with age, associated with apple, pear, and other rosaceous trees in gardens and parks. Larvae feed on the flowers and developing fruitlets of apple, hawthorn, and related trees, making urban orchards and ornamental fruit trees the core habitat. Adults fly in June and July and rest by day on bark or leaves within or near the host tree canopy. The default flower-visitor placement is incorrect; this is a tree-canopy species dependent on rosaceous foliage and flower structures. Amsterdam observations typically come from garden moth traps near fruit trees and hawthorn hedges.
