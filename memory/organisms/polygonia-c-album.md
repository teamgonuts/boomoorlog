The Comma is a distinctive butterfly with ragged, notched wing edges that provide superb dead-leaf camouflage when resting with wings closed. It is a common sight in Amsterdam parks, gardens, and along canal-side vegetation, nectaring freely at flowers from early spring through autumn. It produces two broods per year and adults overwinter in hollow trees and dense vegetation, so it can be encountered in any month during mild spells. Larvae feed on nettles and hops, plants common in Amsterdam's green spaces. The default flower-visitor placement is appropriate as nectar-seeking is the most photogenic and frequent observed behavior.
