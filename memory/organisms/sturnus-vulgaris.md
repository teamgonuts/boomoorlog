The European starling is one of Amsterdam's most abundant urban birds, foraging in noisy flocks on lawns, pavements, and squares while also roosting and breeding in tree cavities and roof spaces. It visits fruiting Morus and Nyssa trees to gorge on berries, giving it a genuine tree-canopy secondary, but the dominant activity visible on streets and parks is ground foraging. Ground-urban is the primary habitat because pavement and lawn foraging is what observers see most; urban-walker captures the characteristic striding gait, with tree-flitter as secondary for the berry-feeding and roost-approach flights.
