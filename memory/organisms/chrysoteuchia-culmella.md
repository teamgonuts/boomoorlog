The Garden Grass-Veneer is a common crambid grass-moth found across Amsterdam parks, lawns, and canal verges wherever fine grasses grow, which is nearly everywhere in the city. Adults rest during the day with their characteristic slim, rolled-wing posture flat against grass stems, relying entirely on crypsis; they fly at dusk and in low light but do not visit flowers. The larvae are grass-root feeders, completing their full life cycle in lawn turf without reference to flowers at all. The insect category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is wrong for this specialist grass-moth; ground-park and idle-only accurately describe its daytime ecology in Amsterdam's parks and verges.
