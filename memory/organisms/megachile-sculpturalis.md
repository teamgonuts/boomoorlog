The Asian giant resin bee is the largest megachilid in Europe, an invasive species now established in the Netherlands that collects resin and pollen from flowering trees including Styphnolobium japonicum (Japanese pagoda tree), commonly planted as a street tree in Amsterdam. Like other leafcutter bees it is a dedicated flower-visitor and its movement between blooms is best represented by flower-bobber. Its large size makes it conspicuous while hovering at flowers. It nests in pre-existing cavities in wood or walls, but its observable activity is entirely at the flower level.
