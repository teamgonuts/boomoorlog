Yarrow is a widespread, feathery-leaved perennial common in Amsterdam's parks, lawns, canal verges, and amenity grasslands. Its flat-topped white flower heads are important nectar sources for many insects from June through October. The plant tolerates mowing and compaction, making it a persistent presence in maintained park lawns and road verges. Yarrow spreads vegetatively by creeping rhizomes in addition to seed, forming patches in suitable open ground. The default ground-park placement is accurate for virtually all Amsterdam observations.
