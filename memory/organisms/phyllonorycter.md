This entry covers the assemblage of leafminer micro-moths (Phyllonorycter and relatives) observed on Castanea (sweet chestnut) in Amsterdam. Their larvae mine inside the leaf lamina, creating characteristic blotch or tentiform mines visible by holding leaves to the light; the caterpillar is entirely hidden within the leaf throughout its development. There is no flower-visiting stage recorded in this tree-associated observation, making tree-canopy and idle-only the appropriate overrides. Adults are tiny night-flying moths whose brief adult lives are not captured in tree-dataset observations.
