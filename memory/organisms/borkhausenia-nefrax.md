Borkhausenia nefrax is a small oecophorid moth whose larvae are associated with dead wood, leaf litter, and dry plant detritus, often found in tree hollows, under bark, or in accumulated organic matter at the base of old trees. Adults rest motionless on bark or dead wood surfaces during the day and fly at night. In Amsterdam they are infrequently recorded, typically near mature trees in parks and cemeteries.
