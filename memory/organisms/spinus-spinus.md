The Eurasian siskin is an acrobatic finch that feeds primarily on the seed catkins of alder and birch, hanging from thin outer twigs while extracting seeds; it also uses larch and spruce in Amsterdam's planted groves. Flocks move between trees through the canopy in the classic flitting pattern of small passerines, making tree-canopy and tree-flitter the correct defaults. In Amsterdam siskins are most reliably seen in winter and early spring when alder and birch catkins are plentiful along park waterways. The bird default fits without any override needed.
