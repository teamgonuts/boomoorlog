The pine hawk-moth lays its eggs on Pinus needles and the caterpillars feed gregariously in the canopy, perfectly camouflaged against the bundled needles. Adults are nocturnal and spend the day pressed flat against pine bark, resting invisibly. Amsterdam's ornamental and planted pines in parks such as Amsterdamse Bos provide the larval host; the species is uncommon but recorded. Tree-canopy is correct for the larval feeding stage, which dominates the observable part of the life cycle; idle-only reflects both the motionless daytime adult resting on bark and the slow deliberate feeding of the caterpillar.
