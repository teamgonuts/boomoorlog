The Heart and Club is a medium-sized noctuid moth with distinctive forewing markings that flies at night from May through July and is regularly recorded at light traps in Amsterdam. By day it rests low on the ground, among grass litter, or on rough surfaces such as fence posts and bark, where its cryptic pale-grey patterning provides camouflage. Larvae are subterranean cutworms that feed on roots and stems of grasses and low herbaceous plants in parks and sandy verges. The default flower-visitor placement is incorrect for a ground-resting noctuid; ground-park is the appropriate dominant habitat reflecting both larval life and adult resting. Most Amsterdam observations are from moth-trap records rather than direct sighting.
