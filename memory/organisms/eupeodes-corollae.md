The Migrant Aphideater is a medium-sized hoverfly with broad yellow abdominal bands, one of the most abundant syrphids in Amsterdam parks and gardens during summer where its larvae prey on aphid colonies on shrubs and trees. Adults are avid nectar feeders, hovering beside umbellifers, composites, and flowering shrubs, making short precise moves between bloom clusters. As a migrant it crosses substantial distances on clear days, appearing suddenly in great numbers in late summer when southerly winds bring individuals from continental Europe. The insect default flower-visitor / flower-bobber fits well; a secondary sky-looper is added to reflect its long migratory transit flights, and tree-canopy captures the larval aphid-colony habitat.
