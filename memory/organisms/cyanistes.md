Blue tit (Cyanistes caeruleus) and great tit (Parus major) are Amsterdam's most abundant and visible small songbirds, recorded across a wide range of host trees including Aesculus, Amelanchier, Carpinus, Ilex, Robinia, and Styphnolobium. Both species are quintessential canopy foragers that move restlessly between trees in short arcing flights, perfectly matching the bird category defaults of tree-canopy and tree-flitter. They are year-round residents found in virtually every Amsterdam park, garden, and tree-lined street. No override is needed; the default tags describe this pair exactly.
