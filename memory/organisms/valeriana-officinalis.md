Common Valerian is a tall perennial herb with clusters of pale pink flowers that grows in damp meadows, riverbanks, reed margins, and moist park corners throughout Amsterdam. It especially favours the edges of canals, ditches, and wetland areas, making water-edge a meaningful secondary habitat alongside ground-park. The plant can reach 1.5 m and is a notable nectar source for long-tongued moths and bees in summer. Movement remains none; a water-edge secondary is added because Amsterdam observations concentrate along canal margins and ditch banks.
