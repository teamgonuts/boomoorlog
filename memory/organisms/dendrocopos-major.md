The Great Spotted Woodpecker is a striking black-and-white bird resident in Amsterdam's larger parks and along Betula and Cedrus avenues. It spends the great majority of its foraging time clinging to trunks and major branches, drilling for beetle larvae and extracting seeds from cones, making tree-bark the dominant habitat over the canopy default. Between foraging bouts it flies to a neighbouring tree with an undulating flight, so tree-flitter is retained as primary movement, while the long stationary drilling sessions warrant idle-only as a secondary. This pair of habitat and movement overrides distinguishes it from ordinary canopy songbirds.
