Perforate St John's-wort is an upright perennial herb of open, sunny ground — found on dry banks, roadsides, park margins, and rough grassland throughout Amsterdam. Its bright yellow five-petalled flowers, which open around midsummer (historically around St John's Day, 24 June), are visited by a wide range of bees and hoverflies. The leaves have characteristic translucent oil glands visible when held to the light, giving the "perforatum" name. The plant-default ground-park placement is correct; this species does not associate strongly with water edges or wall surfaces.
