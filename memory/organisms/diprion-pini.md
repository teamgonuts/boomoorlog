Pine sawflies are hymenopteran larvae that feed gregariously on pine needles (Pinus) in tight clusters, often stripping entire branches in Amsterdam's park pines. The larvae are the most conspicuous life stage — bright green or yellowish caterpillar-like grubs sitting motionless in rows on needle bundles. Adult sawflies are short-lived, unobtrusive and rarely visible. The entire observable lifecycle occurs within the tree canopy on needles and shoots; idle-only captures the gregarious sedentary larval clusters, replacing the insect defaults of flower-visitor and flower-bobber which are entirely inappropriate.
