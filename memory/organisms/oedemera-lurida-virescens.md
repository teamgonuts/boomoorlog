Oedemera lurida and Oedemera virescens are closely related false blister beetles (Oedemeridae) that are obligate flower visitors as adults, feeding on pollen from open flowers — particularly umbellifers, composites, and roses in Amsterdam's parks. They are reliable and easy to observe on warm summer days nectaring alongside bees and hoverflies. The default flower-visitor / flower-bobber combination fits perfectly, with idle-only secondary for resting bouts on flower heads between pollen-feeding sessions. Their shiny metallic green elytra make them distinctive among flower visitors.
