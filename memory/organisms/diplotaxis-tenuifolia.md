Perennial Wall-rocket is a yellow-flowered crucifer with a strong, pungent smell that is characteristic of disturbed urban ground — railway ballast, cracked pavements, ruined walls, and rubble. It is a classic urban coloniser rather than a park plant, thriving where soil is thin, nutrient-poor, and well-drained. In Amsterdam it appears in pavement cracks, along fences, and on the edges of cycle paths. ground-urban overrides the default ground-park because this species actively prefers built-up, disturbed substrates over vegetated park soil. Movement is none.
