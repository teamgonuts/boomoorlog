Goat's-beard is a perennial grassland composite recognisable by its large yellow flower heads — which close by midday, earning the folk name "Jack-go-to-bed-at-noon" — and its spectacular dandelion-like seed clock that can reach 10 cm across. It grows in meadow grassland, road verges, park lawns, and rough grass along canal banks throughout Amsterdam. It is a ground-level plant with no canopy or aquatic associations.
