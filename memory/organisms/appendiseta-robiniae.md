Appendiseta robiniae (the locust aphid or black locust aphid) is a specialist phloem-feeding aphid restricted to Robinia pseudoacacia (black locust), which is common in Amsterdam's parks and along canal-side plantings. Colonies form dense aggregations on the undersides of leaves and on young shoots in the tree canopy from spring through summer. Like all tree-canopy aphids, individuals are effectively immobile on their host — wingless morphs especially so — making idle-only the correct movement class. The tree-canopy habitat override from the insect default (flower-visitor) applies because this is a tree-feeding microorganism on Robinia rather than a pollinator.
