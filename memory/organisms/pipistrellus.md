Roosting bats in Amsterdam use bark crevices and cavities of mature urban trees — Aesculus, Quercus, Tilia, and others — as daytime shelters, making tree-bark the correct resting placement. At dusk they leave to hunt insects on long curved aerial paths above canals, parks, and open water, which drives the sky-only secondary and the dominant sky-looper animation. The idle-only secondary captures the daytime roosting phase when no movement occurs. The mammal default of ground-park / park-roamer does not apply to any bat species, so this is a full override.
