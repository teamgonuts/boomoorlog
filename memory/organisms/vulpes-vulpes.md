This row groups Red Fox, Badger, and Wild Boar as frugivorous mammals that consume fallen fruit from Pyrus (pear) trees in Amsterdam's parks. Red Fox is common in Amsterdam's parks and suburban gardens; Badger and Wild Boar are edge-of-city visitors. All three are ground-dwelling mammals that forage across vegetated park areas. The mammal default of ground-park and park-roamer fits the fox and badger well; wild boar occasional incursions into Amsterdam's green periphery follow the same ground-park pattern.
