The Speckled Bush-cricket is a small, wingless katydid that lives almost entirely in dense shrub and low tree foliage, where its vivid green colour provides excellent camouflage. It does not visit flowers and is not a ground forager; its entire life — feeding on leaves and small insects, singing, mating — takes place within canopy and hedgerow vegetation. Tree-canopy is therefore the appropriate habitat override from the insect default of flower-visitor. It moves very little and only slowly between leaf surfaces, so idle-only is a better movement class than flower-bobber. It is a common late-summer species in Amsterdam parks and gardens.
