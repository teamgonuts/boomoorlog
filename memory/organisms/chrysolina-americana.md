The Rosemary Beetle is a jewel-like leaf beetle with metallic purple and green stripes, introduced from southern Europe and now well established in Dutch urban gardens. Adults and larvae feed on rosemary, lavender, thyme, and sage, making them a regular presence on ornamental herb plantings in Amsterdam parks and courtyards. Despite its name it is not a rosemary specialist and ranges across a variety of aromatic shrubs. It is predominantly a phytophagous crawler that rests on its host plants; flower-visitor does not apply. ground-park and idle-only reflect its plant-clinging, sedentary adult lifestyle.
