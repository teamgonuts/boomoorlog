Myzocallis carpini is a specialist aphid that forms colonies on the undersides of Carpinus betulus (hornbeam) leaves throughout the growing season. Hornbeam is common in Amsterdam parks and cemeteries as both a street tree and a hedge plant. The aphids are essentially immobile during feeding — apterae cling to leaf veins and exude honeydew — making idle-only the only appropriate movement class. Tree-canopy is the sole habitat since the colony lives entirely within the leaf layer. Winged alates disperse briefly in summer but this flight is invisible at the pixel-art scale.
