This grouped entry covers spiders, harvestmen, and woodlice (Isopoda — crustaceans, technically not arachnids) found on Cupressocyparis (Leyland cypress) in Amsterdam. Leyland cypress has dense, fibrous bark that shelters a community of bark-dwelling invertebrates: spiders hunting in the crevices, harvestmen resting by day, and woodlice feeding on decaying bark tissue. The tree-bark habitat override from the arachnid default (anywhere) is appropriate here because the observation context is specifically bark microhabitat. Idle-only movement fits all three groups: woodlice creep too slowly to animate distinctly, and the spiders and harvestmen are mostly stationary unless disturbed.
