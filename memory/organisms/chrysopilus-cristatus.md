The Black Snipefly is a slender rhagionid fly that favours damp habitats — wet meadow margins, canal banks, and lush vegetation alongside water. Adults are typically seen resting motionless on leaves with their legs held out in a characteristic posture; they are predatory but hunt small invertebrates without elaborate movement. In Amsterdam the most likely sightings are along canal and ditch edges where vegetation is rank and humid. water-edge is the dominant placement, with ground-park as a secondary for vegetated margin areas away from standing water. idle-only captures the resting-on-leaf adult behaviour.
