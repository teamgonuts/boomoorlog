Cydia illutana and related larch cone moths spend their larval stages inside larch cones and shoot tips, making them almost entirely canopy-bound on Larix trees in Amsterdam. Adults are small nocturnal tortricid moths that rest motionless on bark or foliage during the day. Because they spend their lives either concealed inside plant tissue or resting still, flower-visitor and flower-bobber defaults are completely wrong; tree-canopy with idle-only captures their actual presence on the tree without the motion they simply do not perform. They are an override from the insect default.
