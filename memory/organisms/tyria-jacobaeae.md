The Cinnabar is a strikingly patterned day-flying moth with bright red and black wings that serve as aposematic warning coloration. It is closely associated with ragwort (Jacobaea vulgaris) and related Senecio species, where larvae feed communally and become equally conspicuous in their black-and-yellow banded coloration. Adults fly from May to July and can be found nectaring at flowers in open parks, dunes fringe, and disturbed ground. Amsterdam observations cluster around areas where ragwort grows on sunny verges and park edges. The default flower-visitor placement is appropriate since adults are genuinely seen visiting flowers.
