Field Bindweed is a persistent climbing perennial with white or pink funnel-shaped flowers that twines through fences, scrambles over pavements, and colonises disturbed ground, road verges, and rubble throughout Amsterdam. It is one of the most difficult urban weeds to eradicate due to deep vertical roots. The dominant habitat is ground-urban because of its strong preference for paved edges, rubble, and built-up disturbed ground, with ground-park as a secondary for park-margin and verge populations. Movement stays none.
