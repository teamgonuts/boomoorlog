Cydia splendana is a tortricid moth whose larvae bore into developing sweet chestnut nuts (Castanea) to feed on the kernel. In Amsterdam this species is observed on the relatively rare Castanea trees in parks and avenues. Adults are small, subtly patterned moths that rest immobile on bark or foliage and are almost never seen in active flight during daylight. The canopy of the host chestnut is the correct placement zone, and idle-only replaces the insect default of flower-bobber since these moths are sedentary during any visible portion of their lifecycle.
