The Painted Lady is a long-distance migrant butterfly that appears in Amsterdam in variable numbers each summer, concentrating wherever thistles, knapweed, and other open-ground composites bloom in parks, waste ground, and canal-side verges. Like other Nymphalid butterflies it is primarily a nectar feeder, moving between flowering plants in the short-bob pattern characteristic of flower-visiting butterflies. In strong migration years it can be extremely numerous across the city, using Amsterdam's canal-side plantings as fuelling stops. The insect category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is correct for this species.
