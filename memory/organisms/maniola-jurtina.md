The Meadow Brown is the commonest grassland butterfly in Amsterdam parks, nectaring on knapweed, bramble, and other open flowers through the long summer flight period. Its larvae feed on fine grasses in unmown park margins and verges. The default flower-visitor / flower-bobber pairing fits well; ground-park secondary reflects the grass-feeding larval stage and the time adults spend resting with wings closed on grass stems between nectar visits. It is reliably seen in Vondelpark, the Plantage, and the Amsterdamse Bos from June through August.
