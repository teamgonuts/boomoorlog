Carulaspis juniperi (juniper scale, family Diaspididae) is an armoured scale insect that anchors to the bark and branchlets of Thuja (arborvitae) and related conifers used in Amsterdam hedges and garden plantings. Once settled, females are permanently immobile beneath their oyster-shaped scale cover; their only mobility phase is the brief crawler stage immediately after hatching.
