The Red Admiral is a familiar and abundant butterfly across Amsterdam parks, gardens, and canal-side plantings throughout spring and autumn. It is a dedicated nectar feeder visiting thistles, buddleia, ivy bloom, and fallen fruit — all well represented in Amsterdam's urban green spaces. Its flight between flowering plants is typical of the flower-bobber pattern: short directional bobs of half a metre or less between nearby blooms, punctuated by basking pauses with wings spread on warm surfaces. It overwinters as an adult in mild urban winters or migrates south, returning from the Mediterranean in spring.
