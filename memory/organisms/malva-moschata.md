Musk-mallow is a perennial herb of grassy verges, meadow edges, and park clearings, recognised by its deeply cut leaves and pale pink flowers with a faint musky scent. It is a ground-level plant with no canopy, wall, or aquatic associations, and its attractive flowers are important nectar sources for bumblebees and hoverflies. The plant default (ground-park, none) applies directly and no override is needed. In Amsterdam it occurs in meadow-style park plantings and along road verges in outer districts.
