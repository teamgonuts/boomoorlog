Paracolopha morrisoni is a specialist gall-forming aphid that creates leaf-fold galls on Zelkova serrata, an increasingly popular Amsterdam street tree. The foundress and her colony spend the season inside the protective gall on the leaf, with no external movement visible. Zelkova does not produce the floral resources that flower-foraging insects seek, confirming that the default insect tags are unsuitable. Tree-canopy placement and idle-only movement correctly represent this highly sedentary, host-specific phloem-feeder.
