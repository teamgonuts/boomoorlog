The striped shield bug is a boldly red-and-black patterned pentatomid bug that feeds on the developing seeds of Apiaceae (umbellifers) such as wild carrot, hogweed, and ground elder; it is almost invariably found perched on umbellifer flower heads in parks and canal-side vegetation. Flower-visitor is the dominant habitat because of its tight association with flowering umbellifers. Adults are slow-moving and spend extended periods stationary on flower heads, making idle-only the appropriate animation over the insect default of flower-bobber.
