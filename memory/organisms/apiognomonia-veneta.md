Apiognomonia veneta is the causal agent of plane anthracnose, a widespread fungal disease of Platanus (plane trees) in Amsterdam's urban canals and parks. The fungus colonises bark, twigs, and leaf scars, overwintering in necrotic tissue on the trunk and branches. In spring it releases conidia during wet conditions, causing dieback of new shoots and blight of emerging leaves. The tree-bark placement is appropriate because the pathogen's fruiting bodies (acervuli in the asexual Discula stage) emerge directly from infected bark tissue rather than soil or canopy foliage. It is one of the most commonly observed diseases on Amsterdam's many mature plane avenues.
