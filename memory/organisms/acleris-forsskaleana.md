Acleris forsskaleana is a small tortrix moth whose larvae spin and roll the young leaves of maples (Acer species), a tree abundant throughout Amsterdam as street and park plantings. Adults are pale straw-coloured with subtle darker markings and rest cryptically against bark or leaves in the tree canopy during the day (idle-only). They are entirely canopy-associated through their life cycle — larval leaf-rolling and adult resting all occur on or within the Acer crown — making tree-canopy the sole and correct habitat.
