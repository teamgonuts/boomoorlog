The Common Pipistrelle is Amsterdam's most frequently recorded bat, roosting by day in tight bark crevices of street trees such as Platanus, Populus, and Acer before emerging at twilight to hunt midges and moths. During active hours it flies fast looping circuits at 2–10 m above canals, parks, and lit streets — a textbook sky-looper. The tree-bark habitat tag places the sprite at a trunk during daylight with an idle-only animation, while the sky-only secondary and sky-looper movement dominate the dusk render. The mammal default of ground-park / park-roamer is entirely wrong for a bat, hence the override.
