Catoptria verellus is a crambid grass moth whose larvae feed in the roots and stems of various grasses, particularly in dry sandy or mossy grassland habitats. Adults rest among low grass and moss during the day with wings tightly folded, flying from dusk onwards. In Amsterdam they are recorded in open grassy areas, dunes, and park margins with sandy substrate.
