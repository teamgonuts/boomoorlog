The Harlequin Ladybird is an invasive Asian ladybird that has become the commonest ladybird in Amsterdam, found on trees, shrubs, and herbaceous plants throughout the city wherever aphid colonies occur. The f. spectabilis form has a black background with red spots, one of the many colour morphs of this extraordinarily variable species. Adults are voracious aphid predators that move through the canopy following aphid populations, making tree-canopy the dominant habitat and tree-flitter the primary movement. Flower-visitor is added as secondary habitat because adults also aggregate on flowering plants in late summer before overwintering in building crevices; idle-only covers the extended sunny perching periods.
