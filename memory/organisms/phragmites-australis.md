Common Reed is a tall perennial grass forming extensive reedbeds along Amsterdam's canal margins, park ponds, ditches, and the IJ waterfront, one of the most ecologically important wetland plants in the Netherlands. It grows in standing or slow-moving water with roots in waterlogged soil, sending up dense stands of stems to 3 m or more. Reedbeds provide nesting habitat for reed warblers, bitterns, and marsh harriers, and winter roosting for Starlings.
