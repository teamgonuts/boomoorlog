Armoured scales (Diaspididae) and mealybugs (Pseudococcidae) are sap-sucking hemipterans that attach to bark, twigs and sometimes leaves of Celtis and Cercidiphyllum trees in Amsterdam. Armoured scales in particular are permanently sedentary once settled, secreting a waxy protective cover and spending their entire feeding life fixed to the bark surface. Mealybugs move very slowly when young but are functionally stationary at sprite scale. Tree-bark is the correct dominant habitat — not flower-visitor — and idle-only replaces flower-bobber entirely, as these insects perform no meaningful locomotion.
