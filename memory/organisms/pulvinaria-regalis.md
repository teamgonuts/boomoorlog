Pulvinaria regalis is a soft scale insect that coats the bark of Aesculus (horse chestnut) and Platanus in Amsterdam streets with conspicuous waxy white egg masses in early summer. Adults and nymphs are glued to the bark surface beneath their waxy shield for virtually their entire lives, crawling only millimetres as first-instar crawlers before permanently settling. Bark is the exclusive habitat and movement is below map-scale perception, so tree-bark / idle-only is the precise override. The insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber has no relevance to a bark-feeding scale insect.
