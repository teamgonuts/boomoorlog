The pine processionary moth is a significant public-health pest whose urticating-haired caterpillars live communally in large white silk nests in the crowns of Pinus trees, descending in distinctive nose-to-tail processions to pupate in spring. The nests in the upper canopy are the most visible sign of the species. In Amsterdam and the broader Dutch urban landscape it has been expanding northward with climate warming and is now recorded on planted pines. Tree-canopy is the correct habitat because the nests and larval feeding are in the pine crown; idle-only captures the colony's predominantly stationary nest-bound existence, with the processionary march being an occasional behavior not warranting a separate movement class.
