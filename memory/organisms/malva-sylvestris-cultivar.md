Common Mallow cultivars are ornamental selections of the native Malva sylvestris, grown for their showy purple-veined flowers in public plantings, garden borders, and park beds across Amsterdam. The species itself also naturalises on rough ground and roadsides. Cultivated forms tend to remain closer to planted park and garden settings. Flowers are visited by a range of bees, particularly common carder bees. Default ground-park and none apply; the cultivar tag indicates horticultural rather than wild provenance.
