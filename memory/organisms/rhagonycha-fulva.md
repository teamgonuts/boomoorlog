The Common Red Soldier Beetle is one of Amsterdam's most conspicuous summer beetles, with its bright orange-red elytra and black tip making it immediately recognisable on umbellifers — hogweed, angelica, and cow parsley — where adults feed on pollen and small insects during July and August. Mating pairs are frequently seen on flower heads, making this genuinely a flower-visitor insect rather than a pure herbivore. The insect default flower-visitor / flower-bobber fits the dominant adult behaviour well; idle-only is added as a secondary movement because adults also spend long periods sitting motionlessly on flowers or stems. Ground-park is a secondary habitat reflecting the larval stage which lives in soil hunting worms and other invertebrates.
