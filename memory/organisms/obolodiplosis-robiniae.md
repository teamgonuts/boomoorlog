The black locust gall midge is a tiny cecidomyiid fly whose larvae induce characteristic leaf-roll galls on Robinia pseudoacacia, an ornamental street tree found across Amsterdam. The adult midges and their larvae spend their entire life cycle within the folded leaf margins of the host canopy, never visiting flowers or foraging on the ground. Default insect tags (flower-visitor, flower-bobber) are overridden because this species is strictly a tree-canopy dweller with no flower-foraging behavior. The larval stage is entirely sedentary inside the gall, making idle-only the correct movement class.
