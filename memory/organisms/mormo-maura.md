The Old Lady is a large, dark noctuid moth that rests by day pressed flat against tree bark, wooden fences, or sheltered walls where its mottled grey-brown patterning provides near-perfect camouflage. Larvae feed on herbaceous plants and low shrubs at night. The species is regularly found in Amsterdam parks and canal-side trees, always in a motionless, pressed-flat resting posture during daylight hours. Tree-bark and idle-only replace the insect defaults of flower-visitor and flower-bobber to reflect this characteristic roosting behaviour.
