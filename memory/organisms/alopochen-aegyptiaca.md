The Egyptian Goose is a highly successful introduced species now ubiquitous in Amsterdam's parks and waterways, with Vondelpark and Amstelpark among its strongholds. It spends time both swimming on open water and grazing on park lawns in large, noisy groups, making both water-surface and ground-park genuine primary habitats. On water it drifts and paddles like other wildfowl, while on land it roams freely across grass with a deliberate walking gait. It nests in trees (an unusual trait for a goose) and sometimes perches on branches and rooftops, but its observable daytime behaviour in Amsterdam is dominated by the water-drift and park-roam cycle. The default bird tag of tree-canopy / tree-flitter is entirely wrong.
