Barklice (Psocoptera) and bark-dwelling springtails (Collembola) are millimetric arthropods that graze algae, fungal spores, and organic films on the outer bark of Taxus and other urban trees in Amsterdam. They are among the most abundant but least visible bark micro-fauna, forming thin drifts visible only under magnification. Their movement is imperceptible at map scale and they never visit flowers, making the insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber inappropriate. Tree-bark placement with idle-only movement is the correct override for sedentary bark-grazing microarthropods.
