Mononychus punctumalbum is a small weevil (Curculionidae) that is an oligophagous specialist on Yellow Iris (Iris pseudacorus), with larvae developing inside the seed capsules. It is therefore tightly bound to the water margins and ditches where Yellow Iris grows throughout Amsterdam's canal system and park ponds. Adults are found crawling on iris leaves and seed pods at the water's edge. Habitat overridden to water-edge (dominant) with ground-park secondary for adults found on adjacent vegetation; movement overridden to idle-only.
