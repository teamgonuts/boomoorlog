Wall Lettuce is a slender annual or biennial composite that specialises in colonising old brick walls, shaded wall bases, and rocky urban surfaces across Amsterdam, its name reflecting this preference for masonry habitats. It produces small pale-yellow dandelion-like flowers and thrives in the mortar of canal-side walls, churchyard boundaries, and building bases where little other vegetation can grow. Despite the plant default of ground-park, Wall Lettuce is distinctively wall-associated and wall-and-roof is the correct dominant habitat; ground-park is a secondary where it grows at the base of walls in shaded park soil. Movement is none as for all plants.
