This entry covers the combined observation of house sparrows and great tits associated with Koelreuteria (golden rain tree) in Amsterdam. House sparrows are quintessential urban birds nesting in building crevices and foraging on pavements, while great tits forage in the canopy — the two species thus pull the habitat in different directions. Wall-and-roof is listed as dominant because house sparrow is the more characteristically urban component and is the species that gives this slug its name; tree-canopy is added as the secondary. Urban-walker suits the sparrow's pavement-foraging, and tree-flitter suits the tit, giving a two-value movement array.
