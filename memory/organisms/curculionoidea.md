This entry covers the diverse community of over 100 weevil species (superfamily Curculionoidea) associated with willows (Salix) in the wider Amsterdam region, including canal-bank pollard willows and park specimens. Willow weevils include leaf-rollers, gall-formers, and bark-miners; all life stages are confined to the willow host, either on leaves and shoots in the canopy or in stems and bark. The insect category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is overridden to tree-canopy / idle-only because this guild is comprised of leaf and tissue feeders, not pollinators, and adults on willow foliage barely move at map sprite scale.
