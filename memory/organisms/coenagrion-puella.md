The Azure Bluet is a common and instantly recognisable damselfly with powder-blue and black banding along the abdomen, one of Amsterdam's most frequent waterside insects in summer. Adults perch motionless on emergent vegetation at canal and pond edges (idle-only dominant), making short hovering sallies over the water surface when hunting small insects or courting. Mating pairs form a wheel and oviposit into submerged or floating plant stems at the water surface. Habitat is overridden to water-edge dominant with water-surface secondary; movement keeps idle-only as dominant with the short hover-and-dart captured approximately by flower-bobber (the closest available class for short positional bobs without long transits).
