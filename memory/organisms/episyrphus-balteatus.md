The Marmalade Hoverfly is the most abundant and recognisable hoverfly in Amsterdam, present from early spring right through to autumn wherever flowers are open. Adults are dedicated nectar and pollen feeders, visiting umbellifers, open-faced composites, and a wide range of ornamental flowers in parks and canal-side plantings. They hover with the characteristic stationary precision of syrphids before dropping onto a bloom, then bob to the next nearby flower — a perfect match for the flower-bobber class. It is explicitly named in the taxonomy as an example of the flower-visitor / flower-bobber default and the insect category default fits exactly.
