The Welted Thistle is a tall biennial thistle of damp, fertile soils in park margins, canal banks, and roadside verges in Amsterdam, reaching up to 1.5 m in height. Its small crimson-purple flower heads are important nectar sources for bumblebees, butterflies, and hoverflies from June through September. The stem is characteristically winged with spiny lobes all the way to the top, distinguishing it from similar species. In Amsterdam it favours the partially shaded, moist edges of parks and tree plantings rather than fully exposed urban ground. The default ground-park placement is appropriate for most observations.
