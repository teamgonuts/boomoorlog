Carulaspis juniper scale insects infest the bark, stems, and foliage of × Cupressocyparis (Leyland cypress), a fast-growing hybrid conifer widely used for hedging in Amsterdam gardens and institutional grounds. Settled crawlers cement themselves to bark with their protective waxy shell and do not move; populations cause yellowing and dieback of affected branchlets.
