The German Scorpionfly is a distinctive insect of shaded, damp hedgerows and woodland edges, where adults scavenge on dead insects and over-ripe fruit on or near the ground. Males are recognised by the upturned, scorpion-like tip of the abdomen. It is not a flower visitor — it feeds on carrion and soft fruit — so the insect defaults of flower-visitor and flower-bobber are inappropriate. Ground-park captures the damp, shaded park-edge habitat; idle-only fits because adults perch on vegetation with occasional short flights that are below the threshold for a distinct movement class. Observations in Amsterdam come chiefly from Amsterdamse Bos and cemetery edges.
