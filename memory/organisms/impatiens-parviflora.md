Small Balsam is an annual herb introduced from Central Asia that has become one of the most widespread invasive plants in Amsterdam's parks, woodland understories, and shaded canal banks. It grows rapidly in moist, shaded soil and produces abundant small pale yellow flowers from summer to autumn, explosively ejecting seeds on touch. Unlike the taller Indian Balsam, this species tolerates deeper shade and drier conditions. The plant defaults of ground-park and none apply without modification.
