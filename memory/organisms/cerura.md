Cerura vinula (puss moth) and Furcula species (sallow kitten and related moths) are Notodontidae whose larvae feed on Populus (poplar) and Salix foliage; poplar is abundant along Amsterdam's canals and in the Amsterdamse Bos. The large, vividly marked caterpillars sit exposed on leaves in the canopy and are well-camouflaged when young; adults are nocturnal and seldom observed. The larval stage is the ecologically dominant and visually interesting phase, fixed in the canopy on poplar.
