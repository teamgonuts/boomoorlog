Phytophthora lateralis is an oomycete root and crown pathogen that infects and kills Chamaecyparis (false cypress) trees in Amsterdam by rotting the roots and basal stem tissue. Although oomycetes are not true fungi, they are assigned to the fungus category as the closest practical equivalent in the taxonomy. The default fungus tag of tree-bark is overridden to tree-rooted because this pathogen operates belowground and at the root collar, not on the aerial bark. Movement is none, as all Phytophthora propagules move only with water flow and have no animated life stage.
