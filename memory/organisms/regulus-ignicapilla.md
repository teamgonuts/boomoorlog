The Firecrest is a scarce but regular winter visitor to Amsterdam, favouring dense evergreen plantings of Chamaecyparis and similar conifers in parks and cemetery gardens. Like its close relative the Goldcrest, it is an energetic needle-gleaner that barely pauses between tree canopies, perfectly fitting the tree-flitter animation. It is smaller than a Blue Tit and even more hyperactive, flicking through foliage at short intervals and rarely descending to ground level. The bird default of tree-canopy / tree-flitter accurately represents this species without modification.
