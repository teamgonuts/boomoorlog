White Campion is a tall annual or short-lived perennial of disturbed ground, road verges, wasteland, and park margins, producing large white flowers that open in the evening and are pollinated by moths. It is a ground-level plant with a preference for recently disturbed or nutrient-enriched soils — common on the edges of Amsterdam construction sites, canal bank restorations, and park border treatments.
