This entry covers the larval stage of generalist tortrix and geometrid moths found feeding on Liquidambar foliage in Amsterdam parks. Liquidambar styraciflua is a popular ornamental tree whose star-shaped leaves are grazed by a range of polyphagous caterpillars that web or roll the leaves for shelter. The adult moths are night-flying and rest motionless during the day, so neither the larval nor adult stage involves flower-visiting. Tree-canopy is the appropriate habitat because the caterpillars feed in the foliage; idle-only reflects the slow, cryptic feeding style of leaf-rolling caterpillars and the static resting adults.
