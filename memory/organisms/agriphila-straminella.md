The Pearl Veneer is a small straw-coloured crambid moth whose larvae feed on grass roots at ground level. Adults rest in grass during the day with wings folded tightly around the body, making them look like a dead grass stem, and fly at dusk. They are common in Amsterdam parks, sports fields, and any area with rank grass. The insect default of flower-visitor/flower-bobber does not fit; this moth does not visit flowers and spends the day motionless on or near the ground.
