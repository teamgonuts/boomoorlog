The Grey Heron is one of Amsterdam's most recognisable large waterbirds, stationed at canal banks, park ponds, and the margins of the IJ wherever fish are accessible. Its hunting strategy alternates between slow, deliberate stalking along the water's edge and standing utterly motionless for minutes at a time waiting for prey — this dual behaviour is exactly why both water-edge-stalker and idle-only are needed. It is colonial in the breeding season, with a large heronry in the Vondelpark and other green corridors, and individuals are often seen perched on canal railings or rooftops waiting for an opportunity. The default bird tag of tree-canopy / tree-flitter is entirely wrong for this large wading bird.
