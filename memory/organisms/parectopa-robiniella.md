Parectopa robiniella and the closely related Macrosaccus robiniella are North American leafminer moths introduced to Europe; their larvae create distinctive upper-surface blotch mines in Robinia pseudoacacia leaves across Amsterdam parks and streets. The tiny caterpillars are confined inside the leaf tissue throughout their larval development and cannot be seen without close inspection. There is no flower-visiting or ground-foraging phase for the larval stage captured in tree observations, so the default insect tags are overridden to tree-canopy and idle-only. Adults are small enough that their brief flights go unrecorded in the tree dataset.
