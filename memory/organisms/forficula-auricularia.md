The common earwig is a nocturnal generalist that shelters under loose bark on urban trees such as Parrotia, Paulownia and Pyrus, then forages at night on the ground among leaf litter and low vegetation in Amsterdam parks and gardens. Its movement is below the animation threshold — earwigs creep very slowly and shelter motionlessly during daylight hours, fitting idle-only. The override from the insect default (flower-visitor / flower-bobber) is warranted because earwigs are bark-sheltering detritivores rather than flower pollinators, with a secondary ground-park tag reflecting night-time foraging.
