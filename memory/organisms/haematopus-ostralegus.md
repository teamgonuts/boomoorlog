The Eurasian Oystercatcher is an increasingly common urban breeding bird in Amsterdam, famously nesting on flat rooftops across the city as well as at water edges along the IJ, the Amstel, and canal margins. In Amsterdam it is as often seen striding along canal towpaths, flat rooftop surfaces, and paved waterfronts as it is at the natural water edge — ground-urban is a genuine secondary habitat in this city specifically. Its movement is a deliberate patrol walk along linear features matching water-edge-stalker, and on urban surfaces it uses the same stride as urban-walker. The distinctive orange bill and piping alarm call make it one of Amsterdam's most identifiable urban waterbirds. The default bird tag of tree-canopy / tree-flitter is entirely wrong.
