Lasius niger (black garden ant) is the most ubiquitous ant in Amsterdam, nesting in pavement cracks, wall bases, and garden soil across the city, and observed on a diverse set of host trees including Laburnum, Styphnolobium, Taxodium, Thuja, Tilia and Zelkova. Workers forage across paved and unpaved surfaces, making them a genuine dual-habitat insect: ground-urban for their typical pavement-foraging and nest-siting, ground-park for green space foraging. Urban-walker is the dominant movement (workers patrolling pavements), with park-roamer for grass and park foraging trails. The six-tree host list reflects their generalism rather than tree-canopy specificity, so an override from the insect default is correct.
