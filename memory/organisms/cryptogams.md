This entry covers the cryptogamic crust of foliose lichens and cushion mosses colonising the bark of Ginkgo in Amsterdam. Ginkgo's rough, deeply fissured bark provides an excellent substrate for both groups, and the clean-air-sensitive lichen community in Amsterdam is increasingly diverse as air quality improves. The lichen category defaults of tree-bark and none fit perfectly: cryptogams are sessile organisms permanently adhered to the bark surface with no movement at any life stage. No override is needed.
