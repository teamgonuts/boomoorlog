The Firebug is a striking red-and-black true bug that congregates in dense clusters at the base of linden trees and mallow plants, feeding on fallen seeds. It is a ground-dwelling species that barely moves — groups sit motionless in sunny patches on bare soil or leaf litter — so idle-only is strongly appropriate and flower-bobber is entirely wrong. The habitat is ground-park rather than the insect default flower-visitor because firebugs are seed-feeders on the ground, not nectar-seekers on blooms. Amsterdam observations are frequently at the bases of Tilia trees in parks.
