This entry covers saproxylic (dead-wood-dependent) beetles associated with Hericium fungus and beech wood decay on Fagus in Amsterdam. Saproxylic beetles spend their larval phase tunnelling inside dead or decaying timber and adult beetles cling to or hide under bark; they are not flower visitors. The insect category default is overridden to tree-bark / idle-only, which correctly places the sprite on the trunk area of the host Fagus and animates it as a nearly immobile bark-surface beetle. Beech with veteran character in the Amsterdamse Bos provides the primary habitat for this guild in the city.
