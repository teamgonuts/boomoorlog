Croesus septentrionalis and related hazel sawflies feed on the leaves of Corylus in the canopy, with the larvae often arching their abdomens in unison as a defense display when disturbed. Adults are small and dark, sitting on foliage or stems without any meaningful flower-visiting behavior. Corylus is present as an understory tree and ornamental in Amsterdam parks and hedgerows. Tree-canopy is the correct habitat because the entire observable life stage is on the foliage; idle-only is appropriate because the larvae are slow feeders and the adults rest motionless between short flights.
