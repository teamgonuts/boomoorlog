This aggregate slug covers the diverse community of flies, bees, wasps, beetles and other insects observed visiting hawthorn (Crataegus) blossom in Amsterdam. Crataegus is a major urban flowering shrub and small tree whose heavy white-scented blooms in May attract a wide range of pollinating insects. The assemblage — hoverflies, solitary bees, bumblebees, beetles — all exhibit classic flower-visitor behaviour, moving from bloom to bloom within and between trees. The insect defaults of flower-visitor habitat and flower-bobber movement fit this group perfectly and no override is required.
