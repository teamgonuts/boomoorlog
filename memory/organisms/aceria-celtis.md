Hackberry gall mites induce small pouch and nipple galls on the leaves of Celtis trees, which are increasingly used as a heat-tolerant street tree in Amsterdam. The mites are microscopic and entirely sedentary inside galls. As with other eriophyid groupings, the override pins them to Celtis canopy rather than the arachnid anywhere default.
