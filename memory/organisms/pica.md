Magpie (Pica pica) and carrion crow (Corvus corone) are two of Amsterdam's most successful urban corvids, observed together on Koelreuteria (golden rain tree) and across the entire city. Both species forage omnivorous on pavements, lawns, and rubbish, while also using trees as perch and nest sites — making anywhere the correct dominant habitat as specified for corvids in the taxonomy. The two-value movement array (tree-flitter, urban-walker) reflects their genuine alternation between tree perching with inter-tree flights and ground foraging walks on urban surfaces. This is the canonical example of the anywhere/multi-movement pattern from the taxonomy guide.
