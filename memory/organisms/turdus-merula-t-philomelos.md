This row groups Blackbird and Song Thrush as the primary Turdidae associated with Malus (crab apple) trees in Amsterdam. Both species feed heavily on fallen and hanging crab apples in autumn. The Blackbird dominates ground level while the Song Thrush is slightly more canopy-oriented, but both are strong ground foragers. Ground-park leads as the dominant habitat, with tree-canopy secondary. Park-roamer leads as the dominant movement, reflecting the bounding ground gait both species use across Amsterdam's parks.
