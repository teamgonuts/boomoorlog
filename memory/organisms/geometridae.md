This entry covers geometrid and noctuid moth caterpillars that feed on Acer, Picea, Tilia and Zelkova foliage in Amsterdam street trees and parks. As larvae they are sedentary leaf-feeders clinging to foliage, making tree-canopy and idle-only the accurate tags. Adult moths are nocturnal and seldom visible during daytime simulation hours, so the caterpillar life stage dominates the placement logic. The override from the insect default (flower-visitor / flower-bobber) reflects their strict leaf-eating caterpillar ecology and lack of flower-visiting behaviour.
