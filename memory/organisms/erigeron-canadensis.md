Canadian Fleabane is a North American annual introduced to Europe in the 17th century and now a common weed of disturbed, dry, and often paved urban ground. It thrives in pavement cracks, rubble, and railway ballast and is rarely found in manicured park lawns. Its tiny white flowers appear from mid-summer and produce masses of wind-dispersed seeds. In Amsterdam it is a quintessential ground-urban species, familiar on streets and footpaths. The override from ground-park to ground-urban reflects its strong preference for hard-standing and impoverished urban substrate over vegetated park soil.
