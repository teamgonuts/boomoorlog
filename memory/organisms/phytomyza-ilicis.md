Phytomyza ilicis is the holly leaf miner fly, whose larvae create the distinctive yellow-brown blotch mines visible on Ilex (holly) leaves throughout Amsterdam. The single larva develops entirely inside the leaf between the upper and lower epidermis, making its habitat strictly tree-canopy by placement. Adults are tiny, inconspicuous flies that mate and oviposit quickly in spring; the larval mines are the persistent visible evidence. The default insect tags are overridden to tree-canopy and idle-only, as this species has no flower-foraging role and the larval stage — the observable one — does not move.
