The Yellow-legged Mining Bee is one of the most abundant solitary bees in Amsterdam, with two broods a year — one in spring and one in late summer — and nesting aggregations sometimes numbering in the hundreds in sandy or compacted bare soil in parks and path edges. It is a broad generalist forager visiting a huge variety of flowers, particularly dandelions in spring and late-season composites in summer. Ground-park is added as secondary habitat for the prominent nesting activity visible to observers.
