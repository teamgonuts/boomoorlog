Hogweed is a tall, robust umbellifer of roadsides, river banks, and park margins, producing large flat-topped white flower heads that are among Amsterdam's most important insect refuelling stations in early summer. It is a ground-level herbaceous plant, firmly ground-park, with no canopy or aquatic associations. Despite its value as a flower-visitor magnet it is itself a plant and carries the standard plant tags. The plant default (ground-park, none) is the exact right assignment.
