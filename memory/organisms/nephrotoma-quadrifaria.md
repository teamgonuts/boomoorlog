The Four-spotted Cranefly is a large, distinctively patterned cranefly with yellow-and-black banding that rests on low vegetation, leaves, and flower heads in parks, meadows, and damp grassland in Amsterdam. Its larvae (leatherjackets) live in moist soil eating roots. Adults are predominantly idle-only, sitting on stems and leaves during the day with characteristic drooping legs, and fly weakly when disturbed. Overridden from the insect flower-visitor default as this species does not visit flowers for nectar and is better placed in the ground vegetation stratum (ground-park) with idle-only as its sole movement class.
