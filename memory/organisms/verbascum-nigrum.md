Dark Mullein is a biennial or perennial with attractive yellow flowers bearing purple-hairy stamens, found in Amsterdam on dry, sunny, disturbed ground — roadsides, rail margins, dyke tops, and scrubby park edges — often on chalky or sandy substrates. It is less common than the Great Mullein but occurs regularly on the drier south-facing embankments and gravel paths of Amsterdam's outer parks and green corridors. The species prefers the drier, more disturbed ground-urban character over typical park soil, so ground-urban is dominant with ground-park as secondary. Movement is none.
