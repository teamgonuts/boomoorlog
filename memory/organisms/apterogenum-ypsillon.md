The Dingy Shears is a noctuid moth whose caterpillars feed on low-growing plants such as plantains and dandelions in open park and ruderal habitats. Adults are nocturnal and rest cryptically during the day on low vegetation or the ground, making them invisible in day-mode simulation; idle-only captures this roosting stillness. Flower-bobber is wrong — this moth does not visit flowers by day. Ground-park is the appropriate habitat override since both larval feeding and adult resting are tied to vegetated open ground rather than canopy or blooms. It is a widespread but easily-overlooked moth in Amsterdam parks.
