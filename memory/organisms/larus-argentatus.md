The European Herring Gull is one of Amsterdam's most visible large birds, nesting on flat rooftops across the city and foraging opportunistically on pavements, canal edges, and open water. Its vocal breeding colonies on urban rooftops make wall-and-roof a genuine secondary habitat, while anywhere captures its true generalist nature. Adults are powerful fliers, regularly soaring over the city and IJ waterfront, earning sky-looper as the dominant movement. On the ground it struts confidently along streets and market squares scavenging scraps, well represented by urban-walker. Observations peak in spring and summer when rooftop colonies are active and noisy.
