Anomala dubia is a scarab beetle whose larvae develop in sandy soil — historically a dune species but increasingly recorded in Amsterdam's sandy park soils and sports-field edges. Adults emerge in summer and visit flowers (particularly roses and other Rosa-family plants) to feed on pollen, so a flower-bobber secondary movement is warranted. However, adults spend considerable time resting on vegetation and are not obligate flower-visitors in the same way as bees, making ground-park the dominant habitat and idle-only the dominant movement. The default flower-visitor / flower-bobber insect assignment partially fits but ground-park better captures the larval and overall ecology.
