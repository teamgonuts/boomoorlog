Cinara pinea and C. pini are among the largest European aphids, forming conspicuous brown colonies on the bark of Pinus shoots and branches. They feed by piercing bark to tap phloem sap and remain anchored in place, occasionally attended by ants. In Amsterdam pines — including those in Vondelpark and the Amsterdamse Bos — colonies persist through the growing season with minimal positional movement, so idle-only is the correct movement class. The insect category default flower-visitor / flower-bobber is overridden in favour of tree-bark / idle-only because these are entirely bark-bound organisms.
