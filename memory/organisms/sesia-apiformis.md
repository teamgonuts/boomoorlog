The hornet moth is a clearwing moth whose larvae tunnel deep into the base and roots of Populus trees, spending two to three years inside the wood before the adult emerges. Adults are mimics of hornets and rest cryptically on bark or low stems near their emergence holes; they do not visit flowers in any meaningful way. In Amsterdam poplars are widespread along canal banks and park edges, giving the hornet moth a consistent set of host trees. Tree-bark is the correct habitat class because the larval galleries and adult emergence are both anchored to the trunk surface; idle-only reflects the adult's tendency to rest still on bark rather than move actively.
