Lagria beetles are soft-bodied, somewhat hairy beetles in the family Tenebrionidae, found as adults on flowers, foliage, and stems of herbaceous plants and low shrubs in Amsterdam parks and rough grassland from late spring onwards. Adults feed on pollen and plant tissue and are commonly observed on umbellifer and composite flower heads, making flower-visitor the appropriate dominant habitat. Larvae live in soil and leaf litter feeding on decaying plant material, so ground-park is a secondary. The flower-bobber primary movement fits adult flower-foraging; idle-only secondary covers the prolonged resting periods on stems between flower visits.
