The Green-veined White is one of Amsterdam's most familiar butterflies, found in parks, gardens, cemetery verges, and along canal-side vegetation throughout the flying season from April to October. It differs from the Large and Small Whites by preferring damp, vegetated habitats and by having green-veined underwings that provide superb camouflage when resting on leaves. Larvae feed on garlic mustard, cuckooflower, and other wild Brassicaceae, plants common in Amsterdam's shadier green spaces. Adults are avid flower visitors, nectaring at a wide range of blooms, making the default flower-visitor placement well suited. Multiple broods are produced each year with populations peaking in late spring and late summer.
