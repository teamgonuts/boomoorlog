The nut weevil (Curculio nucum) is a hazel-specialist curculionid whose larvae develop inside hazelnuts (Corylus). The female drills through the nut shell with her long rostrum to lay a single egg per nut; larvae then consume the kernel. Adult beetles feed on hazel leaves and buds in the canopy during spring and are sluggish walkers, spending most of their time stationary. The insect category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is overridden to tree-canopy / idle-only because the ecological focus is entirely on hazel nuts and foliage, and the adult's on-tree behaviour is characteristically immobile.
