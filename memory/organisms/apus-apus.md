The Common Swift is the most aerial of all Amsterdam's breeding birds — it feeds, drinks, mates, and even sleeps on the wing, descending to its nest cavity only for the briefest possible time. It is a familiar summer presence over Amsterdam from May to August, screaming in parties over canal water and city squares where insect prey concentrates. The swift literally cannot perch: its feet are adapted only for clinging to vertical surfaces at the nest hole entrance, so sky-only is the only correct habitat class. Sky-looper captures the long curved flight paths it traces over open sky above the city's waterways and squares. The default bird tag of tree-canopy / tree-flitter is entirely wrong.
