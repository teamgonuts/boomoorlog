Greater Celandine is a tall perennial poppy-relative with bright yellow four-petalled flowers and toxic orange sap, strongly associated with disturbed urban ground, old walls, rubble, and the base of buildings throughout Amsterdam. It is one of the most characteristic urban nitrophilous weeds, colonising pavement cracks, garden walls, and the rubble margins of streets as readily as park undergrowth. The dominant habitat is ground-urban given its strong preference for built-up and wall-base micro-habitats, with ground-park as a secondary. Movement stays none.
