The Hackberry Emperor (Asterocampa celtis) is a North American butterfly whose sole larval host is Celtis (hackberry), the same tree genus present in Amsterdam as an introduced species. This butterfly is native to North America and is not established in Amsterdam or Europe; its inclusion in the dataset reflects an observation tied to the Celtis tree host rather than an established Amsterdam population. Its ecology: adults glide and perch high in hackberry canopy, rarely visiting flowers (they prefer tree sap, rotting fruit, and carrion). Tree-canopy is the most accurate habitat; flower-bobber is retained from the insect default as the closest available movement class for a butterfly, though this species is atypically non-floral.
