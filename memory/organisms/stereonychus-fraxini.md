The ash weevil feeds on the buds and young leaves of Fraxinus in spring, and the larvae develop inside the leaflets where they cause characteristic leaf-roll damage. Adults are small and cryptic, sitting still on ash foliage or bark rather than visiting flowers. Amsterdam has many ash street trees and park specimens, and the weevil is regularly recorded on them. Tree-canopy replaces the insect default of flower-visitor because the entire life cycle is tied to ash foliage; idle-only replaces flower-bobber because the weevil's movement is negligible at the scale of a map sprite.
