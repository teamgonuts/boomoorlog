This row groups treecreeper (Certhia familiaris) and Eurasian nuthatch (Sitta europaea), both of which forage on the bark of large-diameter trees in Amsterdam parks; Pterocarya (Caucasian wingnut) develops a deeply furrowed bark that is attractive to both species. Nuthatches descend head-first, treecreepers spiral upward, but both spend their active time on bark surfaces. The shared tree-bark habitat is correct; idle-only movement reflects the slow bark-creeping visible in the simulation rather than active flight between trees. Override from the bird default for both constituent species.
