Silverweed is a prostrate perennial with feathery silver-green pinnate leaves and bright yellow flowers, spreading by long surface runners. It favours damp, compacted, or disturbed ground — footpath edges, trampled lawns, and damp depressions in parks. The silvery leaf undersides are diagnostic. In Amsterdam it is commonly found on the margins of park paths and in areas of light foot traffic where grass is thin. Default ground-park and none are appropriate; it is a low herb of open, often slightly disturbed vegetated ground.
