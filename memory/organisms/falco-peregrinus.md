The Peregrine Falcon nests on ledges of tall Amsterdam buildings and church towers, most famously the Westertoren and structures in the port area, making wall-and-roof the dominant placement habitat. From these high perches it launches into powerful stooping dives over open city sky, justifying sky-only as a secondary habitat. Sky-looper is the dominant movement class for the long soaring and hunting circuits it flies over open water and parks; idle-only is secondary for the extended periods it perches motionless on a building edge scanning for prey.
