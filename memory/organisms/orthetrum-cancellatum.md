The Black-tailed Skimmer is a robust dragonfly that breeds in still and slow-moving water across Amsterdam, with males establishing territories over open water surfaces and bare sunny banks. Males characteristically perch motionless on bare ground, stones, or low vegetation at the water's edge — the idle-only posture that observers most often photograph — then launch into brief aerial patrols. The sky-looper secondary captures these fast hunting and territorial sorties over open water. Larval development takes one to two years in the muddy bottom of ponds and slow canals. Adults are on the wing from May to August.
