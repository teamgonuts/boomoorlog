Colletes is a genus of solitary mining bees (Colletidae) that nest in sandy or bare soil burrows while foraging on flowers, often showing strong oligolecty on specific plant families. In Amsterdam, species like Colletes daviesanus and C. hederae are recorded on composite flowers and ivy in parks, cemeteries, and sandy urban soils. The flower-visitor/flower-bobber default fits well for foraging adults, with a sky-looper secondary to capture transit flight between foraging patches. Ground-park is added as a secondary for the nesting behaviour in bare soil.
