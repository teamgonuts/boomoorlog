The Brown rat is Amsterdam's most abundant and widespread urban mammal, foraging nightly along canal sides, under street furniture, and in pavement gaps throughout the city. Ground-urban is dominant because the bulk of activity and visibility is on paved surfaces near food sources — cafés, markets, bins, and canal banks — while water-edge is secondary because Amsterdam's canal network provides ideal burrowing banks and foraging margins. Urban-walker fits the short-distance scurrying along streets and alleys, with park-roamer as secondary for rats that range into park green. The mammal default of ground-park / park-roamer understates the urban pavement dominance for this species.
