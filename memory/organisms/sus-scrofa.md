Wild boar root for mast beneath Fagus and Robinia in forested park areas, turning over the soil surface with their snouts in classic park-roamer fashion. In Amsterdam they are occasional visitors to the edges of the Amsterdamse Bos from the surrounding polder landscape rather than permanent residents, but their association with beech mast makes the park setting correct. Ground-park and park-roamer are both accurate defaults for a large root-foraging mammal. The species is unlikely to be encountered in the inner city but is plausible in larger green zones recorded in the iNaturalist dataset.
