The Six-spot Burnet is a day-flying moth with striking metallic green forewings and six crimson spots, one of the few moths commonly seen visiting flowers in bright sunshine. In Amsterdam it occurs on rough grassland, cemetery margins, and park edges with Bird's-foot Trefoil (Lotus corniculatus), on which the larvae feed. Adults are strong flower visitors — particularly on Knautia, Centaurea, and thistles — moving deliberately between flower heads (flower-bobber dominant) and resting on the flower with wings half-open (idle-only secondary).
