The European Rabbit is a common sight in Amsterdam's larger parks and dune edges, particularly in Amsterdamse Bos and Vondelpark, where it grazes on open lawns and shelters in burrows dug into sandy soil. It is a ground-dwelling species entirely dependent on vegetated open space, making ground-park the clear dominant habitat. Its movement is a characteristic stop-start grazing pattern — a short hop, a pause to listen, another hop — which maps directly onto the park-roamer class. No override is needed as the mammal defaults fit well.
