Apis mellifera, the western honey bee, is one of Amsterdam's most widely observed pollinators and appears on 20 different tree genera in this dataset — the broadest tree association of any single species in the batch. It forages on everything from Tilia (lime) to Robinia (black locust) to Magnolia and Salix, covering virtually every flowering street tree in the city. Amsterdam supports managed hive colonies on rooftops and in parks, and feral colonies in tree cavities. The flower-visitor placement and flower-bobber animation are ideal: honey bees are most visible on blooming canopy trees and move with rapid, purposeful inter-flower hops. Out of bloom season they are effectively invisible, which the flower-visitor fallback logic handles correctly.
