Schoenobius gigantella is a large crambid moth whose larvae are specialist stem-borers in common reed (Phragmites australis) and other emergent water-margin grasses. The entire lifecycle is tied to reedbed and water-edge habitats — adults rest on reed stems during the day, camouflaged against the pale stems. Water-edge is the correct habitat override from the insect default of flower-visitor, since this species is strictly associated with emergent marginal vegetation rather than open flowers. Adults are day-resting on reed stems, making idle-only the right movement class. It is recorded in Amsterdam in the Amstelpark reedbeds and along canal margins with standing Phragmites.
