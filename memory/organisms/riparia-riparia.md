The Sand Martin is a small aerial insectivore that arrives in Amsterdam in spring and hunts low over open water and wetlands, catching insects on the wing in sweeping curved flights. It nests colonially in sandy riverbanks and canal embankments, making water-edge a genuine secondary habitat where it perches briefly near nest entrances. Almost all visible time is spent in continuous aerial loops (sky-looper dominant), with brief idle perches at nest holes or wires (idle-only secondary). Overridden from the default tree-canopy/tree-flitter because this species barely interacts with trees at all; it is a true sky bird that shares the sky-only + sky-looper profile of the swift family.
