Xylotrechus chinensis is a longhorn beetle (Cerambycidae) originally from Asia, recorded as a pest of Morus (mulberry) in Europe. Larvae bore deeply into the woody tissue and bark of mulberry trees, causing significant structural damage. Adults emerge in summer and rest on the bark surface or chew at twigs in the canopy. Tree-bark is dominant as the primary habitat for both larvae and resting adults; tree-canopy secondary captures adult twig feeding and egg-laying on smaller branches. Idle-only is appropriate — adults rest or move slowly on bark surfaces, not visiting flowers.
