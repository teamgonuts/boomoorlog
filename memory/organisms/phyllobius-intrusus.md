Phyllobius intrusus is a leaf-notching weevil recorded on Thuja (arborvitae) in Amsterdam, where adults feed on the scale-like foliage in the canopy. Adults are slow-moving, cryptic beetles that cling to foliage and move only short distances when disturbed. Unlike the default insect category of flower-visitors, this species is a foliage-feeder with no pollinator role, so tree-canopy and idle-only are the correct tags. Adults drop to the ground to overwinter in the soil, but their observable phase on Thuja is canopy-based.
