The woolly beech aphid is a distinctive species whose white waxy secretions make colonies immediately visible on the undersides of beech (Fagus) leaves in Amsterdam parks. Dense colonies cover the leaves from spring until early summer, feeding on phloem sap without any ground-foraging or flower-visiting behavior. Tree-canopy is the precise habitat since colonies are restricted to the foliage of Fagus, and idle-only reflects the sedentary clustered lifestyle of the apterous colony stages. The default flower-visitor and flower-bobber tags are completely inapplicable to this specialist tree-canopy aphid.
