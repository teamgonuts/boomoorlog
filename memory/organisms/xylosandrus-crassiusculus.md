The Granulate Ambrosia Beetle (Xylosandrus crassiusculus) is an invasive bark beetle from East Asia that bores into the wood of stressed trees, including Liquidambar (sweetgum) in Amsterdam. Females excavate galleries in the bark and woody tissue where they cultivate ambrosia fungi for food. The entire lifecycle — boring, feeding, breeding — occurs inside the bark and wood. Tree-bark is the sole appropriate habitat tag. Idle-only overrides the flower-bobber default since this beetle does not visit flowers; it spends its adult life boring into and resting within bark.
