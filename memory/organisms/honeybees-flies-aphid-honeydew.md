This entry captures the assemblage of honeybees and various flies observed feeding on aphid honeydew secreted onto Juglans foliage, rather than visiting flowers directly. In Amsterdam walnut trees in parks produce sticky honeydew-coated leaves that attract Apis mellifera and calyptrate flies in large numbers. The habitat is firmly tree-canopy (honeydew is collected on foliage and branches) rather than flower-visitor despite honeybees being classic pollinators. Flower-bobber is the closest movement analogue as the insects flit between sticky leaf clusters in a similar pattern to flower-to-flower visits. The override reflects the atypical non-floral food source driving their canopy placement.
