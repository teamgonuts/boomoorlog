Common Ragwort is a biennial to short-lived perennial with bright yellow daisy-like flowers that is widespread on park margins, railway embankments, sandy verges, and unmown grassland across Amsterdam. It is the obligate foodplant of the orange-and-black Cinnabar moth caterpillar, making it an ecologically important species despite being legally controlled in some contexts. The ground-park default fits accurately; the plant needs open, often disturbed or nutrient-poor ground in vegetated settings.
