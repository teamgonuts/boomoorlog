Black Mustard is a tall annual archaeophyte of disturbed river margins, waste ground, and rough park edges, favoured by nutrient-rich alluvial soils. In Amsterdam it is particularly characteristic of the banks of the Amstel and the older canal edges where soil disturbance regularly occurs. The bright yellow flowers are visited by a range of generalist pollinators. The default ground-park / none pairing is accurate; this is a rooted annual with no special habitat requirements that would need an override.
