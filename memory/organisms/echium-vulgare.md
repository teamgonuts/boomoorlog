Viper's-bugloss is a biennial wildflower found on dry, disturbed ground in Amsterdam — roadsides, railway embankments, former industrial sites, and sandy park edges — where it forms striking colonies of vivid blue flower spikes in summer. It is a calcareous or sandy-soil specialist and is not particularly common in the city centre, though it appears reliably along the fringes of Amsterdamse Bos and on disturbed substrates near the ring roads. As a non-aquatic vascular plant rooted in the ground it fits the plant category default of ground-park / none exactly. It is also one of the most important nectar plants for bumblebees and specialist solitary bees in Amsterdam, though that is a property of its visitors, not of the plant itself.
