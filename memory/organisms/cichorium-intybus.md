Chicory is a tall, blue-flowered perennial of roadsides, railway embankments, and disturbed urban ground, making ground-urban the more accurate dominant habitat over the plant default of ground-park. Its vivid sky-blue ray florets open only in the morning and are highly attractive to bees and hoverflies. In Amsterdam it colonises dry, compacted soils along verges, cycle paths, and neglected plots, thriving in the nitrogen-rich conditions of urban margins. The tap root is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to persist in poor urban substrates. Observations are most frequent from July to September when it is in full bloom.
