Yorkshire-fog is a softly hairy perennial grass that is extremely common in Amsterdam's parks, meadow strips, and roadside verges. Its pinkish-green flower heads and distinctly velvety leaves make it identifiable even from a distance. It tolerates a wide range of soil conditions and is one of the most frequently recorded grasses in urban green space across the Netherlands. It provides cover and nesting material for small invertebrates. Default ground-park and none are appropriate.
