The lackey moth is a native European lasiocampid whose gregarious caterpillars spin communal silk tents on Cornus, Prunus, and other woody shrubs and trees found in Amsterdam parks and hedgerows. Larvae feed on foliage and are most conspicuous as large silken colonies on branches — tree-canopy dominant — but the egg rings and resting clusters wrap bark — tree-bark secondary. Adult moths are nocturnal and short-lived; the conspicuous life stage is the caterpillar, which barely migrates from its feeding branch, making idle-only the right animation class.
