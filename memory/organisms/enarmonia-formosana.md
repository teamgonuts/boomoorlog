Enarmonia formosana (cherry bark tortrix) larvae bore into the bark and cambium of Prunus trees, creating characteristic gummy entry wounds on trunks and main branches. Argyresthia pruniella larvae mine cherry fruitlets. Both moths rest motionless on bark surfaces during the day. The dominant habitat for this pairing is tree-bark rather than canopy because the most diagnostic and damaging life stage — the bark-boring larva — is anchored to the trunk surface. Idle-only replaces flower-bobber; adults are nocturnal and the observable bark-damage signs are completely stationary.
