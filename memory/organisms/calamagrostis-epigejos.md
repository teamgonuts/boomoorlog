Wood Small-reed is a tall, robust grass that forms dense clonal stands on disturbed, often dry or semi-shaded ground — found in Amsterdam on railway embankments, park edges, brownfield sites, and occasionally on drier canal banks. It is wind-pollinated and flowers conspicuously in summer with large, feathery purple-tinged panicles. Ground-park is the dominant habitat as it colonises rough and semi-natural ground in parks and urban margins; water-edge is secondary because it does occur on drier canal banks and embankments. It is also an important larval foodplant for several grass-feeding moth species. Overridden from the default single ground-park to add the water-edge secondary.
