Leafcutter bees (Megachile spp.) are specialist pollinators that carry pollen on their abdominal scopae rather than corbiculae, visiting a range of flowers in parks and urban gardens. Females are characteristic for cutting neat discs from rose and other smooth leaves to line their nest cells in hollow stems and bee hotels. The default flower-visitor / flower-bobber combination fits perfectly, with a sky-looper secondary for the between-patch transit flights that are visible in Amsterdam's community gardens and park flower beds. Several Megachile species are regularly recorded in central Amsterdam.
