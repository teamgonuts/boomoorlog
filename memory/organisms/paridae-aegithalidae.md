This entry groups the core tit assemblage — blue tit (Cyanistes caeruleus), great tit (Parus major), and long-tailed tit (Aegithalos caudatus) — observed together on Crataegus in Amsterdam parks and gardens. All three species are archetypal canopy insectivores that move in restless mixed flocks, hopping and flitting between branches and trees throughout the year. The default bird tags of tree-canopy and tree-flitter capture this behavior precisely. Long-tailed tits also use hedgerow-style shrubs, but Crataegus qualifies as tree-canopy placement in the taxonomy.
