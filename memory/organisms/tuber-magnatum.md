Tuber magnatum is the prized white truffle, a subterranean mycorrhizal fungus that forms associations with the roots of deciduous trees including hornbeam (Ostrya). In Amsterdam it would grow belowground in the soil near its tree host, making it a ground-park organism rather than the bark-surface default for fungi. It is entirely sessile — a static subterranean body — so none is the correct movement class. The override from tree-bark to ground-park reflects that truffles fruit underground, not on bark surfaces.
