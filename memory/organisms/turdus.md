Song Thrush, Redwing, and Fieldfare are thrushes that visit Amsterdam parks and gardens, especially in autumn and winter when they feed heavily on berries from Cornus, Ilex, Morus, Taxus, and Magnolia. They move between fruiting trees in the canopy but spend substantial time on the ground, hunting earthworms and snails in lawn and park soil. The ground-park secondary habitat captures their frequent ground foraging, and park-roamer secondary movement reflects the characteristically bouncy hopping these thrushes use to work open ground.
