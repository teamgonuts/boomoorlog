The brimstone moth (Opisthograptis luteolata) and grey dagger moth (Acronicta psi) are both recorded as caterpillars on Amelanchier and Prunus in Amsterdam. Their larvae feed on foliage in the tree canopy throughout spring and summer, making tree-canopy the accurate placement habitat. Neither species is a flower-forager in its caterpillar stage, so the default insect tags are overridden. Adult moths are nocturnal and short-lived; the larval stage that appears in the observations is sedentary, justifying idle-only movement.
