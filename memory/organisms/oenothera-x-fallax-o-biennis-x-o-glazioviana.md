Intermediate Evening-Primrose is a hybrid evening-primrose that, like its parents, thrives on dry, disturbed, and often sandy or rubble-strewn ground. It is characteristic of railway embankments, roadsides, dune edges, and wasteland rather than managed park lawns. In Amsterdam it colonises degraded urban ground, road margins, and demolition sites. Its large yellow flowers open in the evening and are visited by hawk-moths. ground-urban better reflects its preference for disturbed urban substrates over maintained park vegetation. Movement is none.
