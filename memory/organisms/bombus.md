Bombus bumblebees are among the most important pollinators in Amsterdam, visiting Catalpa, Cercidiphyllum, Cercis, Liriodendron, and Robinia — a suite of large, exotic-flowering trees that attract both short-tongued and long-tongued species. Amsterdam supports a healthy diversity of Bombus species including B. terrestris, B. lapidarius, B. pascuorum, and B. hortorum. Bumblebees are primarily flower-visitors (their most visible and ecologically important behavior) but also make substantial transit flights between flower patches, justifying the sky-looper secondary movement class per the taxonomy examples. The flower-bobber dominant movement captures inter-bloom foraging; sky-looper captures the longer search flights queen and worker bees make between sites.
