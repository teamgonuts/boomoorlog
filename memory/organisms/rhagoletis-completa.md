The Walnut husk fly is a specialist fruit fly whose larvae develop inside the green husk of Juglans nuts, staining the kernel black and rendering it inedible. In Amsterdam it has established on ornamental and productive walnut trees, with adults resting cryptically on foliage in the canopy and larvae spending their entire feeding phase inside the ripening fruit. Adults do occasionally visit flowers for nectar but their defining ecological role is canopy-based oviposition on ripening fruit, so tree-canopy and idle-only better represent the animal than the insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber.
