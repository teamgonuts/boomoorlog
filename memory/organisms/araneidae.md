Araneidae (orb-weavers) and Theridiidae (cobweb spiders) are generalist web-building spiders that construct webs in the canopy of Ailanthus altissima (tree of heaven) in Amsterdam. Ailanthus is a vigorous coloniser of disturbed urban sites and its open branching architecture makes it an ideal frame for spider webs. These spiders spend most of their time stationary at the hub of their webs waiting for prey, making idle-only the correct movement class. Tree-canopy overrides the arachnid default (anywhere) because the observation context is specifically canopy web habitat; this is a more precise and placement-useful tag than the generic fallback.
