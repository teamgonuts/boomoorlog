The Rose-ringed Parakeet is now an established Amsterdam bird, with a growing population centred on parks with mature Platanus and other large trees where it excavates or usurps cavity roosts. It forages almost entirely in the canopy — feeding on buds, fruits, and seeds — and moves noisily between trees in small groups, matching the tree-flitter movement exactly. In Amsterdam it is most conspicuous in Vondelpark, Sloterpark, and Beatrixpark. The bird default of tree-canopy / tree-flitter fits without modification.
