Bradyrhizobium is a genus of nitrogen-fixing soil bacteria that form symbiotic root nodules on leguminous trees, here specifically on Cercis siliquastrum (Judas tree) in Amsterdam. The bacteria live inside root nodules in the soil, fixing atmospheric nitrogen into forms usable by the host tree. They have no independent movement and are invisible to the naked eye — they are effectively sedentary soil microorganisms. Ground-park is the most accurate habitat (they exist in the soil under the tree), and none (static sprite) is the only logical movement class. This is a significant override from the other category defaults (anywhere / idle-only): a soil bacterium has a fixed, underground location.
