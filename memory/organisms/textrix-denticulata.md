The Toothed Weaver is a funnel-web spider in the family Agelenidae that is strongly associated with old stone walls, rock faces, and the base of building walls rather than open ground. It builds a distinctive flat, slightly funnelled sheet web in crevices in masonry and at the junction of walls and paving. Wall-and-roof is therefore the appropriate habitat override from the arachnid default of anywhere. It is completely sedentary in its web, making idle-only the correct and default movement class. In Amsterdam it turns up in canal-wall crevices and on the rough stonework of older buildings.
