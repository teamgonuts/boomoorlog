The White-letter Hairstreak is a specialist butterfly almost entirely dependent on Ulmus, whose larvae feed on elm flowers and young leaves in the canopy in spring. Adults spend most of their time resting on elm foliage and visiting aphid honeydew high in the canopy, rarely descending, so tree-canopy is the dominant habitat. Flower-visitor is a secondary because adults do take nectar from bramble, privet, and other flowers when these are accessible near elms. Movement is primarily idle-only (perching motionless for long periods on leaves) with flower-bobber as a secondary for the brief nectaring excursions — a different profile from the insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber alone.
