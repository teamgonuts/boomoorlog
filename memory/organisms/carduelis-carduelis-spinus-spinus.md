Goldfinch and Eurasian siskin (Spinus spinus) frequently form mixed feeding flocks on seed-bearing trees in Amsterdam, with Ostrya carpinifolia (hop-hornbeam) providing the seeds that draw this observation. Siskins are specialist canopy feeders on alder and hop-hornbeam catkins, hanging acrobatically from thin branches; goldfinches behave similarly. Both species are small, agile songbirds whose dominant behaviour is short canopy flights between feeding trees, so the default tree-canopy / tree-flitter classification applies without modification.
