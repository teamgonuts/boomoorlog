This grouped entry combines wood mice and bank voles (ground-dwelling small rodents) with squirrels (partly arboreal) observed on Fagus (beech) in Amsterdam's parks. The dominant habitat is ground-park because mice and voles forage primarily on the forest floor and among root systems, but the tree-canopy secondary reflects the squirrels' regular use of the canopy for movement and caching. Similarly, park-roamer is the dominant movement for the nocturnal rodents while tree-flitter captures squirrel movement between branches. The combination is justified because this slug groups ecologically distinct animals; the dominant tags reflect the mice/voles which likely outnumber squirrels in observations.
