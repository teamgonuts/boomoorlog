Prociphilus species form waxy woolly colonies on the roots and bark of Fraxinus, while Aphis fabae feeds on young shoots and leaves in the canopy of the same tree in Amsterdam street plantings. Both are sedentary once settled, forming dense stationary colonies rather than flying between flowers, so the insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber does not apply. Tree-canopy is dominant because the most visible infestations coat new growth and leaf undersides, with tree-bark as secondary for the bark-root colonies. Movement is idle-only: aphid movement is imperceptible at map scale.
