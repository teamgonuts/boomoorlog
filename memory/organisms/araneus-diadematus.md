The Garden Cross Spider builds large orb-webs in shrubs, between tree branches, and across garden paths in Amsterdam parks and canal-side vegetation. It is most visible in late summer and autumn when the adult females reach full size and their webs catch the morning dew. Although the category default places arachnids at `anywhere`, this species has a clear preference for shrubby tree-canopy edges and park margins, making tree-canopy the dominant habitat. Like all web-sitting spiders it is functionally idle-only in animation terms.
