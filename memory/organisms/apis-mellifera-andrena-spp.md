This grouped entry covers honey bees (Apis mellifera) alongside solitary mining bees (Andrena spp.) observed on Malus (crabapple / ornamental apple) in Amsterdam. Malus is a popular street and park tree whose spring blossoms attract dense concentrations of both managed honey bees and numerous ground-nesting Andrena species. Andrena bees nest in bare soil patches in parks but forage almost exclusively on flowers, so the flower-visitor habitat is the most visible and meaningful placement. The shared flower-bobber movement class captures both species foraging behavior accurately during the blooming season.
