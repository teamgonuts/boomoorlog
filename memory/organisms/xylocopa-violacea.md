The Violet Carpenter Bee (Xylocopa violacea) is the largest bee in Europe and a climate-change beneficiary now appearing in Amsterdam. It is attracted to Catalpa (Indian bean tree) flowers, which it can access thanks to its size. Like all Xylocopa, females nest in dry woody materials — fence posts, structural timber — mapped to wall-and-roof here. Flower-visitor dominates as the primary foraging habitat; wall-and-roof secondary captures the nesting site. Its powerful, audible flight makes sky-looper the appropriate secondary movement for transit between flower patches.
