This group covers several moth species whose caterpillars feed on Malus (apple/crab apple): the eyed hawk-moth (Smerinthus ocellata), pale tussock (Calliteara pudibunda), green pug (Pasiphila rectangulata), and Chinese character (Cilix glaucata). As caterpillars they live and feed on tree foliage and bark in the canopy. As adult moths they rest motionless on bark or foliage during the day. Tree-canopy is dominant for larval feeding on leaves; tree-bark secondary captures adult resting posture. Idle-only is correct — resting moths do not move, and the flower-bobber default does not fit.
