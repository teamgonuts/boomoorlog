Ips typographus is the most destructive spruce bark beetle in Europe, boring through the outer bark of Picea to lay eggs and construct breeding galleries in the phloem. In Amsterdam, ornamental Picea trees in parks can be colonised particularly when weakened by drought, and the beetle has expanded its range under climate warming. The organism lives its entire productive life on and beneath the bark surface — tree-bark is the definitive habitat. Movement is idle-only as the beetle is visible only as an exit-hole cluster on bark; the boring activity is below the animation threshold. The override is necessary given the bark-dwelling lifestyle versus the insect flower-visitor default.
