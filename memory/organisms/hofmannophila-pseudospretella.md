The Brown House Moth is a synanthropic moth that lives almost exclusively indoors or in sheltered urban structures, colonising stored organic material, wool, feathers, and dried foodstuffs. Its adult stage is short-lived and rests motionless on walls and ceilings during the day, making it effectively an urban-interior species rather than a flower-visitor. The default insect flower-visitor / flower-bobber pairing is biologically wrong here: this moth has no meaningful relationship with flowers and is never found in open park vegetation. Ground-urban with idle-only best captures its wall-resting adult behaviour in Amsterdam's dense built environment.
