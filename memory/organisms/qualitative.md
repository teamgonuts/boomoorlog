This qualitative grouping covers various moth and butterfly larvae that can feed on Styphnolobium japonicum (Japanese pagoda tree), a relatively toxic leguminous street tree in Amsterdam that most herbivores avoid. The caterpillars feed in the canopy foliage and move slowly along branches — imperceptible at map scale — making idle-only the correct movement class. Tree-canopy is the exclusive habitat because these are foliage-feeding larvae with no association with flowers or bark surfaces. The insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber does not fit leaf-feeding caterpillars, requiring an override.
