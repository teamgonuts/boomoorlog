The Brown Chafer is a small, nocturnally active scarab beetle widespread in Amsterdam's parks and sandy green spaces where its larvae spend two or three years feeding on grass roots and decaying organic matter in the soil. Adults emerge in summer on warm evenings and fly to deciduous trees and shrubs to feed on foliage, but during daylight hours they bury into loose soil or remain hidden at the base of vegetation. The larval stage — lasting the vast majority of the life cycle — is entirely subterranean in park lawns, anchoring this species firmly in ground-park. The insect category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber does not fit a largely nocturnal, soil-larval chafer beetle; idle-only captures the cryptic daytime resting behaviour of the adult.
