Nipplewort is a slender annual composite that favours shaded and semi-shaded ground: the bases of park hedges, woodland edges, the dappled floor beneath canal-side trees, and shaded garden corners throughout Amsterdam. Its small pale-yellow flowerheads are easily overlooked but consistent through summer. The default ground-park placement is accurate, as it needs vegetated shaded soil rather than bare paved ground. It is often found growing alongside goosegrass and nettles in the shadier corners of Vondelpark.
