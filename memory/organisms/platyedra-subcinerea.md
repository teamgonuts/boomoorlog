Platyedra subcinerea is a small gelechiid micro-moth whose larvae feed inside the seed capsules of mallow family plants (Malvaceae), particularly Malva and Althaea, which are ground-level park plants. Adults are nocturnal and rest cryptically during the day on low vegetation or substrate, making them invisible in day simulation. Neither flower-visitor nor flower-bobber is appropriate since this is a moth that conceals itself at rest and visits flowers only for nocturnal nectaring not easily represented. Ground-park captures both the larval host-plant habitat and the adult resting microhabitat. Idle-only reflects the resting adult posture that would be visible in daytime rendering.
