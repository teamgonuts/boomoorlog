The hawthorn leaf beetle is a small chrysomelid that feeds exclusively on Crataegus foliage both as larva and adult, making tree-canopy its clear dominant habitat in Amsterdam's park and street hawthorn plantings. Adults are slow walkers across leaf surfaces; they sit motionless for long periods and do not take meaningful positional flight between trees often enough to warrant tree-flitter. Idle-only correctly captures this almost stationary leaf-surface existence. Populations in Amsterdam track closely with ornamental hawthorn availability and are most visible from late spring through summer.
