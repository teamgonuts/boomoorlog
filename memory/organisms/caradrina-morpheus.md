The Mottled Rustic is a small nocturnal noctuid moth that rests by day on bark, walls, and rough surfaces where its grey-brown mottled pattern provides effective camouflage. It flies from June to August and is attracted to light, making light-trap observations common in Amsterdam gardens and parks. Larvae feed on a range of low herbaceous plants including docks, plantains, and grasses in urban green spaces. The bark-resting daytime posture is the appearance that most observers photograph, making tree-bark the appropriate dominant habitat. Like most resting moths, it shows only the subtle idle movement of gentle wing tension adjustments.
