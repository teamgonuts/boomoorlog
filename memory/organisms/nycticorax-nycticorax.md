The Black-crowned Night Heron is a compact, stocky heron that roosts in trees and shrubs by day and forages at water edges from dusk onwards. Amsterdam has a significant breeding colony, particularly around the Vondelpark and the Herengracht area, and the species is regularly seen hunched motionless on canal railings or overhanging branches during daytime hours. Its nocturnal behaviour means it is more often encountered as a static silhouette than as an active hunter. water-edge is the dominant placement; idle-only is the primary movement reflecting daytime roosting, with water-edge-stalker as a secondary for dusk/night animation. The default tree-canopy / tree-flitter does not apply.
