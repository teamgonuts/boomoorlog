This grouped entry combines the Bohemian waxwing with two common Amsterdam frugivores — the blackbird (Turdus merula) and mistle thrush — observed on Celtis and Sorbus. Waxwings and mistle thrushes are strictly canopy fruit-feeders, but blackbirds are strongly dual-habitat: they forage extensively on the ground under fruiting trees for worms and fallen berries, making the ground-park secondary habitat and park-roamer secondary movement genuinely accurate for the group. The dominant tags reflect the canopy-feeding waxwing and mistle thrush components; the secondaries capture the blackbird's consistent ground-foraging behavior that distinguishes it from the other two species.
