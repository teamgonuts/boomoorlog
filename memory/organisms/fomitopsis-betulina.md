Fomitopsis betulina (formerly Piptoporus betulinus) is a bracket fungus exclusive to birch trees, forming large kidney-shaped fruiting bodies on the bark of Betula trunks in Amsterdam parks and cemeteries. The Mycetophilidae fungus gnats listed here breed inside the decaying bracket, living out their larval stages within the sporocarp tissue — effectively co-located with the fungus on bark. As a fungal organism the category default of tree-bark / none applies cleanly, with the gnat association considered incidental to placement. The bracket is strictly non-motile and the static sprite is appropriate.
