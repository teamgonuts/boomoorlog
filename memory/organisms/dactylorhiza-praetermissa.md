The Southern Marsh-orchid is a native terrestrial orchid that grows in damp, nutrient-poor grasslands, marshes, and the wet margins of ditches and canals, making water-edge the dominant habitat. It is one of the more frequent wild orchids in the Amsterdam region, found in nature reserves, damp meadow remnants, and dike sides in and around the city. Its tall spikes of purple-pink flowers appear from May to July and are important for solitary bees. Being an orchid it is non-mycorrhizal-fungus-dependent for germination, and its populations can be sensitive to drainage changes and grassland management. The ground-park secondary covers damp amenity grassland sites within parks.
