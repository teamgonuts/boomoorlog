The Common Tern is a migratory seabird that breeds on pontoons and artificial platforms on Amsterdam's larger open waterways including the IJ and surrounding lakes. It spends most of its visible time hovering and plunge-diving over open water, making sky-only the dominant placement. Brief rests occur on buoys and floating platforms at water level. Its rapid, buoyant flight with deep wingbeats is very distinctive, and its piercing calls draw attention well above the canal surface. In Amsterdam it is most abundant from late April through August during the breeding season.
