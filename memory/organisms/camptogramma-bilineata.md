The Yellow Shell is a geometrid moth with distinctive yellow-and-brown banded wings, commonly found in parks, garden walls, hedgerows, and road verges across Amsterdam from June to August. Larvae feed on low-growing plants including chickweeds, docks, and nettles. Adults rest by day on vegetation, walls, or fences, folding their wings flat and relying on pattern camouflage. The default flower-visitor / flower-bobber profile does not suit a wall-resting moth; ground-park is dominant (larval and general habitat) with ground-urban secondary for urban-wall resting sites. Idle-only is correct.
