Jadera haematoloma is a seed-feeding rhopalid bug originally from North America that has become established in Europe wherever its host Koelreuteria paniculata (goldenrain tree) is planted. In Amsterdam, Koelreuteria is used as an ornamental street and park tree and Jadera aggregates in large numbers on fallen seed pods beneath the trees, feeding on seeds at ground level. Ground-park is the dominant habitat because the bugs feed on fallen seeds under the tree rather than in the canopy. They are sedentary seed-suckers and idle-only in movement terms.
