The codling moth is the classic apple and pear fruit borer, with larvae tunnelling inside the developing fruit of Juglans, Malus and Pyrus. In Amsterdam's urban orchards and park trees these moths are present throughout summer, but adults are cryptic night-fliers that rest immobile on bark or foliage during daylight. The larval stage is hidden entirely within fruit tissue and the adult rarely moves when at rest. Tree-canopy placement is correct since the lifecycle is centred on the fruit-bearing canopy; idle-only replaces the inappropriate flower-bobber default because there is no meaningful positional movement to animate.
