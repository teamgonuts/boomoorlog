This entry captures Blue Tit and Great Tit observations specifically associated with hazel (Corylus) trees in Amsterdam. Both species exploit hazel catkins for seeds in late winter and systematically probe hazel twigs for aphids and moth larvae in summer. Their behaviour is identical to the main slug: rapid canopy foraging with frequent hops between branches and nearby trees. The tree-canopy habitat and tree-flitter movement defaults apply without modification. Being observed on a single Corylus genus makes no difference to how they are animated or placed.
