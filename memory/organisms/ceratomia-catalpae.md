The catalpa sphinx (catalpa hawkmoth) is a North American sphingid moth whose larvae defoliate Catalpa trees; it has been recorded in the Netherlands as an introduced pest on ornamental Catalpa bignonioides, which is planted in Amsterdam parks for its large leaves and showy flowers. Caterpillars cluster on leaves and feed voraciously within the canopy, while the nocturnal adults are seldom visible; the larval stage is the ecologically dominant phase.
