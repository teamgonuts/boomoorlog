The domestic duck is a feral descendant of the mallard, ubiquitous on Amsterdam's canals and park ponds. Like mallards, it drifts along waterways and frequently begs for bread on canal banks and paved squares. Plumage varies enormously — white, brown, piebald — making it visually distinctive but behaviourally identical to its wild relative. It tolerates dense human presence and regularly forages on pavements, earning a dual water-surface and ground-urban placement. Multi-movement is appropriate: water-drifter dominates, with urban-walker firing whenever it waddles onto adjacent paving.
