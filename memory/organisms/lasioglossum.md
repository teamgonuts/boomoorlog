Halictid sweat bees are small ground-nesting solitary bees observed visiting Gleditsia (honey locust) flowers in Amsterdam, a tree common along streets and in parks. While adults visit flowers for pollen and nectar — fitting the flower-visitor default — females also nest in bare or sparsely vegetated ground in parks, requiring a secondary ground-park tag. Flower-bobber is the dominant movement for foraging adults, with park-roamer secondary for ground-level nest-searching and nest approach behaviour. The override adds the ground-park secondary to the otherwise default-fitting insect tags.
