Chaffinch and Brambling are common finches in Amsterdam's beech-dominated parks, observed feeding in Fagus canopy and descending in flocks to forage on fallen beech mast on the ground below. The dual habitat reflects this well-documented alternation: canopy for singing, roosting and invertebrate gleaning, ground-park for seed foraging under the trees in autumn and winter. Accordingly tree-flitter is dominant but park-roamer captures the ground-feeding flocks. The override is warranted because these species demonstrably split time between canopy and ground more than a default small passerine would.
