Oak gall wasps (Cynipidae) are a diverse family of tiny parasitoid wasps whose larvae develop entirely inside galls they induce on Quercus leaves, buds, catkins, acorns or roots. Amsterdam's oak trees host dozens of cynipid species producing the characteristic spherical, spangle, cherry and artichoke galls visible in parks and along streets. Adults are minute, dark wasps that are rarely visible in flight; the observable manifestation of their presence is always the stationary gall structure anchored in the canopy.
