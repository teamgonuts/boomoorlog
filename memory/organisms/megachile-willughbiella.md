Willughby's leafcutter bee is a large, robust solitary bee that visits a wide range of flowers for pollen and nectar, and is particularly attracted to Fabaceae and various garden plants. Females cut neat semicircular sections from leaves to line their nest cells, which are built in hollow plant stems or soil cavities. In Amsterdam it is regularly observed in parks and gardens throughout summer. The insect default of flower-visitor and flower-bobber fits the species' foraging behaviour well, and no override is needed.
