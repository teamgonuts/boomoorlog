The Wood Leopard Moth (Zeuzera pyrina) is a large, distinctive moth whose caterpillars bore into the woody stems of many deciduous trees including Populus (poplar). The Figure of Eight moth (Diloba caeruleocephala) also uses Populus and related trees. Zeuzera caterpillars spend up to three years tunnelling through sapwood; adults are nocturnal fliers that rest on bark and trunks during the day. Tree-bark is dominant given the deep wood-boring larval phase; tree-canopy secondary for leaf-feeding by Diloba caterpillars. Idle-only overrides the flower-bobber default as neither species is a flower visitor.
