The caterpillars of both the eyed hawk-moth and the puss moth feed openly on willow and poplar foliage, clinging to leaves and shoots in the tree canopy throughout their larval phase. Adult eyed hawk-moths and puss moths are night-flying and rest motionless on bark or foliage during the day, neither visiting flowers nor moving actively in daylight. Salix trees along Amsterdam's canal banks provide abundant larval habitat. Tree-canopy is correct because the larvae spend their entire feeding period in the foliage; idle-only reflects the cryptic resting posture of both adults and older larvae.
