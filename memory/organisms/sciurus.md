This pairing groups grey squirrels with bank voles, both recorded in the same diverse tree stands (Castanea, Cedrus, Nyssa, Taxus, and others) in Amsterdam parks and wooded areas. Squirrels are the dominant visual element, spending much of their time in the canopy leaping between branches, with frequent descents to the ground to cache food — hence tree-canopy dominant and ground-park secondary. Bank voles are ground-dwelling and nocturnal, better captured by the park-roamer secondary. Tree-flitter is the dominant animation for the squirrel aerial phase, and park-roamer covers both vole ground movement and squirrel foraging on the grass. The mammal default of ground-park / park-roamer alone misses the arboreal component, requiring an override.
