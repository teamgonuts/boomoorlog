Meconema bush-crickets (most likely Meconema thalassinum, the Oak Bush-cricket, in Amsterdam) are slender, pale-green katydids that live almost exclusively in the canopy of broadleaved trees — oak, lime, ash, and hazel — where they hunt other invertebrates at night. By day they rest motionless among leaves, making tree-canopy the obvious dominant habitat and idle-only the correct movement class. The flower-visitor / flower-bobber insect default does not apply; this is a predatory tree-canopy insect rather than a pollinator.
