Both the Eurasian nuthatch and the great spotted woodpecker forage almost exclusively on bark and woody stems, probing crevices and excavating for invertebrates rather than flitting between canopy foliage like a typical songbird. They are recorded on the Corylus and other mature deciduous trees in Amsterdam's parks such as Vondelpark and the Amsterdamse Bos. The bird default of tree-canopy / tree-flitter is wrong for these bark-specialists; tree-bark places them on the trunk and larger branches where they actually operate. Idle-only captures their characteristic freeze-in-place posture on vertical bark rather than any continuous locomotion.
