This catch-all grouping covers woodpeckers, nuthatches, and other cavity-excavating birds alongside small bats that roost in the same hollows in Amsterdam's urban trees. Woodpeckers are active on bark surfaces and in the canopy of large trees in parks like Vondelpark and the Amsterdamse Bos, while bats emerge at dusk to use hollows for daytime roost. The dominant tag is tree-canopy for animated placement, with tree-bark as secondary because woodpeckers cling vertically on trunks. Movement is primarily tree-flitter for the avian component, with idle-only capturing roosting bats that sit entirely still during daylight.
