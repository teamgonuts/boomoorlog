This invasive bruchid seed weevil develops entirely inside the pods and seeds of Gleditsia triacanthos, completing its larval stage concealed within the bean-like fruits hanging in the tree canopy. Adults emerge in summer, mate on the pods, and lay eggs — all without leaving the tree crown. Tree-canopy is the sole relevant habitat since the insect's visible stages (adults on pods) occur there. Idle-only fits because adults are slow-moving and spend most time resting on pods rather than flying. The species is now widespread in Dutch cities wherever honey-locust is planted as a street tree.
