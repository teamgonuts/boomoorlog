Ancylis achatana is a small tortricid leaf-roller moth whose larvae feed on hawthorn, blackthorn, and other rosaceous trees. Adults rest cryptically on leaves and bark with wings folded flat, and are rarely seen in flight except at dusk. In Amsterdam parks and green corridors, it is most likely encountered resting on shrub or tree foliage.
