The Common Toad is the most widespread amphibian in Amsterdam and breeds in ponds and canals, but spends most of the year on dry land in parks, gardens, and woodland edges — a genuine dual-habitat species. Water-edge is dominant because spawning aggregations and toadlet emergence are the most spectacular and recordable events, but ground-park is a real secondary since adults are terrestrial for roughly nine months of the year. Movement alternates between slow nocturnal walks through park vegetation (park-roamer) and the slow stalking of invertebrate prey near water edges (water-edge-stalker). The Common Toad is one of the commonest amphibians observed in Amsterdam's Vondelpark ponds.
