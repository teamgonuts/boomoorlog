The Dark Arches is one of the most common noctuid moths in Amsterdam, with a variable dark-brown and pale pattern that blends well against bark and wooden surfaces when resting during the day. Its larvae feed on the roots and stems of grasses, particularly coarser species, so it is closely associated with rough grassland, park margins, and untended lawn areas — placing ground-park as the sole appropriate habitat. Adults are nocturnal and rest motionless during the day (idle-only); they visit light traps in large numbers in mid-summer.
