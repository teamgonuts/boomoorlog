The 14-spot Ladybird is one of the more common small ladybirds in Amsterdam, recognisable by its rectangular black-and-yellow chequered pattern rather than the classic round spots. Unlike many ladybirds it is strongly associated with low shrubs, tall herbs, and tree canopy rather than purely with flowers, hunting aphid colonies along stems and leaves. It overwinters in bark crevices or leaf litter and becomes active in spring when aphid populations explode. Habitat is overridden to tree-canopy dominant (where aphid colonies concentrate) with flower-visitor secondary for warm-season foraging on open flower umbels.
