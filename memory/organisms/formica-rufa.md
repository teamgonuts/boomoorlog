Red wood ants build large mound nests at the base of Pinus trees and forage extensively over the ground in parks and woodland patches in and around Amsterdam. While they do ascend pine trunks and canopy to tend aphids and harvest resin, their dominant placement is the ground-park mound and foraging trails, making ground-park the primary habitat. The park-roamer animation captures their constant ground movement, with idle-only as secondary for workers motionless on the mound surface. The override reflects their ground-dominant ecology, distinct from canopy-tending ants.
