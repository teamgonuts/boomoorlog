Verticillium species are soil-borne pathogenic fungi that infect plant roots and vascular tissue, causing Verticillium wilt in a wide range of hosts including Catalpa (Indian bean tree). In Amsterdam they persist in park and garden soils, entering through roots. They are invisible in the soil and produce no above-ground fruiting bodies, making ground-park the appropriate placement tag. The override from tree-bark to ground-park reflects their soil-dwelling, root-infecting lifestyle rather than any bark-surface presence.
