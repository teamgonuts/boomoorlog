The sycamore moth's striking yellow-and-black hairy caterpillar feeds on Acer leaves in mid to late summer and is occasionally found on horse chestnut and oak. In Amsterdam it turns up on park and street maples once larvae are large enough to spot. The adult is a night-flying moth not typically seen, so the grouping is dominated by the visible larval stage on foliage: tree-canopy + idle-only rather than the flower-visitor insect default.
