This composite entry groups cavity-nesting and bark-associated birds that use Fraxinus and other mature trees with natural hollows or decayed sections in Amsterdam parks. The tawny owl roosts and nests in tree cavities, largely motionless by day; the common redstart perches in canopy foliage and sallies for insects; the nuthatch and woodpeckers work the bark surface for invertebrates. Tree-canopy is dominant because the canopy-perching and cavity-entrance activity is most visible; tree-bark is the secondary because nuthatch and woodpecker spend real time on trunk surfaces. Idle-only is dominant reflecting the owl's daytime roost; tree-flitter captures the redstart's active flitting between foliage perches.
