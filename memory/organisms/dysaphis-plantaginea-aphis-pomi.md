Dysaphis plantaginea (rosy apple aphid) and Aphis pomi (green apple aphid) are two common aphid species that colonise apple (Malus) shoots and leaves in Amsterdam's parks and urban gardens. Rosy apple aphid causes characteristic leaf curling and distortion while green apple aphid forms dense colonies on young shoots; both species are sedentary feeders that remain stationary on the plant tissue. Tree-canopy captures their presence in the leafy zone of the host apple tree, and idle-only replaces the default flower-bobber since neither species visits flowers or moves meaningfully at animation scale.
