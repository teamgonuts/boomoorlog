Thelephoraceae are ectomycorrhizal fungi that form obligate symbiotic associations with the roots of Cedrus and other conifers, with their mycelium extending through the soil beneath the tree rather than on the bark surface. Small leathery or disc-shaped fruitbodies appear at ground level near the base of host trees, not on the trunk. In Amsterdam Cedrus is planted as an ornamental in parks and the ectomycorrhizal community beneath them can include Thelephora and related genera. Ground-park overrides the fungus default of tree-bark because the fruitbodies emerge from the soil rather than on the trunk surface; none is retained as the correct movement class for all fungi.
