This pairing covers two bat species recorded roosting in the same Fagus tree in Amsterdam: the Common Pipistrelle and the larger Noctule, both of which use hollow branches and bark crevices as daytime roosts. The Noctule is one of Europe's highest-flying bats, often hunting above tree-canopy level well into open sky, while the Pipistrelle takes lower circuits; both share the sky-looper animation at dusk. Placement snaps to the tree bark during idle hours and transitions to sky-only during active flight. The mammal default does not fit either species, requiring a full override.
