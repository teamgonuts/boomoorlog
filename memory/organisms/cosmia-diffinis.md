The white-spotted pinion is a noctuid moth whose caterpillar feeds inside the developing buds and leaves of elm (Ulmus), an increasingly rare host in Amsterdam following Dutch elm disease but still present in some parks and canal streets. The larva mines buds in spring and the adult rests cryptically on bark or among leaves during the day, making idle-only the correct animation for any daylight sprite. The insect category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is overridden to tree-canopy / idle-only because the species is wholly dependent on elm foliage and has no flower-visiting stage relevant to map animation.
