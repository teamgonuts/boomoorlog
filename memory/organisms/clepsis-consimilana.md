Clepsis consimilana is a small tortricid leaf-roller moth whose larvae feed inside rolled leaves of a wide range of deciduous trees and shrubs common in Amsterdam, including linden, hawthorn, and rose — all abundant in the city's parks and canal-side plantings. Adults rest by day on foliage and bark in the canopy layer, relying on their mottled tan-and-cream pattern for camouflage; like most tortricids they fly only after dark and do not feed at flowers as adults. Tree-canopy is the correct habitat as the entire life cycle is tree-associated, and idle-only captures the motionless daytime resting posture.
