The nut-tree tussock moth (Colocasia coryli) is a noctuid whose caterpillars feed on Carpinus (hornbeam) foliage in Amsterdam parks and avenues. The larva is a foliage-resting caterpillar that moves only slowly between leaves; the adult moth is nocturnal and rests immobile on bark or leaves during the day. Neither the larval nor adult stage involves flower-visiting, so the insect category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is overridden in favour of tree-canopy / idle-only, which places the sprite in the foliage zone of the host hornbeam and renders it as a near-stationary caterpillar or resting moth.
