Scoparia ambigualis is a small grey crambid moth whose larvae feed in mosses and liverworts on tree trunks and damp walls. Adults rest cryptically pressed flat against bark or lichen-covered surfaces during the day, making them nearly invisible. In Amsterdam they are recorded from parks with old trees and moss-covered walls. The default flower-visitor/flower-bobber combination does not apply; this moth never visits flowers and spends its adult life motionless on bark surfaces.
