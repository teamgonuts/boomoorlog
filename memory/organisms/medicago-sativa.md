Alfalfa is a perennial legume naturalised on dry, nitrogen-poor soils along roadsides, railway banks, and open park margins in Amsterdam, where it persists as a relic of historical cultivation and spontaneous colonisation. Its purple-blue flowers are particularly attractive to long-tongued bumblebees that trigger the explosive pollen-release mechanism. The plant fixes atmospheric nitrogen through root nodules, enriching impoverished urban soils. The default ground-park / none assignment is correct for this rooted herb.
