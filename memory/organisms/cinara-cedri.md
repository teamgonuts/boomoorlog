Cinara cedri is a large, grey-brown conifer aphid that feeds on the shoots and young needles of Cedrus (cedar) trees, producing copious honeydew that coats the ground beneath and supports sooty mould. Cedar trees — Cedrus atlantica, C. libani, and cultivars — are planted in Amsterdam parks such as the Vondelpark and Sarphatipark. Colonies cluster on young shoots and remain on cedar without alternating to another host plant.
