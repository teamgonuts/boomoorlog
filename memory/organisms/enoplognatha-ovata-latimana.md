Enoplognatha ovata and the closely-related E. latimana are small comb-footed spiders (Theridiidae) that build small tangled webs on flower heads, bramble, and low shrubs — they are almost diagnostic of flower and seed heads in summer and are frequently photographed on umbellifers and thistles. Flower-visitor is the appropriate habitat override from the arachnid default of anywhere, since the spider genuinely lives on or near blooms to intercept visiting insects. It stays put in its web, so idle-only movement is correct and unchanged. In Amsterdam these spiders are common in park margins, rough grassland, and hedgerows throughout July and August.
