The Domestic Mallard is the semi-feral descendant of the wild mallard, found in Amsterdam's canals, park ponds, and any water body where people feed ducks. Like the wild mallard it spends much of its time drifting on water surfaces, but it is even more inclined to waddle onto pavements and tourist-heavy areas to beg for food, making ground-urban a strong secondary habitat. Movement is water-drifter dominant with urban-walker as a regular secondary. It is found throughout Amsterdam wherever there is open water and human foot traffic, including Vondelpark pond, the inner canals, and the Amstel waterfront.
