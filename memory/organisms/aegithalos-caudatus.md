The Long-tailed Tit is a sociable, acrobatic small bird that moves through Amsterdam's parks, canal-side trees, and scrubby hedges in roving family parties, constantly flitting from branch to branch in search of tiny invertebrates and spiders. Its extremely long tail, fluffy pink-and-black plumage, and high-pitched contact calls make it unmistakable. The default tree-canopy / tree-flitter combination fits perfectly: it almost never descends to ground level and epitomises the small canopy-flitting songbird. Vondelpark and Artis zoo gardens are reliable sites.
