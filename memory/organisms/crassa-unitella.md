Crassa unitella (formerly Borkhausenia unitella) is a small oecophorid moth strongly associated with dead wood, bark debris, and dry plant litter in sheltered spots — often in and around buildings, wood stores, and dead timber in parks. Like its family relative Hofmannophila pseudospretella it rests motionless against surfaces during the day. Its larvae feed on decaying vegetable matter and dead wood rather than live flowers, so the default flower-visitor / flower-bobber assignment is inappropriate. Ground-urban with idle-only covers its low-profile, resting-adult urban presence in Amsterdam.
