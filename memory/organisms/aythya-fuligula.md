The Tufted Duck is Amsterdam's most abundant diving duck, present year-round on virtually every canal, pond, and lake in the city. Unlike dabbling ducks it dives frequently and deeply to feed on molluscs and invertebrates on the water bottom, so water-body is an important secondary habitat. Water-surface is dominant because the bird spends most visible time floating; water-drifter covers the steady drift along canals with idle-only secondary for the long periods of preening and sleeping on the surface. The default bird tree-canopy / tree-flitter is completely wrong for this aquatic specialist.
