Chromaphis juglandicola is a specialist aphid that feeds on the undersides of Juglans (walnut) leaflets, forming pale-yellow colonies that excrete heavy honeydew and can cause early leaf-fall. Juglans regia is an occasional tree in Amsterdam parks and larger gardens. The aphid does not use an alternate host and remains on walnut from spring until autumn leaf-fall; colonies are effectively stationary on the leaf underside.
