Opium Poppy is a tall annual with distinctive glaucous (blue-grey) leaves and large flowers in shades of white, pink, purple, or red, followed by large ornamental seed capsules. It is widely grown as a garden plant in Amsterdam and frequently escapes to parks, waste ground, and roadsides, naturalising readily on disturbed soils. The flowers are rich in pollen and attract bumblebees and other bees in large numbers. It self-seeds prolifically and can persist in the same location for many years.
