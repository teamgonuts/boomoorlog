Hoary Alison is a grey-woolly annual or biennial crucifer of disturbed, dry, and sandy ground, most commonly encountered in Amsterdam on railway embankments, road margins, gravel areas, and wasteland — distinctly urban-disturbed habitats rather than managed park lawns. Its small white four-petalled flowers are modest but attract small bees and hoverflies during the long summer flowering period. As a pioneer of dry disturbed ground it fits ground-urban as the dominant habitat rather than the plant default of ground-park; ground-park is a secondary for dry park margins and gravel paths within parks. Movement is none.
