The Red-legged Shieldbug is a large, handsome shield bug with a dark bronze-green body and distinctively red-orange legs; it is strongly associated with broadleaved trees — oak, alder, hazel, and beech — making tree-canopy the dominant habitat in Amsterdam's parks and street trees. Adults and nymphs feed on developing seeds and buds in the canopy and descend to bark to overwinter under loose flakes (tree-bark secondary). They are almost entirely idle-only, sitting motionless on leaves or bark for extended periods. Overridden from the insect flower-visitor default: this bug feeds on tree material, not flower nectar.
