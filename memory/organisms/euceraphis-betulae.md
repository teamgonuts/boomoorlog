Euceraphis betulae is a large, elegant aphid found on silver birch (Betula) trees in Amsterdam's parks and along birch-lined paths. Unlike many aphids it tends to occur in scattered individuals rather than dense colonies, but it is still a sedentary canopy feeder that sits immobile on leaf undersides extracting sap. Adults are winged but flights are brief dispersal events rather than sustained visible movement. Tree-canopy captures the leaf-surface foraging zone correctly, and idle-only replaces the inappropriate insect defaults of flower-visitor and flower-bobber.
