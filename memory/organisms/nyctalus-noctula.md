The noctule is the largest common bat in the Netherlands and a classic tree-hole roosting species, using cavities in mature Betula and other deciduous trees in Amsterdam's parks and cemeteries. By day it roosts motionless in tree holes (idle-only, tree-canopy); at dusk it launches into high fast aerial foraging circuits over parks and open water (sky-looper, sky-only). Tree-canopy is dominant for placement because that is where the roosting colony is anchored; sky-only is secondary for the foraging flight. Noctules are often the first bats visible each evening in Amsterdam, flying high before dark.
