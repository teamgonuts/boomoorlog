Tenthredinid sawflies lay their eggs into the leaf tissue of Cornus and related trees, and the larvae feed gregariously on foliage in the tree canopy, often stripping individual branches. Adults are small wasp-like insects that rest on leaves or stems rather than visiting flowers. Cornus is a common ornamental shrub-tree in Amsterdam parks and garden plantings. Tree-canopy is correct because larval feeding is confined to the foliage layer; idle-only replaces flower-bobber because sawfly adults do not feed on flowers in any significant way and the larvae are slow leaf-feeders.
