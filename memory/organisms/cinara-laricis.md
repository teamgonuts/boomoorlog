The speckled larch aphid is a large lachnid that feeds in dense colonies on the bark of young larch shoots and the main stem of Larix trees. In Amsterdam it is recorded from the single Larix specimen in the dataset and appears in spring and early summer. Colonies are sedentary, covered in a fine wax bloom, and show no foraging movement, making idle-only the correct animation tag. The insect category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is overridden because this species is strictly bark-feeding and never associated with flowers.
