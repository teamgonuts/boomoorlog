The Small Dusty Wave is a small geometrid moth whose larvae feed on dead and decaying plant matter, algae, and lichens on walls, fences, and bark. Adults rest by day on lichen-covered walls, wooden fences, and the bark of trees where their pale dusty pattern provides camouflage. The default flower-visitor / flower-bobber profile is completely wrong for a resting moth; ground-park is retained as a secondary for larvae in leaf-litter, while wall-and-roof or tree-bark would be equally valid for adults. Idle-only is the correct movement class.
