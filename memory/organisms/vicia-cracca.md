Tufted Vetch is a climbing perennial legume common in Amsterdam's grassy park borders, roadsides, and scrubby canal banks, clambering over neighbouring vegetation with tendrils to reach the light. It produces dense racemes of violet-blue flowers from June to August that are visited by bumblebees, particularly long-tongued species that can reach the nectar. As a nitrogen-fixing legume it enriches the soil in parks and meadow patches where it establishes. The plant default of ground-park / none applies without modification: it roots in vegetated ground and does not move.
