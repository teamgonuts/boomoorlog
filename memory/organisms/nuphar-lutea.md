Yellow Water-lily is a large aquatic perennial rooted in the mud of slow-moving and still water, with floating leaves and distinctive yellow, globe-shaped flowers that float on or just above the surface. It is native to Dutch waterways and is found in Amsterdam's canals, ditches, and park ponds wherever the water is sufficiently clear and shallow.
