The Ferruginous Bee-grabber is a conopid fly whose adults are faithful flower visitors, feeding on nectar from umbellifers and composites while ambushing bumblebees in which they lay their internal parasitoid eggs. In Amsterdam it is observed on patches of wildflowers in parks and rough urban ground, where it perches on flower heads or nearby vegetation waiting for bee hosts. Its movement on the map is appropriately flower-bobber as it moves between nearby blooms, with idle-only resting bouts between forays. The default flower-visitor/flower-bobber fits well and no override is needed.
