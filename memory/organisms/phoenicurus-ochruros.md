The Black Redstart is a striking urban specialist that nests in gaps and crevices of buildings, industrial rooftops, and along railway embankments in Amsterdam. Unlike the default small-songbird profile, it rarely forages in tree canopies; instead it drops from walls or low perches onto paved ground to snatch invertebrates, then returns to a rooftop vantage point. Its characteristic tail-quivering habit makes it visually distinctive even when perched still. The wall-and-roof habitat and urban-walker movement fit this species far better than the tree-canopy default.
