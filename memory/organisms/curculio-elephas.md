The chestnut weevil (Curculio elephas) is a long-snouted curculionid whose larvae develop inside the nuts of Castanea (sweet chestnut). The female drills into developing chestnuts using her elongated rostrum and lays eggs inside; the larvae feed within the nut and emerge in autumn to pupate in soil. Adult weevils cling to foliage and nuts in the canopy and move very slowly. The insect category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is overridden to tree-canopy / idle-only because all activity is centred on the nut-bearing canopy, not on flowers, and the adults' positional movement is negligible at sprite scale.
