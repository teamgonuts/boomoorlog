Winter moth (Operophtera brumata) and tortrix moths (Tortricidae) are generalist leaf-feeders whose caterpillars attack the buds and young leaves of Pterocarya (Caucasian wingnut) and many other deciduous trees in Amsterdam. Caterpillars spin silken shelters on shoots and hang on silk threads. Adult winter moths are flightless females that crawl up trunks in winter. Tree-canopy is dominant for caterpillar feeding on buds and leaves; tree-bark secondary for the trunk-crawling adult females and pupation. Idle-only overrides flower-bobber — neither caterpillars nor resting moths visit flowers.
