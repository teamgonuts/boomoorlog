Common Bird's-foot-trefoil is a low-growing perennial clover-relative with bright yellow-and-orange flowers that is widespread on dry, short-grass areas in Amsterdam's parks, dune edges, and unmown road verges. Its common name refers to the claw-shaped seed pods that resemble a bird's foot. The default ground-park placement fits well: it grows in open grassy sward on well-drained soils and is an important nectar plant for bumblebees and the foodplant of Common Blue butterfly larvae.
