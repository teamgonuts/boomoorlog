Anthomyia procellaris is a small anthomyiid fly whose larvae are associated with decaying organic matter and dung in vegetated habitats. Adults are often seen basking on foliage or resting on low vegetation in parks and green spaces. This is not a flower-visitor in any meaningful sense; adults rarely visit flowers and are more characteristic of shaded ground-level habitats. ground-park placement reflects where this species is realistically observed. The idle-only movement fits its typical resting, basking behaviour as an adult.
