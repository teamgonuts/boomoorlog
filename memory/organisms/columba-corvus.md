This combined entry records wood pigeons and corvids (jackdaws, crows, or magpies) together on Metasequoia and Nyssa in Amsterdam. Wood pigeons roost and forage in the canopy while corvids — especially hooded crows and jackdaws — regularly descend to paved areas to forage. The dual habitat of tree-canopy and anywhere reflects this genuine split, and the dual movement of tree-flitter and urban-walker captures the between-tree flight of pigeons alongside the pavement-walking of corvids. The bird default of tree-canopy / tree-flitter is extended rather than replaced to account for the corvid ground-foraging component of this mixed observation.
