Marsh-marigold is a robust perennial of wet ground, growing at the margins of ditches, ponds, and canals — exactly the water-edge habitat that lines Amsterdam's grachten. Its brilliant yellow flowers appear in March–April, making it one of the first splashes of colour at the canal edge. It grows in standing water or saturated soil and cannot tolerate dry ground-park conditions. The water-edge override is biologically essential; placement along OSM water polygon boundaries will look natural. Movement is none — it is a static plant.
