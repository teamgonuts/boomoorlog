The Holly Blue is a dainty metalite-blue butterfly that uses Ilex aquifolium (holly) as its primary spring larval foodplant and ivy flowers as a secondary host; both plants occur widely in Amsterdam parks, gardens, and churchyards. Adults are active flower-visitors that flit between holly and ivy blooms, nectar sources, and damp patches in a characteristic erratic flight close to shrub and tree height. The insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is a natural fit for this species without any override needed.
