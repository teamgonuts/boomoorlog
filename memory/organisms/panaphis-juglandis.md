The large walnut aphid is a conspicuous, bright-yellow aphid that colonises the upper sides of walnut (Juglans) leaves in Amsterdam from spring to autumn. Colonies cluster on leaf veins and midribs in the canopy, feeding on phloem sap without ever descending to flowers or ground. The default insect tags (flower-visitor, flower-bobber) are completely inapplicable to this obligate tree-canopy phloem-feeder. Individuals within a colony barely move, making idle-only the correct animation class; winged dispersal forms are brief and infrequent.
