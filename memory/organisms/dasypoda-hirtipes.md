The Pantaloon Bee is a striking solitary mining bee, named for the enormous pollen-collecting hairs on the females' hind legs. It nests in extensive burrows in sandy, sparsely vegetated ground — dunes, sandy path edges, and open park soils — and is among the most dramatic excavators in the bee world, kicking out visible fans of sand. Adults visit flowers for pollen and nectar but spend most of their observed time at or near their nest entrances in open ground. ground-park placement reflects nesting habitat; idle-only captures nest-entrance hovering and resting. The default flower-visitor would miss the critical ground-nesting behaviour.
