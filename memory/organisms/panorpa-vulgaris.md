The Common Scorpionfly is a distinctive insect with the male's scorpion-like upturned abdomen tip and long "beak." It inhabits shaded, humid, and vegetated areas — woodland edges, dense hedgerows, and shrubbery in parks. Adults are scavengers and kleptoparasites rather than flower visitors, feeding on dead insects and fruit. In Amsterdam they are found in the more sheltered, shrubby parts of larger parks. The default flower-visitor tag does not apply to this scavenging species. ground-park and idle-only reflect its shrubby low-vegetation resting habitat and sedentary adult behaviour.
