Chironomid midges are the dominant aquatic macroinvertebrates of Amsterdam's canals and ponds, with larvae living in bottom sediments where they process organic matter and form a critical food base for fish, waterfowl, and bats. Adults emerge in vast numbers on warm evenings, forming the dancing mating swarms visible over canal surfaces and park ponds throughout spring and summer. As aquatic insects, water-edge and water-body correctly replace the flower-visitor default: larvae are water-body, emergence and adult swarming are water-edge. The sky-looper secondary captures the aerial mating swarm behaviour; idle-only covers adults resting on vegetation near water.
