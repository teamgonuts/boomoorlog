Celypha lacunana is a small tortrix moth whose larvae feed on low herbaceous plants — docks, dandelions, plantain — in parks, verges, and disturbed ground. The adult rests during the day cryptically on leaf litter or low vegetation with wings pressed flat, and only flies at dusk or night. It has no particular association with flowers as a visitor, so the default flower-visitor / flower-bobber pairing does not apply. Ground-park with idle-only best captures the adult's motionless daytime resting posture on park-edge vegetation; it is the commonest tortrix moth in Amsterdam green spaces.
