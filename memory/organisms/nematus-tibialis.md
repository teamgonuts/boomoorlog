Nematus tibialis is a tenthredinid sawfly whose larvae feed gregariously on Robinia pseudoacacia (black locust) foliage, a tree widely planted on Amsterdam's sandy soils and disturbed sites. The larvae cluster on leaflets, consuming them from the margins inward, and barely move between feeding bouts — idle-only is the right animation. Tree-canopy is the dominant and sufficient habitat; no bark or ground stage is visible or relevant at this scale. Outbreaks can cause dramatic defoliation on Amsterdam's Robinia rows, making the species conspicuous to city residents in summer.
