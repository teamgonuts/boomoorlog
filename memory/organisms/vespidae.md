Vespidae (social and solitary wasps) and Syrphidae (hoverflies) are both common in Amsterdam and are drawn to Liriodendron (tulip tree) and Morus (mulberry) for nectar and as hunting/foraging grounds. Hoverflies are classic flower visitors, hovering between blooms; wasps are more varied but visit flowers for nectar and hunt prey in tree canopy. Flower-visitor leads as dominant habitat since both groups are observed primarily in the flowering context here. Sky-looper secondary captures the strong sustained flight both groups use in transit between flower patches.
