The Riband Wave is a small geometrid moth common in Amsterdam's parks, hedgerows, and tree-lined streets, where adults rest by day on bark, wooden fences, and shaded leaf litter blending in with their subtly patterned pale wings. Its larvae feed on low herbs including dock, dandelion, and similar plants found abundantly in Amsterdam's verges and park understorey. The moth does not feed at flowers — it takes no adult nectar and its flight activity is entirely nocturnal and triggered by darkness rather than bloom. The insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber does not fit; tree-bark (dominant daytime resting surface in urban Amsterdam) and idle-only reflect its real ecology.
