Buprestid leaf-miners of the Trachys type lay eggs on the surface of Zelkova leaves and the larvae mine the leaf tissue, creating characteristic blotch mines visible as discoloured patches on the upper leaf surface. Adults are small, jewel-like beetles that bask on foliage in sunshine but do not visit flowers. Zelkova serrata is increasingly planted as a street and park tree in Amsterdam as an elm replacement. Tree-canopy is the correct habitat because both larval mining and adult basking are on the leaf surface in the canopy; idle-only replaces flower-bobber because neither adult nor larva has any flower-visiting behavior. A photo suggestion for this species without a photo: close-up of a Zelkova leaf showing blotch mines, or an adult beetle on a leaf in sunlight.
