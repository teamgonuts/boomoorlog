Chrysomela populi and C. tremula are chunky red-and-black leaf beetles whose adults and larvae feed openly on Populus (poplar) leaves; poplar is common along Amsterdam's canals, in the Amsterdamse Bos, and as street plantings. Larvae congregate on leaves and can skeletonise entire branches; adults overwinter in bark but are prominently visible in the canopy during summer. Despite adults being mobile on the leaf surface, their movement at pixel-art scale is imperceptible.
