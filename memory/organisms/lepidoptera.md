This entry covers four caterpillar species observed on Corylus, Fagus, Fraxinus and Nyssa in Amsterdam parks: Colocasia coryli (Nut-tree Tussock), Geometra papilionaria (Large Emerald), Plagodis pulveraria (Barred Umber) and Asthena albulata (Small White Wave). All four are foliage-feeding larvae that cling to leaves or rest motionlessly on branches during the day, perfectly matching tree-canopy and idle-only. Their cryptic resting behaviour means they are essentially static sprites in the canopy. The override from the insect default is required because these are caterpillar-stage defoliators, not flower-visitors.
