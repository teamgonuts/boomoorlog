Argyresthia conjugella, the apple fruit moth, is a small moth whose larvae tunnel into the fruits and seeds of Sorbus (rowan) and Malus (apple) in Amsterdam. The larvae develop entirely inside the fruit, feeding on the flesh and seeds, then drop to the soil to pupate. Adults are tiny (wingspan ~10mm), silvery-white moths that rest on foliage by day. The primary ecological interaction — the larval mining stage — occurs within the tree canopy on the fruit, making tree-canopy the appropriate habitat override. Idle-only reflects the larval immobility within the fruit mine; this is a tree-feeding microorganism on Sorbus.
