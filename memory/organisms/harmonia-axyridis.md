The Harlequin Ladybird is the dominant ladybird species in Amsterdam, introduced from Asia and now found abundantly on linden, lime, and other deciduous street trees where aphid colonies provide its primary food source. Adults spend most of their active season clambering slowly through foliage and along twigs in tree canopies, pausing frequently — movement is too slow and non-directional to qualify as tree-flitter, so idle-only with positional drifting better represents the sprite animation. In autumn large aggregations gather on walls, window frames, and tree trunks to overwinter. The insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is wrong; this is an aphid predator of tree canopy, not a flower visitor.
