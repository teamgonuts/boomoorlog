The Bird-cherry Ermine Moth (Yponomeuta evonymella) and its close relative Y. padella are small moths notorious for completely encasing Prunus (cherry, bird cherry) trees in dense communal silk webs spun by the caterpillars. In Amsterdam, silked-up Prunus trees are a common summer sight, the web shrouds covering entire crowns. Caterpillars feed on leaves within the silk tent in the tree canopy; adults rest on bark and foliage. Tree-canopy dominates for the conspicuous canopy webbing; tree-bark secondary for adult moths at rest. Idle-only overrides flower-bobber — neither the webbing caterpillars nor resting adults visit flowers.
