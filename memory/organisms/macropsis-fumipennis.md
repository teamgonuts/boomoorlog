The honey locust leafhopper is a specialist sap-sucker on Gleditsia triacanthos foliage, a tree increasingly planted as a street tree in Amsterdam's newer districts. Adults and nymphs sit on the undersides of leaflets, piercing phloem and barely moving during long feeding bouts. Tree-canopy is the sole relevant habitat since the entire life cycle plays out in the leaf layer. Idle-only fits because leafhoppers are virtually motionless while feeding, only making small sideways jumps when disturbed — below the threshold for a distinct movement animation.
