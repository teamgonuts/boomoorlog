The European fruit lecanium scale is a soft scale insect (family Coccidae) that encrusts the twigs and small branches of Taxodium and many other trees in Amsterdam. Adult females are permanently fixed to the bark surface under their waxy scale covering, making tree-bark the precise habitat class rather than tree-canopy. There is no flower-visiting, no ground presence, and no meaningful movement at any life stage visible in the tree dataset. Idle-only is the only appropriate movement class for this bark-anchored, entirely sedentary scale insect.
