Lobarion and Graphidion are epiphytic lichen communities that colonise the smooth to furrowed bark of mature Fraxinus (ash) and other broadleaved trees in damp, well-lit urban parks. In Amsterdam these crusts and foliose thalli are found on older park trees with high air-quality scores, particularly in quieter canal-side and cemetery settings. They are entirely static — no movement whatsoever — and fully bound to the bark surface where they grow. The lichen category default of tree-bark + none fits perfectly and no override is needed.
