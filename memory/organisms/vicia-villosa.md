Fodder Vetch is a climbing or scrambling annual legume with dense racemes of purple and white flowers, introduced across Europe as a fodder crop and now naturalised on roadsides, railway verges, and rough grassland. In Amsterdam it appears in parks, along cycle paths, and on sandy or disturbed soils. Its tendrils allow it to climb through neighbouring vegetation but it remains ground-rooted.
