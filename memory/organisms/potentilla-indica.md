The Yellow-flowered Strawberry (Mock Strawberry, formerly Duchesnea indica) is a low mat-forming perennial originally from Asia that has naturalised widely in Amsterdam's park lawns, cemetery margins, and grassy verges. Its creeping runners, trifoliate leaves, and small yellow flowers closely resemble a wild strawberry. The ground-park default fits well: it grows in established grassy or lightly shaded sward and does not favour paved surfaces. It is increasingly common as a lawn weed in Vondelpark and community gardens.
