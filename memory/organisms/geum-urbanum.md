Wood Avens is a common perennial of shaded and semi-shaded park margins, woodland edges, canal-side hedgerows, and damp spots throughout Amsterdam, named after its urban tolerance (the species epithet urbanum). Its small yellow flowers appear from late spring and are followed by hooked burr-like fruits that attach to animal fur and human clothing for dispersal. The plant grows readily in the shadow of park shrub layers and under canal-side trees, making it one of the most reliably-observed ground herbs in Amsterdam.
