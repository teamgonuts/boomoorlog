Creeping Thistle is one of Amsterdam's most widespread and persistent wild plants, occurring in parks, allotments, canal verges, and any disturbed or neglected green space. It spreads aggressively via horizontal roots and wind-dispersed plumed seeds, rapidly colonising open ground. Its small, pale lilac flower heads are intensely attractive to bumblebees, honey bees, butterflies, and many beetle species from July through September. Despite being considered a weed in managed settings, it is ecologically valuable as one of the highest-yield nectar plants in the urban flora. The default ground-park placement is appropriate for Amsterdam observations.
