The Eurasian blackcap is a common and conspicuous warbler in Amsterdam parks and gardens, singing loudly from shrubby canopy edges and feeding on insects in foliage as well as berries from Amelanchier and other fruiting shrubs in autumn. It is an active forager that moves continuously between perches in the canopy layer, fitting the tree-flitter movement perfectly. Both migratory birds passing through and an increasing number of overwintering birds are recorded in Amsterdam. The bird default of tree-canopy / tree-flitter fits without any override.
