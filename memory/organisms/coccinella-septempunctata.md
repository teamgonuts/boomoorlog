The seven-spot ladybird is Amsterdam's most familiar aphid predator, observed on Tilia where it hunts lime aphid colonies in the canopy. Adults and larvae cling to leaves and stems, pausing frequently between aphid catches, so their net positional change is negligible and idle-only captures the on-screen animation well. The insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is overridden because the primary habitat is leafy canopy rather than flowers, and the movement is predatory stalking-in-place rather than flower-bobbing. Tree-canopy placement snaps the sprite to the leaf zone of the nearest Tilia, which matches the field observation context.
