Phloeosinus aubei is a small scolytine bark beetle whose adults and larvae bore beneath the bark of Metasequoia (dawn redwood) and cypress-family trees in Amsterdam. Both the egg galleries and larval tunnels are entirely within the cambium and inner bark, making tree-bark the correct placement habitat. The beetle's presence is detected by its exit holes and frass rather than by visible surface activity, confirming idle-only as the movement class. The default insect tags (flower-visitor, flower-bobber) have no relevance to this bark-boring specialist.
