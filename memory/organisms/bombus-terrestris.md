Bombus terrestris (buff-tailed bumblebee) and B. hortorum (garden bumblebee) are two of Amsterdam's most common and conspicuous bumblebee species, observed here on Aesculus, Amelanchier, Koelreuteria, Laburnum, Paulownia, and Styphnolobium — a rich set of flowering street and park trees. B. terrestris is a short-tongued generalist that can robber-nectar on tubular flowers; B. hortorum is long-tongued and specializes on deeper blooms. Both nest underground in parks and gardens. Flower-visitor and flower-bobber are the dominant tags per the taxonomy example for bumblebees; the sky-looper secondary movement is retained from the Bombus default to capture inter-patch transit flights.
