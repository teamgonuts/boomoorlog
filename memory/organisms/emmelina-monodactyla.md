The Common Plume is a plume moth (Pterophoridae) whose larvae feed on Field Bindweed and related Convolvulaceae. Adults rest with wings rolled into a characteristic T-shape on stems of their host plant or nearby fencing, and are common wherever bindweed grows in Amsterdam — roadsides, garden fences, rough ground, and park margins. It can be found year-round as adults overwinter. The flower-visitor default does not apply; habitat overridden to ground-park with ground-urban secondary to reflect its close association with urban weed habitats; movement overridden to idle-only.
