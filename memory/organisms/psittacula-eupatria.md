The Alexandrine Parakeet is the largest naturalised parakeet in Amsterdam, closely related to the more abundant Ring-necked Parakeet. Like its congener it roosts and nests in tree cavities and dense canopy, flying in noisy flocks between park trees. Tree-canopy is the clear dominant habitat; wall-and-roof secondary covers the cavity-nesting behaviour in building facades and nest boxes. Movement is primarily tree-flitter between canopy perches with idle-only during the prolonged vocal resting bouts that make the species so audible in Vondelpark and Beatrixpark.
