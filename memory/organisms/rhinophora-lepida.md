Rhinophora lepida is a small tachinid-family blowfly (Calliphoridae) whose adults are flower visitors, frequently found on umbellifers and composite flowers in parks and meadow margins. Its larvae are parasitoids of woodlice, which are abundant in damp ground-park habitats, so the adult is closely tied to vegetated open ground where both host and flower are available. The dominant habitat is ground-park rather than flower-visitor because adult sightings are often on or near ground-level vegetation rather than in the canopy. Movement is flower-bobber since adults make short flights between flower heads when foraging for nectar.
