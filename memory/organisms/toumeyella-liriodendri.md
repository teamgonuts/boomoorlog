The tulip-tree scale is a soft scale insect in the family Coccidae that anchors itself to the bark and young stems of Liriodendron tulipifera, feeding by tapping phloem sap through its stylet. Mature females are large, hemispherical, and orange-brown, forming prominent colonies on the bark of ornamental tulip trees. Liriodendron is planted as a specimen tree in Amsterdam parks and avenues. Tree-bark is the correct habitat because scale insects are entirely bark-surface organisms that never move appreciably once settled; idle-only reflects their completely sessile adult life, overriding the insect flower-visitor default.
