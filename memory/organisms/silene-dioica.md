Red Campion is a familiar, cheerful woodland-edge and parkland plant producing vivid deep-pink flowers from April through October in Amsterdam's parks, cemetery margins, and shaded verges. It is dioecious, with separate male and female plants, and is primarily pollinated by long-tongued bumblebees and butterflies. It thrives in the partial shade and moist, fertile soils typical of Amsterdam's older park plantings and tree-lined canal sides. Hybridisation with the rarer White Campion can produce pale pink intermediate forms. The default ground-park placement is accurate for virtually all Amsterdam observations.
