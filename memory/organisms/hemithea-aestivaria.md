The Common Emerald is a delicate pale-green geometrid moth that rests with wings fully spread flat against leaves, making it almost invisible against summer foliage in Amsterdam parks and wooded canal margins. Larvae feed on a wide variety of broadleaf trees and shrubs — oak, hawthorn, bramble, and others — so adults are most likely found near mature canopy trees. It flies at night and comes readily to light traps; during the day it is completely still on a leaf surface. The default flower-visitor / flower-bobber is wrong for a resting foliage moth: tree-canopy / idle-only fits the adult resting behaviour, with ground-park as secondary for individuals found resting on low shrub vegetation.
