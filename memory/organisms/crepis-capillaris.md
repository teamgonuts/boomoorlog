Smooth Hawk's-beard is a common annual or biennial of short, grazed or mown grassland, roadside verges, and park lawns — one of the characteristic small yellow composites of Amsterdam's open green spaces. It tolerates heavy mowing and regular disturbance, persisting in the gaps between pavement slabs and along park path edges. The yellow flower heads attract small bees and hoverflies.
