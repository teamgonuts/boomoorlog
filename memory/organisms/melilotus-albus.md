White Melilot is a tall biennial legume of disturbed ground, waste lots, railway embankments, and open park margins throughout Amsterdam. Its strongly honey-scented white flowers are an important late-summer nectar source for bees, and the plant fixes atmospheric nitrogen through its root nodules. Like its yellow-flowered relative Melilotus officinalis it colonises open, nutrient-poor soils quickly after disturbance. The default ground-park / none pairing is accurate for this rooted herb.
