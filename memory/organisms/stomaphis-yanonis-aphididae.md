Stomaphis yanonis is an unusually large bark-feeding aphid that inserts its long stylet into the phloem of Zelkova bark rather than feeding on leaf tissue; colonies aggregate on the trunk and major branches beneath ant tents. Zelkova is a planted street and park tree in Amsterdam, and this aphid is an uncommon but notable associate. Tree-bark is correct because the colonies are anchored to the trunk surface rather than the canopy foliage; idle-only reflects the completely sessile colony life of phloem-feeding Stomaphis.
