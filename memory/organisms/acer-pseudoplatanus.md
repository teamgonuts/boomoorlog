The Sycamore is a large deciduous tree widespread in Amsterdam as a street and park tree, self-seeding readily in disturbed ground and canal margins. Although the GBIF record carries category "plant" (taxon_group 10), biologically this is unambiguously a tree; the category is overridden to "tree" with tree-rooted placement. It produces distinctive winged samara fruits in paired keys and is important for aphid communities, supporting species such as Drepanosiphum platanoidis which in turn attract tits and hoverflies. Its large palmate leaves create deep shade beneath the canopy in summer.
