This unusual entry describes the community of overwintering invertebrates sheltering in the dead but persistent (marcescent) leaves of Carpinus (hornbeam), Cercidiphyllum, and Chamaecyparis in Amsterdam. Marcescent leaves on these trees retain their foliage through winter, providing insulation for dormant spiders, true bugs (Hemiptera), and beetles (Coleoptera). The dominant taxon listed is Araneae so the arachnid category is most appropriate, though this is a mixed community. Tree-canopy placement reflects the fact that the leaves hang on the tree rather than on the ground; idle-only movement is correct for dormant, overwintering animals making no voluntary movements until spring warmth returns.
