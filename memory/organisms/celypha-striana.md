Celypha striana is a tortrix moth whose larvae feed on the roots of various herbaceous plants including dandelions, plantains, and docks in open, grassy areas. The adult rests cryptically during the day on grass stems or dead vegetation near ground level, flying briefly at night. Like other tortrix moths in this batch it has no meaningful flower-visiting behaviour and the default flower-visitor / flower-bobber pairing is inappropriate. Ground-park with idle-only correctly places the adult's resting posture on park grassland and path edges in Amsterdam.
