The Hornet Plumehorn is the largest hoverfly in northwest Europe and a spectacular hornet mimic with amber and black banding. It is a recent range expander in the Netherlands, now well established in Amsterdam's parks and gardens. Adults are avid flower visitors, favouring large umbellifers such as hogweed, and garden flowers including Buddleia and Angelica, making flower-visitor the dominant placement. Larvae develop in the nests of common wasps and hornets where they feed on nest debris, but the conspicuous adult flower-visiting behaviour is what observers record in Amsterdam. The sky-looper secondary captures the powerful, direct transit flights between flower patches. Adults fly from July to October.
