Dogwood is a native deciduous shrub common in park margins, woodland edges, and scrubby areas throughout Amsterdam and the wider Dutch lowlands. It is a woody plant but not classified as a tree in the roster — it grows as a multi-stemmed shrub rarely exceeding 4 metres — so ground-park is the correct habitat rather than tree-rooted. Its deep crimson autumn stems make it a popular ornamental choice in parks such as Amsterdamse Bos. The plant default (ground-park, none) fits exactly and no override is needed.
