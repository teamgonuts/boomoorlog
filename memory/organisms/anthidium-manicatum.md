The Common Wool Carder Bee is a solitary bee that visits a wide range of flowers, with a particular fondness for plants in the mint and dead-nettle families. Males are notably territorial, hovering aggressively around floral patches and intercepting other insects. Females collect plant fibres ("wool") from hairy-leaved plants to line nest cells in pre-existing cavities. In Amsterdam gardens and parks it is a reliable summer visitor, often seen working lamb's ear or woundwort. The default flower-visitor placement and flower-bobber animation fit well.
