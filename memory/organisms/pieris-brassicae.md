The Large White is the most conspicuous of Amsterdam's white butterflies, abundant in parks, allotment gardens, and any area where brassica crops or nasturtiums grow — the larval host plants. Adults are active nectar-feeders visiting a wide range of flowers from spring to autumn and are a familiar sight over Amsterdam's community gardens and park flower beds. Their flight between blooms is a classic flower-bobber pattern: purposeful but short, with frequent pauses to feed. The insect category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is correct for this species.
