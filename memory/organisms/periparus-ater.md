The coal tit is a small conifer-specialist tit that regularly forages in the needle canopy of cedars (Cedrus), false cypresses (Chamaecyparis), dawn redwood (Metasequoia), spruces (Picea), and pines (Pinus) in Amsterdam parks. It is an agile canopy bird that flits quickly between branches and adjacent trees, often in mixed tit flocks during winter. The default bird tags — tree-canopy and tree-flitter — are a precise fit for this small, highly active songbird. Coal tits also cache seeds in bark crevices but their primary visible behavior is canopy foraging and inter-tree movement.
