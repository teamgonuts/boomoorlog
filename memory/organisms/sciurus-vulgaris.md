This pairing covers both the native Eurasian Red Squirrel (now very rare in Amsterdam) and the introduced Grey Squirrel, observed together in stands of Juglans, Picea, Pinus, and Pterocarya. Both species share an essentially identical ecology for placement purposes: dominant time in the canopy leaping between conifers and large deciduous trees, with regular descents to cache and retrieve nuts on park ground. Tree-canopy is dominant and ground-park secondary for habitat; tree-flitter and park-roamer cover the two movement phases. The mammal default of ground-park alone does not capture the strongly arboreal lifestyle, requiring the override.
