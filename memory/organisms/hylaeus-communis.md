The common yellow-face bee is a small, wasp-like solitary bee that visits a wide range of flowers for nectar and pollen, which it transports internally rather than in pollen baskets. It is one of the most frequently recorded small bees in Amsterdam parks and gardens. Nests are built in hollow stems, old beetle borings in wood, and similar narrow cavities. The insect default of flower-visitor and flower-bobber matches its observed foraging behaviour well. No override is needed.
