The European hare (Lepus europaeus) and roe deer (Capreolus capreolus) are occasional browsers on young laburnum shoots and bark in Amsterdam's larger green areas such as the Amsterdamse Bos. Both are ground-dwelling mammals that range through open parkland and semi-wild green zones in search of woody browse. The park-roamer movement class fits perfectly: they walk and pause within vegetated polygons, pausing to feed. Roe deer are rare inside the ring-road but hares are regularly recorded in the Bos and city-edge parkland.
