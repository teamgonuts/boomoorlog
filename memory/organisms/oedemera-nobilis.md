The Swollen-thighed Beetle is an unmistakable slender green beetle named for the grotesquely enlarged hind femora of the male, commonly found in Amsterdam parks and gardens feeding on pollen in daisy and buttercup flowers from May to August. It is one of the most reliably flower-associated beetles in the Netherlands, spending nearly all adult life on open bowl-shaped flowers — particularly Leucanthemum, Ranunculus, and Rosa — feeding on pollen. The insect default flower-visitor / flower-bobber is an excellent fit for this species; ground-park is added as secondary for the larval stage which develops in dead plant stems at ground level. Idle-only secondary reflects the prolonged pollen-eating posture on a single flower.
