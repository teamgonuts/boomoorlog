Choristoneura tortricid moths (budworm complex) attack the new shoots and needles of Larix (larch) and occasionally spruce in northern European plantations and ornamental settings; Larix is used in Amsterdam parks and the Amsterdamse Bos. Young larvae mine needles; later instars spin together new growth and feed within the shelter. The caterpillars are sedentary within their web-bound feeding shelter in the canopy and the adult moths are nocturnal. Override from the insect default to tree-canopy / idle-only as a tree-feeding microorganism on Larix.
