House sparrows and Eurasian tree sparrows are both recorded on Ginkgo in Amsterdam, but their key characteristic is their tight association with the built environment — nesting in wall cavities and roof spaces, then foraging on pavements and squares below. Wall-and-roof is the dominant habitat because both sparrow species are closely tied to buildings for shelter and nesting; ground-urban captures their pavement-foraging behavior. Urban-walker is the appropriate movement class, as both species hop along streets and squares picking up seeds and crumbs rather than flitting between trees.
