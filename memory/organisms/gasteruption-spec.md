Gasteruption wasps are slender, long-necked parasitoid wasps whose adults are among the more distinctive flower visitors on umbellifer blooms — they are regularly seen on Daucus, Anthriscus, and Angelica in Amsterdam parks and canal banks. Their larvae parasitise solitary bees nesting in dead wood or hollow stems. Adults move between flower heads with deliberate, hovering approaches (flower-bobber), and spend long periods stationed on a flower head with the abdomen curved beneath (idle-only). The insect default of flower-visitor/flower-bobber is an excellent fit; no override needed.
