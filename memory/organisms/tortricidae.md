Tortricid and geometrid moths encompass many species whose caterpillars roll, tie, or graze leaves in the canopy of a wide range of host trees; on Parrotia and Paulownia the larvae feed in the foliage layer during spring. Adult moths of both families are night-flying and rest cryptically on bark or foliage by day without visiting flowers. Tree-canopy is the correct habitat class because the caterpillar stage — the primary observable phase — is spent feeding on leaf tissue in the canopy; idle-only replaces flower-bobber because the resting adults are motionless and the larvae are slow leaf-rollers.
