The Large Yellow Underwing is one of the most abundant noctuid moths in Amsterdam, drawn to park lawns, canal-side verges, and any patch of herbaceous vegetation where its larvae feed on low-growing plants. Adults rest motionless during the day on leaf litter, soil, or low bark, relying on camouflage; the vivid yellow hindwings are a startle display only revealed in flight. At night they fly to light sources — a familiar sight at Amsterdam street lamps in late summer — but do not feed significantly at flowers and are not flower-visitors in any meaningful sense. The category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is wrong for this moth; ground-park and idle-only reflect its actual daytime resting ecology in the city.
