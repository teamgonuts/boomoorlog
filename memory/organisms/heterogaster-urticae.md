The Nettle Ground Bug is a small ground bug (Heterogastridae) that feeds almost exclusively on the seeds of Common Nettle, spending most of its life clustered at the base of nettle patches or among fallen seed heads on the soil surface. In Amsterdam it is found wherever nettles grow — park edges, allotment borders, canal banks, and rough urban ground. Adults and nymphs move only short distances and spend most time stationary. The flower-visitor default does not apply; habitat overridden to ground-park with ground-urban secondary; movement overridden to idle-only.
