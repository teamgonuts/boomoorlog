This entry captures ants (exemplified by Lasius niger) observed specifically in the canopy of Liriodendron tulip trees, tending aphid colonies and harvesting honeydew. Their placement is tree-canopy because the observational context is on-tree honeydew feeding rather than ground nesting. On the trunk and branch surfaces they move slowly enough to be rendered as idle-only. The override from the insect default reflects a tree-canopy microhabitat association rather than the flower-visitor default for insects.
