Issus coleoptratus is a small, squat planthopper in the family Issidae that feeds on phloem sap from the stems and bark of ivy, elder, and various deciduous shrubs and trees. Its mottled brown-grey coloration is cryptic against bark surfaces, where it rests and feeds, making tree-bark the dominant habitat. It moves very little when disturbed, relying on camouflage rather than flight, which makes idle-only the correct movement class. The default insect habitat of flower-visitor does not apply as this species is entirely bark- and stem-associated throughout its life. Amsterdam observations are typically found on ivy-clad walls and elder bushes in parks and gardens.
