The Broad-leaved Helleborine is a native orchid that has made a surprising adaptation to urban life, colonising shaded parks, pavement cracks under tree cover, and even car parks. In Amsterdam it is regularly recorded in Vondelpark and other shaded public green spaces, often growing directly beneath old trees. It produces nectar with mild narcotic properties that intoxicates visiting wasps, its primary pollinators.
