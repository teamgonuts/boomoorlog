Deraeocoris ruber is a predatory mirid plant bug with striking red and black patterning, common in Amsterdam parks and urban gardens on nettles, willowherb, and various shrubs where it hunts small insects and mites. Despite its name and category as a plant bug it is notably carnivorous, patrolling the upper stems and flower heads of tall herbaceous plants and low shrubs. The insect default flower-visitor / flower-bobber partially fits since it is found on flowering plants, but the dominant behaviour is predatory ambush from stems rather than nectar-foraging. Tree-canopy / idle-only captures the ambush-predator posture on stems; ground-park secondary and flower-bobber secondary reflect observations on tall herbs and flower heads.
