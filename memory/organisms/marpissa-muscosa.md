The Fencepost Jumping Spider is a flat-bodied salticid specialized for life on sun-warmed vertical wooden surfaces — fence posts, boardwalks, and tree bark. In Amsterdam it is most often found on the timber structures of park fences and on the coarser-barked trees such as oak and ash. Its cryptic mottled brown pattern blends perfectly with weathered wood. Tree-bark is the dominant habitat; wall-and-roof covers its preference for man-made timber surfaces. Like all jumping spiders it sits in ambush rather than moving continuously, fitting idle-only.
