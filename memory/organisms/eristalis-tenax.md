The Common Drone Fly is a robust, honeybee-mimicking hoverfly found throughout Amsterdam from early spring to late autumn, abundant wherever open flowers are available in parks, garden strips, and canal-side plantings. Its larvae are aquatic "rat-tailed maggots" that develop in stagnant, oxygen-poor water such as polluted ditches and canal margins — but the adults that Amsterdam observers record are the flower-visiting forms living above ground. Adults hover at flowers, feed on pollen and nectar, and move between blooms with short directional flights matching the flower-bobber pattern.
