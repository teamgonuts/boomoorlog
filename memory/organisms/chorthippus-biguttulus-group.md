The Chorthippus biguttulus species complex are medium-sized field grasshoppers that live almost entirely in dry to moderately dry grassland, park edges, and rough vegetation; they are not flower visitors. Males produce a characteristic buzzing stridulation while perched on grass stems or bare ground. Their movement is predominantly short hops followed by long stationary periods, making idle-only the most honest animation. Ground-park replaces the insect default of flower-visitor because these orthopterans never visit flowers to forage.
