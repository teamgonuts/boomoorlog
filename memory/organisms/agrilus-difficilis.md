Agrilus difficilis is a metallic wood-boring beetle whose larvae mine under the bark of Gleditsia triacanthos, a common Amsterdam street tree. Adults emerge through D-shaped exit holes and bask on trunks and branches in summer. The visible stage is on bark; tree-bark + idle-only fits better than the flower-visitor insect default for a buprestid borer.
