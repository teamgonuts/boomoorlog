Prickly Sow-thistle is a common annual weed of pavements, wall bases, rubble ground, and urban disturbed habitats, as well as park edges and allotments. In Amsterdam it is one of the most abundant pavement-crack plants, found growing from joins in paving stones and at the base of walls throughout the inner city, making ground-urban the dominant habitat override from the plant default of ground-park alone. Ground-park is a genuine secondary since it also appears in parks and rough ground. Movement is none as for all plants. The species is ecologically important as an early-season pollen source for short-tongued bees.
