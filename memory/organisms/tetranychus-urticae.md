Tetranychus urticae is one of the world's most polyphagous pest mites, feeding on the undersides of leaves of Cercidiphyllum, Parrotia, Paulownia, Robinia, and many other trees by puncturing epidermal cells and withdrawing the contents, producing a fine webbing over the affected foliage in warm dry summers. In Amsterdam it is especially prominent on ornamental trees during drought periods. Tree-canopy overrides the arachnid default of anywhere because all feeding and reproduction occurs on leaf surfaces in the canopy; idle-only is retained as the mite's movement is invisible at sprite scale.
