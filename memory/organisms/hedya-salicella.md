Hedya salicella is a tortricid leaf-roller moth whose larvae feed on willows, alders, and birch — all trees common along Amsterdam's canal banks and in city parks. Adults are white with a characteristic dark-flecked forewing pattern and are typically encountered resting on leaves or bark in sheltered spots during the day. Willows overhanging canals are a primary habitat, making tree-canopy the most accurate placement. The default flower-visitor tag does not apply; this is a bark-and-foliage resting moth. idle-only reflects daytime motionless resting behaviour.
