Hackberry psyllids are tiny jumping plant lice (family Psyllidae) that induce nipple galls, blister galls, and bud galls on Celtis (hackberry) trees in Amsterdam. Their entire feeding and reproductive cycle is spent within the leaf tissue of the host canopy, making tree-canopy the only appropriate habitat class. Unlike flower-foraging insects, psyllids are phloem-feeders firmly anchored to their gall structures and rarely move once settled. Idle-only captures this stationary lifestyle; the default insect flower-visitor/flower-bobber tags are not applicable.
