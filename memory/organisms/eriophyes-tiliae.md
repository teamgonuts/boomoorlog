Eriophyes tiliae is the mite responsible for the distinctive nail-like or pouch-like galls (Aceria-type ceratonea or unguicula forms) that project from the upper surface of linden (Tilia) leaves throughout Amsterdam. These mites are invisible to the naked eye and spend their entire lives within the gall tissue or on leaf surfaces. The visible feature — the red or green nail galls dotting the leaf — is firmly within the canopy of Tilia street trees that line most of Amsterdam's boulevards. Tree-canopy overrides the arachnid default of anywhere, while idle-only is retained as movement.
