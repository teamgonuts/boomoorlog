Closterotomus trivialis is a mirid plant bug found on various flowering plants in Amsterdam parks and rough grassland, typically feeding on the soft tissues and developing seeds of Asteraceae, Apiaceae, and similar composites and umbellifers. Adults are green-brown with subtle patterning and are most often found on flower heads or upper stem leaves of tall herbs in summer. The insect default flower-visitor / flower-bobber is a reasonable fit since this species genuinely spends its time on flowering plants; tree-canopy is added as a secondary because it can also be found on low-growing shrubby vegetation in the park understorey. Idle-only is added as secondary movement for the long periods spent motionless on a flower head.
