The European Paper Wasp is a slender social wasp common in Amsterdam parks and urban gardens, readily recognised by its yellow-and-black banding and habit of hanging long legs in flight. It builds small open paper nests under eaves, in shrubs, or in wall cavities, and forages widely on flowers for nectar as well as hunting caterpillars to feed larvae. Unlike bees it moves between flower patches but also walks along stems and ground-level vegetation hunting prey, so a secondary ground-park placement captures that foraging behaviour. The flower-visitor dominant habitat and flower-bobber primary movement cover the nectar-foraging animation, with urban-walker as secondary for its ground-level prey-hunting passes.
