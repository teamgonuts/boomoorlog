Waxwings (Bombycilla garrulus in Europe, the Bohemian waxwing) are irruptive winter visitors to Amsterdam, arriving in variable numbers from their Scandinavian and Russian breeding grounds when berry crops fail in the north. They are observed on Morus (mulberry) here, stripping the fruit from the canopy in tight flocks. Waxwings are quintessentially tree-canopy birds — they perch, feed, and move between fruiting trees almost exclusively — rarely coming to the ground. The bird category defaults of tree-canopy and tree-flitter are an accurate fit: waxwings move in compact flocks between fruiting trees with direct, starling-like flight, which the tree-flitter animation captures reasonably well.
