Fieldfare and Redwing are migratory winter thrushes that flock through Amsterdam from October to March, stripping Sorbus (rowan) berries. Fieldfares often travel in large flocks, colonising a tree and stripping it bare before moving on. When not in trees they work grassy park areas for earthworms, particularly after rain. Tree-canopy is dominant as the primary feeding location on Sorbus berries; ground-park secondary captures the Fieldfare's characteristic ground-foraging in open lawns.
