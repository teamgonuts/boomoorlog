The Gadwall is a subtly plumaged dabbling duck — the male grey-brown with a black rear, the female resembling a small Mallard — that has become a regular breeding bird on Amsterdam's larger water bodies and canal-fringed parks. It forages by dabbling and up-ending for aquatic vegetation on the water surface (water-drifter dominant) and rests on the bank or emergent vegetation at the water edge (idle-only secondary with water-edge secondary habitat). It is a quieter, less urbanised duck than the Mallard and rarely begs on pavements, so no ground-urban tag is warranted. Overridden from tree-canopy default as it is entirely water-associated.
