Crambus perlella is a small grass-veneer moth whose larvae develop in the roots and stems of grasses; adults rest head-downward on grass stems in parks and rough grassland, relying on cryptic patterning for camouflage. They are not flower visitors and do not forage openly, so the insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is replaced with ground-park and idle-only. Adults are active at dusk and night but spend the day completely motionless in grass. The resting posture on a grass blade is the animation target.
