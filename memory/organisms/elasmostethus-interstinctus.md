The birch shieldbug is a medium-sized green and brown shieldbug strongly associated with birch (Betula) trees, where adults and nymphs feed on developing catkins and seeds in the canopy. In Amsterdam it is found in parks with mature birch trees, typically sitting immobile on leaves or bark during the day. Although adults can fly, they are rarely seen in sustained flight and spend most of their visible time resting or slowly feeding. Tree-canopy is the correct dominant habitat over the insect default flower-visitor, and idle-only replaces flower-bobber as the shieldbug's typical demeanour is thoroughly sedentary.
