The Common Whitethroat is a summer-visiting warbler that breeds in scrubby hedgerows, bramble thickets, and rough vegetation at or near ground level, moving up into low tree canopy to sing from exposed perches. In Amsterdam it is recorded during spring migration and occasionally breeds in parks with dense scrub and overgrown edges. It is more a ground-scrub bird than a true high-canopy species, so tree-canopy is dominant for placement purposes (it perches in shrubs/low trees) with ground-park secondary for its skulking foraging behaviour. Movement is tree-flitter (hops between shrub perches) with idle-only secondary when it pauses to sing.
