Carpenter bees (Xylocopa) are large solitary bees that have expanded their range northward and are increasingly recorded in Amsterdam on warm, south-facing days. They visit Cercis (redbud) and Platanus flowers for pollen and nectar. Female carpenter bees nest by boring tunnels into dry wood — fence posts, wooden beams, and old timber structures count as wall-and-roof in the placement system. Flower-visitor is the dominant habitat for their foraging activity; wall-and-roof secondary captures the nest site. Sky-looper secondary reflects their powerful, long-distance transit flights between patches.
