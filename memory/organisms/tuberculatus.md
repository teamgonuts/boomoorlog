Oak aphids (Tuberculatus, Lachnus, and Thelaxes) are sap-sucking insects that feed on the leaves and young twigs of Quercus in Amsterdam's urban parks. They are sessile organisms that sit fixed on leaf undersides or young shoots, barely moving over the course of a day. The dominant habitat is tree-canopy (leaf and twig feeding) with tree-bark as a secondary for bark-dwelling Lachnus species. Movement is idle-only since their positional motion is imperceptibly slow; flower-bobber default does not apply to these bark/leaf feeders.
