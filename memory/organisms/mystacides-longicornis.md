The Grouse Wing is a caddisfly (Trichoptera) whose larvae build portable cases and live in the sediment of slow-moving canals and ditches — classic Amsterdam water-body habitat. Adults emerge in summer and rest near the water's edge on waterside vegetation and canal walls, making water-edge the dominant surface placement. The default flower-visitor / flower-bobber profile does not fit aquatic insects; water-edge-stalker captures the adult's slow resting-and-drifting behaviour along the canal margin, with idle-only as secondary for extended resting bouts.
