The Brown-lipped Snail is one of Amsterdam's most conspicuous land snails, recognised by its variably-banded shell in shades of yellow, pink, and brown. It occupies a wide range of vegetated habitats — hedgerows, park edges, cemetery walls, and garden beds — but is always tied to vegetated ground and damp leaf litter, making ground-park the correct dominant habitat. Its movement speed is far below any useful animation threshold (a few centimetres per minute at best), so idle-only is the right class, consistent with the taxonomy note on slow-creep. The default mollusc tags fit perfectly and no override is needed.
