Leafcutter bees (Megachile) are solitary bees that visit flowers for nectar and pollen while also cutting neat discs from leaves — notably from Cercis siliquastrum (Judas tree) in Amsterdam parks and city gardens — to line their nest cells. They are strongly tied to flowering plants as their food source, making flower-visitor the dominant and sufficient habitat class. The flower-bobber movement class captures their characteristic short bobbing flights between blooms. In Amsterdam leafcutter bees are common visitors to garden flowers and the ornamental Cercis trees that bloom in late spring.
