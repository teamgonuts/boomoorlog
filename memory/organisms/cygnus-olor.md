The Mute Swan is one of Amsterdam's most iconic waterbirds, a permanent resident on the canals, Amstel, and larger city ponds. It spends nearly all visible time on the water surface, drifting slowly along canal stretches or grazing submerged vegetation with its long neck (water-drifter dominant). Between foraging bouts it tucks its head under a wing and drifts motionless (idle-only secondary), matching the taxonomy note example exactly.
