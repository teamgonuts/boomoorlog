Fascista cercerisella is a small gracillariid moth whose larvae fold and tie the leaves of Cercis (Judas tree) in Amsterdam. The aggregate also covers the broader community of some 10–19 Lepidoptera larval species associated with this ornamental tree. All these larvae feed and shelter within folded or mined leaf tissue in the canopy, and adults are inconspicuous micro-moths that rest motionless on foliage or bark. Tree-canopy is the correct habitat for this leaf-feeding guild; idle-only replaces flower-bobber, as none of these species exhibit flower-visiting behaviour as their dominant trait.
