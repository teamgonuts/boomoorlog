The Red Swamp Crayfish is an invasive North American freshwater crayfish, not an insect — the GBIF taxon-group column is incorrect for this row. It is now established in Amsterdam's canals and ditches, where it is a significant ecological concern and regularly observed. Its primary habitat is water-body (submerged in canals and ponds), with a secondary presence at water-edge where it emerges onto banks at night. Movement is water-drifter as the dominant locomotion (walking along the bottom, which at surface-view renders like drifting), with idle-only as a secondary for when it sits still on the bank. Category is overridden to other since it is a crustacean.
