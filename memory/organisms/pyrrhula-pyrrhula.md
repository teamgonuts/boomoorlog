The Bullfinch is a stocky, shy finch that visits Amsterdam parks and tree-lined streets in autumn and winter, feeding on buds, berries, and seeds of Fraxinus, Sorbus, and Ulmus. It moves quietly through the mid- to upper canopy, typically in pairs or small family groups, flitting deliberately between trees rather than making long open-air crossings. In Amsterdam it is a scarce but regular winter visitor in places like Vondelpark and the Amsterdamse Bos wherever fruiting Sorbus or seeding ash are present.
