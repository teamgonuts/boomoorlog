The lime aphid is among the most abundant and noticeable aphids in Amsterdam, forming dense colonies on the undersides of linden (Tilia) leaves from late spring through summer. It produces copious honeydew that falls as sticky rain on cars and pavements beneath linden avenues — a well-known nuisance in the city. Colonies are stationary; individual aphids pierce the leaf phloem and feed without moving for extended periods. Tree-canopy is the exact correct placement for this canopy leaf-feeder, and idle-only replaces the insect default flower-bobber as no pollinating or flower-visiting behaviour occurs.
