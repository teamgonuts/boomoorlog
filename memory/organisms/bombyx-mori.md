Bombyx mori, the domestic silkworm, is a fully domesticated insect that cannot survive in the wild; it is incapable of flight and entirely dependent on humans for reproduction. Its association with Morus (white mulberry) in Amsterdam almost certainly reflects a novelty / educational observation rather than a feral population. The larvae feed exclusively on Morus leaves in the canopy; they are entirely immobile beyond slow crawling between leaves. Tree-canopy is the appropriate habitat override (the larvae live and feed on mulberry foliage); idle-only is correct for an animal that does virtually nothing but eat leaf tissue and spin silk. This is a tree-feeding microorganism on Morus in the context of this dataset.
