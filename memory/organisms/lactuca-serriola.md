Prickly Lettuce is a tall annual or biennial composite with small pale yellow flowerheads and prickly leaves that orient themselves north-south to minimise afternoon sun exposure. It is a classic urban ruderal, abundantly colonising roadsides, building edges, rubble, pavement cracks, and disturbed ground across Amsterdam. The wild ancestor of cultivated lettuce, it thrives on nutrient-rich disturbed soils in full sun. The dominant habitat is ground-urban due to its strong preference for built surfaces and rubble, with ground-park secondary; movement stays none.
