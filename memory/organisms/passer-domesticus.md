This entry records house sparrows and wood pigeons using Catalpa and Paulownia trees in Amsterdam specifically as roost sites, rather than in active foraging. Both species are common Amsterdam birds that spend the night roosting in trees, remaining motionless in the canopy for long periods. The tree-canopy habitat is correct for roosting birds perched in the crown, but the typical movement default (tree-flitter) is replaced with idle-only to reflect the roosting context of this observation. Wood pigeon and house sparrow differ in their active-phase habitats, but both roost in canopy foliage.
