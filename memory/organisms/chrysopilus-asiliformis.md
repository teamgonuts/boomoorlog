The Little Snipefly is a predatory rhagionid fly of damp, shaded park margins and woodland edges where it perches on low vegetation waiting to capture small insects. It is not a flower-feeder and has no meaningful relationship with blooms, so the default flower-visitor / flower-bobber combination does not apply. Adults perch motionless on leaves with the characteristic head-down stance of the family. Ground-park captures its preferred damp vegetated margins in Amsterdam's parks and canal-side green strips; idle-only is the correct movement for a sit-and-wait predator.
