The pine bark beetle bores through the outer bark of Pinus to construct brood galleries in the phloem; adults also feed by mining out the pith of young pine shoots for maturation feeding, causing characteristic shoot death called "top-shot." The entire life cycle from larva to adult is spent in or very close to the bark surface. Amsterdam's planted pines provide host material, and the beetle is a primary colonizer of stressed or storm-damaged pine trees. Tree-bark is the correct habitat because all feeding and reproduction is on or immediately under the bark; idle-only replaces flower-bobber as there is no flower-visiting in this species.
