This aggregate covers moths in Geometridae and Notodontidae — including the spectacular Kentish glory (Endromis versicolora) — whose larvae feed on birch (Betula) foliage in Amsterdam. Geometrid larvae are the classic "inchworm" caterpillars that rest on twigs mimicking small twigs; notodontid larvae also rest on foliage with characteristic postures. Adults of both families are large, cryptically patterned moths that rest motionless on bark or foliage during daylight. Tree-canopy is the dominant habitat for the leaf-feeding larval stage, and idle-only captures the characteristic still-resting behaviour of both larvae and adults.
