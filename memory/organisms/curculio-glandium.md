Acorn weevils (Curculio glandium and the related C. elephas on oak) are classic canopy-foraging curculionids whose females use their very long curved rostrum to bore into developing acorns on Quercus to deposit eggs. Amsterdam's numerous oak-lined streets and park oaks support a resident population, most visible in late summer when adults are active on foliage and acorns. The insect category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is overridden to tree-canopy / idle-only because the entire life cycle revolves around acorns in the canopy, and adults move only slowly across leaf and twig surfaces.
