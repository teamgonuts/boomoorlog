Rhynchaenus fagi is a tiny jumping weevil (2–3 mm) whose larvae mine blister-like galleries in beech leaves, producing characteristic brown blotches visible from below Fagus canopies in Amsterdam parks such as the Amsterdamse Bos. Adults overwinter in leaf litter and ascend to the canopy in spring to feed and oviposit; their distinctive leaf-mines persist through summer. The entire life cycle is canopy-centred on Fagus foliage, with no association with flowers or open ground, making tree-canopy / idle-only the correct override from the insect default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber.
