The sycamore aphid is one of the most common and visible aphids on Acer (maple) trees throughout Amsterdam, forming dense colonies on the undersides of leaves from spring through autumn. Like all aphids it is essentially stationary once feeding — individuals sit immobile piercing leaf veins and exuding honeydew that drips onto pavements below. Tree-canopy is the correct placement since colonies occupy the leaf surfaces; idle-only replaces the insect default of flower-bobber because the aphid performs no flower-visiting or meaningful locomotion at sprite scale.
