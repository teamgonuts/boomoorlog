Dysaphis pyri and its close relative D. plantaginea are pear-specialising aphids observed on Pyrus trees in Amsterdam. They colonise leaf undersides and shoot tips from spring, causing the leaves to curl and redden in characteristic clusters that make colonies easy to spot in the canopy. These aphids are sedentary feeders — once settled on a feeding site they remain essentially stationary, piercing the phloem and extracting sap.
