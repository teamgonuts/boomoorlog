This combined slug groups the Great Spotted Woodpecker with the Eurasian Nuthatch, both recorded on the beech (Fagus) tree in Amsterdam. The Nuthatch is a compact bird that runs head-first down trunks and along major branches probing bark crevices for insects and cached nuts — a lifestyle almost identical in placement terms to the woodpecker. Both species are bark specialists who also spend time in the canopy and make short flights between nearby trees. Tree-bark is the dominant habitat with tree-canopy as secondary; tree-flitter is the primary movement with idle-only secondary for the frequent stationary foraging postures of both species.
