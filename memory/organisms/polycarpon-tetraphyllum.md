Four-leaved Allseed is a tiny annual weed of southern European origin that has colonised Amsterdam's warmest urban micro-habitats: pavement cracks, wall bases, cobbled squares, and the dry sandy margins of tram tracks. It requires bare or disturbed mineral ground rather than vegetated park soil, so the default ground-park placement is too green; ground-urban is the biologically accurate choice. The plant is inconspicuous — reaching only 5–15 cm — but consistent with Amsterdam's expanding thermophilic pavement flora.
