This entry groups honey bees (Apis mellifera) and bumblebees (Bombus spp.) found on Acer, Gleditsia, and Ilex in Amsterdam. Both genera are generalist pollinators highly abundant in Amsterdam's parks, canal-side plantings, and gardens. They forage actively among flowering trees and shrubs, making short bobbing transits between blooms and returning to hive or nest between foraging bouts. The flower-visitor habitat and flower-bobber movement defaults are a perfect fit: these insects are most conspicuous when working a flowering tree canopy and largely invisible when underground or in the hive. Amsterdam populations benefit from the city's diverse street tree flora and the many small gardens.
