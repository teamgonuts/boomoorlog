Argynnis fritillary butterflies (silver-washed fritillary A. paphia and high brown fritillary A. adippe) are large, powerful woodland-edge butterflies that would be unusual in central Amsterdam but may occur in the Amsterdamse Bos and larger wooded parks. They are associated with Fraxinus (ash) here, whose canopy shade creates the dappled woodland edge conditions these species prefer. Their primary habitat is flower-visitor when foraging on bramble, thistles, and other blooms, but they also glide and rest at ground level in grassy clearings, warranting the ground-park secondary. The park-roamer secondary captures the territorial gliding flights these large fritillaries make through woodland glades.
