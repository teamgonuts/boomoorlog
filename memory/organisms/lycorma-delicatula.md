The spotted lanternfly is an invasive planthopper native to Asia that feeds on phloem sap of Ailanthus altissima (tree of heaven) and other woody plants. In areas where it has established, nymphs and adults spend most time pressed flat against bark surfaces sucking sap, only briefly hopping to new positions. Tree-bark is dominant since this is where they congregate in mass; tree-canopy is secondary for adults that feed on foliage tips. Idle-only matches their characteristic motionless sap-feeding posture. Not yet established in Amsterdam but listed here as a potential invasive on the city's Ailanthus street trees.
