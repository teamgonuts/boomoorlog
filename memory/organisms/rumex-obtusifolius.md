Broad-leaved Dock is one of the Netherlands' most ubiquitous plants, thriving in Amsterdam parks, roadsides, pavement cracks, canal banks, and disturbed ground wherever soil is enriched with nitrogen. Its large paddle-shaped leaves are the traditional folk remedy for nettle stings, and in autumn the rusty red seed spikes are a conspicuous sight along urban paths. The plant is a true generalist of both vegetated and paved ground, warranting a ground-urban secondary alongside the dominant ground-park habitat. Movement is none as for all plants.
