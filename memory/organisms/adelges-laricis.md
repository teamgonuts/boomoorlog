The larch woolly adelgid is a sap-sucking insect that produces fluffy white wax on Larix needles; the related life cycle alternates onto spruce. In Amsterdam Larix is uncommon but present in older park plantings such as the Amsterdamse Bos. The visible stage is sessile and tree-feeding, so tree-canopy + idle-only overrides the flower-visitor default.
