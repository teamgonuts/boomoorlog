Cow Parsley is one of the most abundant and conspicuous spring plants in Amsterdam, forming tall white-flowered stands along canal banks, park margins, roadsides, and under trees from April to June. It is among the most important nectar and pollen sources in the city, supporting hundreds of insect species including hoverflies, beetles, and parasitoid wasps. Ground-park is dominant as it occurs throughout park margins and rough ground, with water-edge as secondary because canal banks are one of its most characteristic Amsterdam habitats.
