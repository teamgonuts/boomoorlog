This combined entry covers canopy-foraging ladybird beetles and lacewings observed across Juglans, Larix, Magnolia, and Parrotia in Amsterdam. Both groups are aphid and soft-bodied insect predators that spend most of their time perched on leaves or moving slowly between prey clusters; lacewings in particular rest motionless during the day. The insect category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is overridden: neither ladybirds nor lacewings are primarily flower-associated in their feeding behaviour. Tree-canopy and idle-only place the sprite realistically in the leafy zone of the host tree.
