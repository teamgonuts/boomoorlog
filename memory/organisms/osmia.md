Mason bees (Osmia) and mining bees (Andrena) are solitary bees that are conspicuous spring pollinators in Amsterdam, observed on Cercis and Prunus blossoms. They forage energetically between flowers, collecting pollen and nectar for their underground or cavity nests. The default insect tags of flower-visitor and flower-bobber are a perfect fit — these bees are quintessential flower visitors that bob between blooms in season. Outside the flowering window they are absent from view as adults, consistent with the flower-visitor fallback behavior.
