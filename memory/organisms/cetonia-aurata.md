The rose chafer (Cetonia aurata) is a vivid metallic-green scarab beetle that visits open flowers to feed on pollen and petals; it is recorded on Magnolia flowers in Amsterdam parks where Magnolia trees bloom early in spring. Adults fly strongly from flower to flower and are attracted to large, scented blooms; larvae develop in rotting wood or compost elsewhere. The flower-visitor / flower-bobber pairing fits well, but this is an override (not default) because the insect default applies to Hymenoptera/Lepidoptera pollinators rather than flower-feeding beetles — the ecological role is similar but the taxon is not the default case.
