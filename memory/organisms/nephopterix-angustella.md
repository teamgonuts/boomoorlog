Nephopterix angustella is a pyralid moth whose larvae feed inside the berries and shoots of spindle (Euonymus europaeus) and occasionally other shrubs, making it associated with scrubby park edges, hedgerows, and gardens where spindle is planted or grows wild. Adults rest on the foliage or bark of host shrubs during the day. In Amsterdam it is found in parks and cemeteries where spindle occurs.
