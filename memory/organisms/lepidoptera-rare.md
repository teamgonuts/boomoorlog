This catch-all entry covers rare or incidental geometrid loopers and generalist caterpillars observed on Ginkgo and other Amsterdam street trees. These larvae are leaf-miners or free-feeding defoliators that spend nearly all their larval life anchored to foliage, inching very slowly and spending long periods motionless — hence idle-only movement. Tree-canopy is the sole habitat because the caterpillars feed in the leaf layer rather than on bark. In Amsterdam Ginkgo biloba is a popular ornamental street tree and records of loopers on it are rare and opportunistic.
