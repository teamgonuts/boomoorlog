Redwing (Turdus iliacus) and Fieldfare (Turdus pilaris) are winter thrushes that arrive in Amsterdam from October onwards, stripping hawthorn (Crataegus) and other berry-bearing trees. They often travel in mixed flocks, alternating between fruiting tree canopies and foraging on grassy ground in parks and verges. The tree-canopy habitat covers their berry feeding and the ground-park secondary captures their earthworm-hunting on lawns. Park-roamer secondary movement fits the Fieldfare's particularly active ground-walking style.
