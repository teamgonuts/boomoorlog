This grouping represents the mixed assemblage of small songbirds — tits, finches, sparrows, and thrushes — that use dense Cupressocyparis hedges and conifers in Amsterdam gardens as communal winter roosts after dark. During roosting hours the birds are entirely still, huddled in the foliage for warmth, so idle-only replaces the usual tree-flitter movement. Tree-canopy placement in a nearby evergreen is correct for a roosting group; the birds are not flitting but are genuinely present and stationary in the canopy. This is a nocturnal / crepuscular phenomenon, so the override from tree-flitter to idle-only is necessary.
