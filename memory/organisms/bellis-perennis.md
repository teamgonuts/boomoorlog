The Common Daisy is one of the most familiar lawn plants in Amsterdam, forming rosettes that resist mowing and bloom from early spring well into autumn. It thrives in short-grazed or mown grass in parks, cemeteries, and roadside verges. The white ray florets and yellow disc are an important early nectar source for small bees and hoverflies. It is essentially indestructible under light foot traffic and spreads readily by seed. Default ground-park and none tags fit perfectly.
