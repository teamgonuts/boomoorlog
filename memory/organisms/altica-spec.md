Altica is a genus of shiny metallic-blue flea beetles whose adults chew distinctive shot-hole patterns in the leaves of their host plants, often willowherbs (Epilobium) and related Onagraceae along damp ground and canal banks. In Amsterdam the most likely species are associated with waterside vegetation, making water-edge a relevant secondary habitat alongside ground-park. Adults jump powerfully when disturbed — hence "flea beetle" — but between disturbances they sit motionless on leaf surfaces (idle-only). Overridden from the default flower-visitor/flower-bobber because Altica is a leaf-feeding beetle, not a pollinator; it does not visit flowers for nectar.
