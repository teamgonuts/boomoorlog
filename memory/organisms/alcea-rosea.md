Hollyhock is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial that in Amsterdam is almost exclusively a garden escape, growing at the base of canal-house walls, in cracks of kerb edges, and along sunny south-facing facades rather than in managed park lawns. Its classic habitat in the city is the warm base of a brick wall on a narrow street, which makes ground-urban the dominant placement over the default ground-park. Ground-park is retained as secondary because it also naturalises in unmown park margins and cemetery corners.
