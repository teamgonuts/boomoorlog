The Western Jackdaw is a common corvid in Amsterdam that nests in building cavities, church towers, and chimney stacks, making wall-and-roof its primary habitat anchor for breeding and roosting. Outside the nest it is a true urban generalist, foraging on pavements, in parks, along canal banks, and in tree canopies — essentially anywhere food is available, particularly in the city centre around Centraal Station and the old church towers. Its movement alternates between short tree-to-tree or roof-to-roof hops (tree-flitter pattern) and deliberate ground walking while pecking for scraps (urban-walker). The default bird tag of tree-canopy / tree-flitter only partially captures this; wall-and-roof as the dominant habitat and the dual movement classes better represent Amsterdam Jackdaw ecology.
