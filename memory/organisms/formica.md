This entry covers wood ants and sugar-tending Formicidae observed on Acer trees in Amsterdam, primarily tending aphid colonies in the canopy for honeydew. Workers travel from ground nests up into the canopy and back, so both tree-canopy (dominant — where they are visible tending aphids) and ground-park (where nests and foraging trails are found) apply. Their movement on the tree surface is a slow crawl below the animation threshold, making idle-only appropriate. The override from the insect default reflects their canopy-tending ecology rather than flower-visiting.
