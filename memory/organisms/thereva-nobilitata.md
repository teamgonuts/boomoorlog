Thereva nobilitata is a stiletto fly (Therevidae) whose adults are typically seen basking on bare ground, sandy soil, or low vegetation in sunny clearings and park edges. Unlike nectar-focused pollinators, stiletto fly adults either do not feed or take minimal fluid, so flower-visitor and flower-bobber are inappropriate defaults. The larvae are predatory in the soil, further anchoring this species to ground-park. Adults remain motionless for long periods when basking, making idle-only a better fit than flower-bobber. It is an occasional but genuine Amsterdam recorder in dry, open park habitats.
