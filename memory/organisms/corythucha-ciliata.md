The sycamore lace bug is a North American tingid invasive now widespread in continental Europe and well established on London plane (Platanus) in Amsterdam. Adults and nymphs feed gregariously on the undersides of Platanus leaves, causing characteristic pale stippling; they remain on the leaf surface for extended periods and only move slowly between feeding sites. The insect category default of flower-visitor / flower-bobber is overridden to tree-canopy / idle-only because this species is strictly a foliage undersurface feeder on a single host genus, with no association with flowers.
