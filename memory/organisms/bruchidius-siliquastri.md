Bruchidius siliquastri is a bruchid seed weevil that specializes on Cercis siliquastrum (Judas tree), whose seed pods hang decoratively on the tree through summer and autumn. The beetle's larvae develop inside the seeds within the hanging pods in the canopy, consuming the seed contents before pupating inside the pod. Adults emerge and can be found on or near the pods. Tree-canopy is the correct habitat override because the entire life cycle relevant to observation occurs in the hanging seed pods on the tree; idle-only reflects the larval immobility within the seed. This is a tree-feeding microorganism on Cercis, warranting the override from the insect default of flower-visitor.
