The Eurasian otter (Lutra lutra) and water vole (Arvicola amphibius) are semi-aquatic mammals strongly tied to Amsterdam's canals, ditches, and waterway edges lined with alder. Otters hunt in the water body itself but emerge and patrol along canal banks, while water voles build burrows in earthen water-edges and forage at the bank. Water-edge is dominant for placement since both are most visually present at the bank interface; water-body is added because otters swim and hunt below the surface. Movement alternates between bank-patrolling (water-edge-stalker) and in-water swimming (water-drifter).
